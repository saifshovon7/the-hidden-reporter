Global Automotive Industry Threatened by Chip Company Conflict

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Gizmodo.com

A bizarre international conflict within semiconductor company Nexperia is threatening the global automotive industry. The Netherlands-based part of the company, owned by a Chinese parent company, is at the center of the dispute.

The Chinese Ministry of Commerce has stated that a disruption in operations by the Netherlands-based part of Nexperia could trigger a global semiconductor shortage, which the ministry claims would be the responsibility of the Netherlands.

Advertisement

Nexperia is a deeply European company with a rich history, but it is also deeply Chinese, as it was acquired by Wingtech in 2018. Wingtech is 30% owned by interests tied to the Chinese government.

The conflict between the Netherlands and China has its roots in a trade war between the U.S. and China, which led to concerns that Nexperia's secret recipes were being handed over to its parent company.

In 2022, the Dutch government invoked a Cold War-era law to seize control of Nexperia due to these concerns, leading China to suspend Nexperia's exports of basic automotive microprocessors.

The semiconductor shortage could have significant implications for the global automotive industry, with potential delays and disruptions to car production.
