AI Enthusiasts Gather at ClawCon: A Community on the Cusp of a New Era

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Thefp.com

A sea of enthusiastic fans gathered at New York City's inaugural ClawCon, a two-day event dedicated to the personalized AI agent OpenClaw.

Launched in November, OpenClaw has taken the AI world by storm, drawing over two million visitors in a single week and charting unprecedented growth on GitHub.

The brainchild of Austrian developer Peter Steinberger, OpenClaw has been rebranded multiple times, from Clawdbot to Moltbot and finally to its current name.

Attendees at ClawCon included Michael Galpert, a mega-fan of the product who has come from San Francisco to network with fellow enthusiasts.

Advertisement

According to Galpert, the community is 'ready to ride the claw,' referring to the AI technology that is poised to revolutionize various aspects of life, from email organization to options trading.

The event, co-sponsored by Kilo Code, CEO Scott Breitenother described it as 'a movement,' with people 'hungry for the claw.'

As attendees sipped on liquor and danced to the music, they were also able to network with other fans and engage in discussions about the potential of OpenClaw.

With its rapid growth and the creation of a social media platform for its bots, Moltbook, OpenClaw has sparked a new era in AI development, with some even creating their own 'Crustafarianism' religion.

The Claw community, comprised of enthusiasts and developers, is a testament to the potential of AI to transform the world.
