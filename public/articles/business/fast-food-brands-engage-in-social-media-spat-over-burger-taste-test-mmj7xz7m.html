Fast Food Brands Engage in Social Media Spat Over Burger Taste Test

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: USA Today

McDonald's recent video of its CEO, Chris Kempczinski, taste-testing the Big Arch burger sparked a social media frenzy among fast-food brands.

The video, posted in February, showed Kempczinski taking a single bite of the XXL burger, prompting sharp-tongued viewers to mock him on social media.

Competitors Burger King and Wendy's quickly responded with their own videos of their executives happily chomping on their burgers, poking fun at Kempczinski's fumble.

McDonald's attempted to regain control of the situation with an Instagram post, but the competition wasn't ready to let the action die down.

Jack in the Box posted a video of its mascot saying, 'Small bites? We don't do that here,' while A&W Canada posted a TikTok of its taste tester munching on a Teen Burger.

The online food fight escalated beyond burgers, with fast-food chains discussing the quality of their chicken and biscuits.

Wendy's announced a new contest to hire a Chief Tasting Officer, with a $100,000 salary, prompting Popeyes to respond with a jab about the quality of their food.

The discussion moved to the quality of each chain's biscuits, with Church's Chicken and Popeyes engaging in a heated exchange.

In the end, Kentucky Fried Chicken joined the fray, noting, 'We'll leave the beef to the boys. No products – just finger lickin' good chicken.'
