Hawaii's Diversification Dilemma: Risks of Over-Reliance on Tourism

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: SFGate

For years, thousands of Hawaii's residents have left the Islands for the continental U.S., where better-paying jobs and lower housing costs are easier to find. While Hawaii's high cost of living is often blamed, experts say the deeper issue is that there aren't enough high-paying jobs in industries beyond tourism to keep up with the soaring cost of living.

According to Steven Bond-Smith, an economist at the Economic Research Organization at the University of Hawaii, a healthy economy in the US averages about 2% growth over long periods of time, but Hawaii is averaging much lower, at around 0.6%. This discrepancy has driven residents to leave the state in search of better opportunities.

Bond-Smith emphasized the need for higher- and higher-value jobs and activities in Hawaii, stating that the jobs that tend to offer higher wages are in very large US cities, particularly in tech or biotech. The main activity in Hawaii is tourism, but it hasn't had the kind of income growth that other places have had.

Hawaii state Rep. Greggor Ilagan echoed Bond-Smith's concerns, citing the state's reliance on two major industries, tourism and the military. Ilagan emphasized the need to diversify the state's economy to weather potential challenges in the future.

In the last 10 years, the state has faced significant drops in tourism due to the 2018 eruption on the island of Hawaii, the COVID-19 pandemic, and the Maui wildfires. The number of visitors still hasn't recovered to what it was before the pandemic.

Bond-Smith and Ilagan agree that diversification doesn't mean anti-tourism, but rather includes tourism as well as diversifying tourism. Bond-Smith emphasized the importance of increasing the value added to visitors in the tourism industry.
