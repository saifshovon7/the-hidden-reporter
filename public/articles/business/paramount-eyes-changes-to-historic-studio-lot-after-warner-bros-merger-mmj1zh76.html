Paramount Eyes Changes to Historic Studio Lot After Warner Bros. Merger

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Hollywood Reporter

Hollywood is anticipating changes following Paramount Skydance's acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery, with Paramount reportedly planning to keep both its Burbank and Hollywood studios operating.

However, the goal is to eventually consolidate many of the studio operations around the Warner Bros. lot in Burbank to eliminate redundancies, according to people close to David Ellison.

Advertisement

Paramount is not looking to destroy its lot, but instead evolve other parts of it and rent space for commercial offices and possible retail use, allowing for construction of up to 1.9 million square feet.

The space may also be leased out for film productions, including those of Paramount and HBO's combined streaming options.

Entertainment property broker Nicole Mihalka told the L.A. Times that both the Warner Bros. and Paramount lots are prime pieces of real estate with potential to be tourist attractions in addition to working studios.

Paramount won the bidding war for Warner Bros. Discovery in a $111 billion megadeal, paying $31 per share for WBD.
