Ford Recalls 1.74 Million Vehicles Over Rearview Display Issues

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Associated Press

Ford has issued two recalls affecting nearly 1.74 million vehicles in the U.S., due to software issues that impact the vehicles' rearview camera displays.

The recalls cover 849,310 Ford Broncos and Edges, as well as 889,950 Ford Escapes, Lincoln Corsairs, Lincoln Aviators, and Lincoln Explorers, all from model years 2020 to 2026.

According to notices published by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, an internal component inside the infotainment system of certain vehicles may overheat and shut down, preventing the rearview image from displaying when drivers are going in reverse.

Some vehicles may show a flipped or inverted rearview image, increasing the risk of crashes.

Ford estimates that all of these vehicles have the defects, but is not aware of any injuries or accidents related to the recalls.

The company is offering a free software update for impacted Bronco and Edge owners, which can be installed at a dealer or through an 'over-the-air' update.

A remedy is still under development for the recall impacting the Escapes, Corsairs, Aviators, and Explorers, but interim letters to notify owners of the safety risks are set to be mailed out in the coming months.

Drivers can visit the NHTSA website or Ford's online recall lookup using their vehicle's VIN number for more information, or call Ford's customer service line at 1-866-436-7332.
