Cincinnati Tops US Metro Areas in Gas Price Spike Since Iran War

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: WLWT Cincinnati

A new report from GasBuddy indicates that Cincinnati has seen the largest spike in gas prices among all US metro areas since the start of the U.S.-Iran war. According to the report, gas stations around Greater Cincinnati have seen an average increase of 80.1 cents between Feb. 28 and March 7 for a regular gallon of gas.

The average price of gas in Cincinnati stands at $3.429 per gallon as of Saturday, up from $2.628 per gallon just one week ago, the date of the U.S. military's first strikes on Iran.

Dayton, Ohio, comes in second place with a 76.4 cent increase, while Fort Wayne, Indiana, is in third place with a 76.1 cent increase. When ranked by average prices seen across states as a whole, Indiana has seen the largest average increase in gas prices, while Ohio has seen the second-largest increase.

The economists attribute this rise in gas prices to the fact that commercial traffic across the Straight of Hormuz has been cut off to the world over the past week as a result of the war, a passageway that sees 20% of all oil consumed worldwide pass through it off of Iran's coast.
