US Small Businesses Face 'Silver Tsunami' as Baby Boomer Owners Retire

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Fox Business

A looming 'silver tsunami' of retiring baby boomer business owners could dramatically reshape America's small-business landscape, experts warn.

Nearly half of U.S. small-business owners are 55 or older, yet just 54% have a succession plan in place, setting the stage for a potential retirement shock that could leave many companies vulnerable over the next decade, according to Forbes.

Small businesses employ more than 62 million Americans and account for roughly 43% of U.S. GDP, according to the U.S. Small Business Administration.

According to American Operator founder William Fry, if a significant share of those businesses close instead of transitioning to new leadership, communities nationwide could feel the effects.

Fry told FOX Business, 'They're huge creators of wealth, and in my opinion, they're the most pure version of the American Dream — you come to this country, and you can build a better life for yourself.'

Many owners have spent decades building their businesses on relationships and reputation, making succession decisions deeply personal.

Erik and Kassie Hansen, the husband-and-wife team behind Greenway Painting in Jackson, Wyoming, began considering their next chapter after 12 years growing their business.

By 2024, 90% of the company's revenue came from commercial clients, and 85% came from repeat customers, a testament to the loyalty they had cultivated.

Erik Hansen told FOX Business, 'All of our clients are very important. Especially in a small town, they are like friends or family when you work with them for years and years. You want to make sure they get well taken care of. That's important.'

Companies like American Operator aim to bridge the succession gap by pairing retiring owners with operators ready to take the reins.

Anthony Douglas, a former U.S. Air Force combat controller, became CEO and a day-one equity partner of Greenway Painting in October 2025, acquiring a 10% stake with a structured path toward majority ownership over time.

'One of the reasons why I was attracted to Greenway Painting was that it was a relational business,' Douglas told FOX Business. 'Greenway was pretty successful without having any advertising or marketing.'
