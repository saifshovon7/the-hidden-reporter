Microsoft Warns of Widespread AI Abuse in Cyberattacks

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: BleepingComputer

Microsoft has issued a warning about the increasing use of artificial intelligence in cyberattacks, with threat actors exploiting AI at every stage of their operations to accelerate attacks and lower technical barriers.

According to a new report from Microsoft Threat Intelligence, attackers are using generative AI tools for a wide range of tasks, including reconnaissance, phishing, infrastructure development, malware creation, and post-compromise activity.

In many cases, AI is used to draft phishing emails, translate content, summarize stolen data, debug malware, and assist with scripting or infrastructure configuration.

Microsoft has observed multiple threat groups incorporating AI into their cyberattacks, including North Korean actors tracked as Jasper Sleet and Coral Sleet, who use the technology as part of remote IT worker schemes.

Jasper Sleet leverages generative AI platforms to streamline the development of fraudulent digital personas, including generating culturally appropriate name lists and email address formats to match specific identity profiles.

The report also describes how AI is being used to assist with malware development and infrastructure creation, with threat actors using AI coding tools to generate and refine malicious code, troubleshoot errors, or port malware components to different programming languages.

Microsoft observed Coral Sleet using AI to quickly generate fake company sites, provision infrastructure, and test and troubleshoot their deployments.
