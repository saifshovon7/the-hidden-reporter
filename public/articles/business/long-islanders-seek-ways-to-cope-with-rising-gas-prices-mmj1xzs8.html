Long Islanders Seek Ways to Cope with Rising Gas Prices

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Newsday

Rising gasoline prices on Long Island are causing residents to look for ways to lessen the impact. The cost of gas has jumped more than 13% since the Iran conflict disrupted the production and movement of oil in the Middle East.

“I watch the news, and it doesn’t look like this war is going to end soon, which means we’re going to see even higher prices at the pump,” said Marie Weiss, 71, a retired teacher from Lynbrook.

The average price of regular unleaded gasoline on Long Island was $3.25 per gallon on Friday, up 12 cents overnight and 39 cents from Feb. 28, when the United States and Israel began strikes against Iran, according to AAA data.

“It’s only going to get worse,” said Robert Sinclair Jr., senior manager at AAA Northeast, “but there are steps you can take to improve your situation.”

He and others advised motorists to maintain their vehicles, make fewer trips and change their driving habits to maximize the number of miles they get out of each tank.

According to Sinclair, cars and trucks run more efficiently with new spark plugs and fuel injectors as well as regular oil changes. Fully synthetic oil can slightly increase engine horsepower.

“Tire pressure is also critical and needs to be checked at least once a month and ideally once a week” he said, adding that fuel economy is reduced by 1% for every tire that’s underinflated by one pound of pressure.

Patrick De Haan, head of petroleum analysis at GasBuddy.com, said motorists can save 4 to 9 cents per gallon by filling up on Sundays, which tend to be the most affordable day.

Kevin Beyer, vice president of government affairs at the Long Island Gasoline Retailers Association, advised drivers to buy now or risk paying more in the future.

To avoid being overcharged, drivers can make sure the price advertised on the gas station’s sign is the same as the one on the pump, and check that it doesn’t change while you are pumping, the state Division of Consumer Protection said.

Drivers also can often save “several cents per gallon” if they pay in cash, the division said.

Finally, Sinclair said pump prices are generally lower at independent stations and warehouse clubs, such as BJ’s Wholesale Club.
