Middle East War Threatens Global Economy, Oil Prices

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Seeking Alpha

Qatar's energy minister has issued a stark warning about the potential consequences of the ongoing war in the Middle East, stating that it could bring down the economies of the world.

In an alarming prediction, the minister said that all Persian Gulf energy exporters would likely shut down production within days, sending oil prices skyrocketing to $150 per barrel.

The war has already had a significant impact on global oil markets, with prices increasing in recent weeks.

The minister's warning highlights the potential for widespread economic disruption and instability if the conflict continues to escalate.

The international community is continuing to monitor the situation closely, with many countries calling for a peaceful resolution to the conflict.
