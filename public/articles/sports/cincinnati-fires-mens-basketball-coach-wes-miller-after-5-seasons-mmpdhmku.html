Cincinnati Fires Men's Basketball Coach Wes Miller After 5 Seasons

Cincinnati has fired men's basketball coach Wes Miller after five seasons, citing the team's inability to reach the NCAA tournament. Miller's contract states that he is owed $9.9 million if fired before March 31, 2026.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 13, 2026 · Source: ESPN

Cincinnati has fired men's basketball coach Wes Miller after five seasons, according to sources. Miller's tenure was marked by the team's inability to reach the NCAA tournament in any of his five seasons.

The Bearcats finished the 2023-24 season with an 18-15 record, despite a strong finish to the season where they won seven of nine games. However, they failed to secure a spot in the NCAA tournament after losing 66-65 in overtime to UCF in the Big 12 tournament.

Miller was hired in 2021 to replace John Brannen, following a successful 10-year run at UNC Greensboro. During his time at UNC Greensboro, Miller led the Spartans to three regular-season championships and two NCAA tournament appearances. He went on to win at least 21 games in each of his final five seasons.

Despite his initial success at Cincinnati, Miller was unable to lead the team to the NCAA tournament in either of his last two seasons. The Bearcats entered the 2023-24 season with high expectations, but ultimately fell short, finishing 7-13 in Big 12 play.

Miller's contract states that he is owed $9.9 million if fired before March 31, 2026, and $4.69 million if fired on April 1 or later.

Miller is the son of Kenneth D. Miller, whose name is on the Wake Forest athletic facility. He began his college career at James Madison before transferring to North Carolina, where he won a national championship with the Tar Heels in 2005.
