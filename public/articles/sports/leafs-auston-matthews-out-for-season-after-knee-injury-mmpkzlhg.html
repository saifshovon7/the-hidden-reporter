Leafs' Auston Matthews Out for Season After Knee Injury

Toronto Maple Leafs captain Auston Matthews will miss the remainder of the 2025-26 NHL season due to a knee injury sustained in a game against the Anaheim Ducks. Matthews will be re-evaluated in two weeks.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 14, 2026 · Source: ESPN

Toronto Maple Leafs captain Auston Matthews will miss the remainder of the 2025-26 NHL season due to a torn medial collateral ligament in his left knee, the team announced.

The injury occurred on Thursday night when Matthews took a knee-on-knee hit from Radko Gudas during a game against the Anaheim Ducks. Gudas was given a major penalty and ejected from the game.

Matthews, 28, suffered a Grade 3 MCL tear and also bruised his quadriceps muscle in the incident. He will be re-evaluated in two weeks.

Leafs coach Craig Berube described the hit as a 'dirty play' and expressed disappointment that none of Matthews' teammates intervened to engage with Gudas after the incident.

Matthews finishes the season with 27 goals and 53 points in 60 games. The Leafs are currently third-to-last in the Eastern Conference with a 28-27-11 record.

The team's loss of Matthews will be a significant blow as they take on the Atlantic Division-leading Buffalo Sabres on Saturday.
