Titans Release CB L'Jarius Sneed, Clear $11.4M in Cap Space

The Tennessee Titans are releasing cornerback L'Jarius Sneed, saving $11.4 million in cap space. Sneed struggled with injuries during his time with the team.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 13, 2026 · Source: ESPN

The Tennessee Titans are releasing cornerback L'Jarius Sneed, according to sources, a move that frees up $11.4 million in cap space.

Sneed, who signed a four-year, $76 million contract after being acquired by the Titans in a 2024 trade with the Kansas City Chiefs, struggled to stay on the field for Tennessee, appearing in 12 games over two seasons.

Despite being a two-time Super Bowl champion with the Chiefs, who drafted him in the fourth round in 2020, Sneed's time with the Titans was marked by injuries, including a knee procedure in May and a lingering quad injury that forced him to start the 2025 season on the physically unable to perform list.

He played seven games last season and finished with 26 tackles and three passes defended, but ultimately suffered another quad injury in Week 7 against the New England Patriots and missed the final 10 games of the season after going on injured reserve.

The Titans signed defensive backs Alontae Taylor, Cor'Dale Flott, and Josh Williams earlier this week to restock their cornerbacks room.
