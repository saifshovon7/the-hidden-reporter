Epic Raises Fortnite V-Bucks Price Amid Cost Increases

Epic Games has announced that it will be increasing the price of Fortnite's V-Bucks, citing rising costs as the reason. The price hike will take effect on March 19, and will see the cost of V-Bucks increase, while bonus currency rewards will be removed from the game's main battle pass.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Epic Games, the developer of Fortnite, has announced that it will be increasing the price of the game's in-game currency, V-Bucks, to help cover rising costs.

The price hike, which will take effect on March 19, will see the cost of V-Bucks increase, while bonus currency rewards will be removed from the game's main battle pass.

However, players will see a decrease in the cost of the battle pass, an optional seasonal pass that players pay for to get special cosmetics.

Fortnite is estimated to generate billions of dollars in revenue every year, making it one of the world's most successful games.

The game makes most of its money through in-game subscriptions and passes, known as microtransactions.

The price increases mean that players will get fewer V-Bucks when buying the in-game currency, and people who are subscribed to Fortnite's monthly "Crew" membership will receive 800 V-Bucks a month rather than 1,000.

The main battle pass will now cost 800 V-Bucks, down from 1,000, and the game's other three passes will also see price drops of 200.

Some fans have expressed dismay at the price hike, questioning the justification given for the increase, particularly given Google's recent settlement with Epic over in-app purchase fees.

Freelance games journalist Vic Hood told the BBC that the decision may come as a surprise given a recent reveal by Epic that player spending on third-party titles had reached an all-time record of $400 million.

However, Hood noted that the Epic Games Store had also seen a decline in third-party spending across 2023/2024.

The games industry has seen a wave of price increases in hardware, software, and subscription prices over the past year, driven by rising development costs, inflation, and tariffs.
