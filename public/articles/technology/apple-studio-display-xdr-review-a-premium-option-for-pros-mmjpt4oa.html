Apple Studio Display XDR Review: A Premium Option for Pros

Apple's Studio Display XDR boasts a new mini-LED display with 2,304 local dimming zones and adaptive refresh rate up to 120Hz, making it a great option for creative professionals. However, its starting price of $3,299 may be a barrier for many.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: The Verge

Apple has revamped its Studio Display lineup with two new models: the Studio Display and the Studio Display XDR. While the former may look familiar, the latter is a more interesting option, boasting a completely new mini-LED display with 2,304 local dimming zones, a max brightness of 2,000 nits, and adaptive refresh rate up to 120Hz.

Advertisement

The Studio Display XDR's panel is the most advanced we've seen on a standalone Apple monitor, utilizing mini-LEDs with a quantum dot-based optical stack and IPS LCD panel. Images and text are crisp and clear, with dimming control able to tame light bleed from bright images next to dark areas pretty well.

Professionals will love the accurate and plentiful reference modes, native support for both P3 and Adobe RGB color spaces, and two general picture modes calibrated to Apple CMF 2026. However, the starting price of $3,299 may be a barrier for many.

The Studio Display XDR is replacing the 32-inch 6K Pro Display XDR, which cost $5,000 and was released in 2020. While it's a significant upgrade, it's still a premium option and may not be the best choice for those on a budget.
