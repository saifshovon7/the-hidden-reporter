Pentagon's Anthropic Controversy May Deter Startups from Defense Work

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

In a series of events over the past week, the Pentagon's use of Anthropic's Claude technology has been marred by controversy, including a designation as a supply-chain risk by the Trump administration and a court challenge from Anthropic itself.

Meanwhile, OpenAI has announced a deal with the Pentagon, which has sparked backlash and led to a surge in popularity for Anthropic's Claude on the App Store.

On the latest episode of TechCrunch's Equity podcast, industry experts discussed the implications of this controversy for startups seeking to work with the federal government, particularly the Pentagon.

Kirsten Korosec, Sean O'Kane, and a third expert weighed in on the issue, with Korosec wondering whether this controversy might lead to a change in the way startups approach federal contracts.

Advertisement

Sean O'Kane noted that the situation is unusual due to the high profile of OpenAI and Claude, as well as the fact that the dispute centers on the use of technologies that could be used to harm people.

However, Korosec argued that this controversy should give any startup pause, as it highlights the potential risks and scrutiny that come with working with the federal government.

The experts also touched on the fact that many startups and established companies already work with the government on defense-related projects, often without drawing much attention.

General Motors, for example, has a long history of producing defense vehicles for the Army and has explored electric and autonomous versions of these vehicles.

Despite this, the Anthropic controversy may still serve as a cautionary tale for startups considering federal contracts, particularly those related to defense work.
