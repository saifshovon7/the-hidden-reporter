Mabe Fratti's Experimental Cello Pop Explores New Age, Industrial, and Folk

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: The Verge

Mabe Fratti's 2024 record Sentir Que No Sabes is an experimental cello pop album that dabbles in new age, industrial, and folk elements without feeling disjointed.

The album's opening track, 'Kravitz', features a haunting cello sound that sets the tone for the rest of the record. The cello is plucked like an upright bass, creating a unique and captivating sound.

Fratti's lyrics are paranoid and introspective, often exploring themes of surveillance and control. The addition of horns and piano adds a level of depth and complexity to the album's sound.

Fratti's musical influences are eclectic, ranging from Christian and classical music to experimental composers like György Ligeti. This diverse background is evident in tracks like 'Pantalla Azul', which flits between goth rock and new age.

The production from I. La Católica (Héctor Tosta) is key to holding together Fratti's stylistic shifts and cello manipulations. The album's undercurrent of unease and lightly crushed drums provides a thread that ties the disparate pieces together.

While Fratti indulges her more abstract musical inclinations on interludes like 'Elástica' I and II, the album's core sound is firmly rooted in pop music. This makes Sentir Que No Sabes a compelling listen for fans of experimental and pop music alike.
