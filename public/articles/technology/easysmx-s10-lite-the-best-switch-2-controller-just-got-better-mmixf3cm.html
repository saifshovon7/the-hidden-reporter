EasySMX S10 Lite: The Best Switch 2 Controller Just Got Better

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: The Verge

The EasySMX S10 Lite is the latest iteration of the popular Switch 2 controller, and it's a game-changer. Building on the success of the original S10, the S10 Lite offers native Switch 2 support for easy wake-up and per-game rear paddle customization.

One of the biggest advantages of the S10 Lite is its native support for the Switch 2. This means that it can quickly pair to the console and remotely wake it by tapping the Home button, just like Nintendo's pricey controller. It's also compatible with the original Switch, although the S10 Lite's native OS-level integration and GameChat button don't work on that platform.

The S10 Lite's native support also lets you configure the controller's GL and GR rear paddles through the Switch 2's operating system by holding the controller's Home button while in-game. This is a major improvement over other third-party controllers, which require manual configuration every time you change games.

However, the S10 Lite is not without its omissions. It lacks amiibo support, and its rumble doesn't feel as high quality as the original S10. Still, the Lite's rumble is better than what 8BitDo's Switch controllers offer.

The S10 Lite's native support is a major perk, and it's something that other companies are likely to copy. However, the decision between the S10 Lite and the original S10 ultimately comes down to personal preference. If you value the convenience of native Switch 2 support and per-game rear paddle customization, the S10 Lite is the better choice. But if you're willing to sacrifice those features for a lower price and better rumble, the original S10 is still a great option.
