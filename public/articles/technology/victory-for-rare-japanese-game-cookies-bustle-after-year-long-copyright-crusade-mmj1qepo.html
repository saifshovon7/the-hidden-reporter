Victory for Rare Japanese Game Cookie's Bustle After Year-Long Copyright Crusade

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Kotaku

The Video Game History Foundation has announced a significant victory in the case of the obscure Japanese game Cookie's Bustle. The game, which has been the subject of a year-long copyright crusade, has finally been liberated from the clutches of copyright claims.

For those unfamiliar, Cookie's Bustle is a rare and surreal '90s Japanese PC game that has been the subject of a concerted effort to erase its online presence. Since 2022, an individual known as Brandon White, operating under the company Graceware, has filed numerous copyright claims against any online material related to the game, including YouTube videos, Twitch streams, fan art, ROM sites, and Discord posts.

The strikes extended far beyond YouTube, with the Video Game History Foundation itself being targeted when a physical copy of the game was donated to the organization. The page created to display the game was also hit with a copyright strike, which disrupted the web host's archive.

Despite the disruption, the VGHF saw this as an opportunity to investigate the matter further. In a lengthy post, the organization's library director, Phil Salvador, explained how they went on the offensive, uncovering a network of non-scrupulous firms and copyright services used by White to launch the campaign.

White had used a smokescreen to utilize resources from the Association for UK Interactive Entertainment (Ukie), despite having no evidence of ownership of Cookie's Bustle and even misspelling his own company as Gracewear in the Ukie membership. The VGHF confronted Ukie, which was unable to prompt the web host to drop the game's entry a second time.

The creator of Cookie's Bustle remains unknown, making it an orphaned game. However, the VGHF's victory has solved many of the 'how' questions but not the 'why' behind the copyright crusade. The game is now free from copyright claims, and YouTube account ClassicsOfGame has triumphantly reuploaded its content.
