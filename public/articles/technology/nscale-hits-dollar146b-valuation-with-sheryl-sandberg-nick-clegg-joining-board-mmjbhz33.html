Nscale Hits $14.6B Valuation with Sheryl Sandberg, Nick Clegg Joining Board

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

Nvidia-backed British AI infrastructure company Nscale has reached a $14.6 billion valuation, making it one of Europe's latest decacorns. The valuation stems from a $2 billion Series C funding round, which includes a $433 million pre-Series C SAFE backed by Blue Owl, Dell, Nvidia, and Nokia in October.

The funding was led by Aker, a public Norwegian company with interests in energy, and co-led by New York-based investment firm 8090 Industries. As part of the deal, Aker's joint venture with Nscale will now be fully managed by the startup.

Nscale has ambitious plans to run on 100,000 Nvidia GPUs by the end of 2026, with OpenAI as an initial customer. The company is also expanding its data center infrastructure, having signed an expanded deal with Microsoft to bring approximately 200,000 Nvidia GPUs to three data centers in Europe and one in the U.S.

Alongside its funding and plans, Nscale announced that former Meta COO Sheryl Sandberg, former Yahoo president Susan Decker, and former UK deputy prime minister Nick Clegg are joining its board. Nscale CEO Josh Payne has stated that his company might seek to go public "as early as this year" to generate more capital.

The company's vertical integration strategy, which includes energy and data centers to compute and orchestration software, has been a key factor in its success. Nscale expects the new funding to accelerate the development of its AI infrastructure across Europe, North America, and other regions.
