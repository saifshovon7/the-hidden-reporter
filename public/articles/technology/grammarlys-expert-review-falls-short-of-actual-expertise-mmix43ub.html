Grammarly's 'Expert Review' Falls Short of Actual Expertise

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

A feature in Grammarly's writing assistant, Expert Review, purports to improve users' writing with suggestions from the world's great writers and thinkers, including some tech journalists.

However, a closer look reveals that the feedback is not actually coming from these experts, but rather generated by AI algorithms that reference their published works.

Grammarly's vice president of product and corporate marketing, Alex Gay, told The Verge that the experts are mentioned because their works are publicly available and widely cited.

Advertisement

In its user guide, Grammarly clarifies that references to experts are for informational purposes only and do not indicate any affiliation with the company or endorsement by the individuals or entities.

Despite this, the feature has raised questions about its validity, with historian C.E. Aubin telling Wired that 'these are not expert reviews, because there are no 'experts' involved in producing them'.

Grammarly's use of well-known authors and journalists' names may be seen as misleading, and some users may feel that the feature is not living up to its promise of providing expert feedback.
