Meta Urged to Boost Oversight of Fake AI Videos

Meta's Oversight Board has called on the company to boost oversight of fake AI videos on its platforms, citing concerns about the proliferation of such content during global military conflicts.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Meta, the social media giant, has been urged by its own advisors to do more to address the proliferation of fake content made with artificial intelligence (AI) tools on its platforms.

The 21-person Oversight Board raised concerns about the company's handling of an AI-generated video that claimed to show extensive damage in Haifa, Israel by Iranian forces without a label.

The board rebuked the company for leaving up the video and called on Meta to overhaul its AI rules, warning that an increase in fake AI videos related to global military conflicts had challenged the public's ability to distinguish fabrication from fact.

The board said the firm's current methods were neither robust nor comprehensive enough to contend with the scale and velocity of AI-generated content, particularly during a crisis or conflict.

Meta launched the Oversight Board in 2020 as a semi-independent group providing supervision of content moderation decisions across its platforms, which include Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp.

The board said the firm should be proactively labelling fake AI content "much more frequently" and that relying on users to self-disclose when content they post is produced by an AI tool was not enough.

The issue was sparked by a video posted last June by a Facebook account based in the Philippines describing itself as a news source, which quickly collected at least 100 million views.

Despite receiving several user complaints, Meta did not label the video as AI-generated or remove it until a Facebook user appealed directly to the Oversight Board.

The company then claimed the video did not require any kind of label and did not need to be taken down because it did not directly contribute to the risk of imminent physical harm.

The board said this was too high of a bar for labeling AI-generated content, particularly when the subject is armed conflict.
