Can Snacks Really Help You Sleep?

Alice Mushrooms' Nightcap chocolate, featuring reishi mushrooms and chamomile, claims to improve sleep quality. However, the scientific evidence behind its ingredients is mixed, with some effects being short-term and influenced by placebo effects.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 6, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Alice Mushrooms, a mushroom-infused chocolate brand, has gained a cult following since its launch three years ago. Its original product, Nightcap, is now available in 2,000 stores across the US and features reishi mushrooms, chamomile, magnesium, and zinc that claim to improve sleep quality.

Developed by co-founders Charlotte Cruze and Lindsay Goodstein, Nightcap includes L-theanine, an amino acid found in green tea, which is said to have a relaxing effect on the body to aid sleep.

Cruze notes that sleep is a complex issue, and the company worked with homeopathic doctors, PhD researchers, and product formulators to create a product that can help with every part of the sleep cycle.

The growing demand for sleep-enhancing products is reflected in a survey by Sleepopolis, which found that 47% of Americans take a sleep aid, with natural supplements like melatonin being the most popular.

However, the scientific evidence behind these trendy products is mixed. While the long-term sleep benefits of magnesium and melatonin have been proven, the effects of chamomile and reishi mushrooms appear to be short-term and may be influenced by placebo effects.

Other sleep-enhancing snack foods include Goodnite dark chocolate sleep bites, Lagoon's Night Bites, and Good Day Chocolate's "Calm" range, which all feature a combination of ingredients like L-theanine, melatonin, and magnesium.

As the sleep treats category becomes more competitive, consumers are left wondering if these products really work or if they're just a fad.
