Vibe-Coded OS Falls Short in Usability

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Futurism

Last year, OpenAI cofounder Andrej Karpathy coined the term 'vibe coding,' a new approach involving the rapid development of software by feeding an AI model a series of natural language prompts. But the popular approach comes with some glaring shortcomings, as evidenced in an entirely vibe-coded operating system, dubbed Vib-OS.

The project, hosted on GitHub, can be run on ARM64 and x86-based computers and features a 'custom kernel,' a 'modern macOS-inspired graphical user interface,' and a 'virtual file system.' However, a recent video by YouTuber Tirimid highlights the limitations of relying on vibe-coding to quickly construct complex pieces of software.

Advertisement

Tirimid discovered that the operating system, in its current state, is a buggy and largely unusable mess. The YouTuber spent several hours going through the operating system's purported features, finding that it failed to connect to the internet, buttons in the File Manager didn't respond to clicks, the Notepad app refused to save any documents, and games, such as Snake, didn't work very well.

Additionally, an app labeled 'Browser' turned out to be an image viewer, not a web browser that can render websites. Despite the documentation noting that the OS was capable of running Python scripts, Tirimid was also disappointed to find there was no support for the programming language.

Netizens were amused by the YouTuber's experience, with some comparing it to an early build of Windows 12. Tirimid begged others not to harass the creators of Vib-OS, stating that while he was negative about many parts of the OS, he didn't want others to be negative in the issues section.
