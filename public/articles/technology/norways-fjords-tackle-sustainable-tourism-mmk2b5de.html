Norway's Fjords Tackle Sustainable Tourism

Norway's fjords are pioneering sustainable tourism practices, including eco-friendly transportation and electronic waste recycling, to minimize the environmental impact of tourism.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · February 14, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

As the world grapples with the challenges of climate change, Norway's picturesque fjords are at the forefront of a sustainable tourism revolution. With over 1 million visitors in 2022, the country is working to minimize the environmental impact of this growing industry.

Journalist Alasdair Keane recently boarded an electric boat in Norway's stunning fjords, a symbol of the country's commitment to eco-friendly transportation. The vessel, powered by a state-of-the-art battery system, is just one example of the innovative solutions being implemented to reduce emissions and preserve the region's natural beauty.

Meanwhile, in a separate project, researcher Yasmin Morgan-Griffiths is exploring ways to make electronic circuits in devices more recyclable. Her work aims to close the loop on e-waste, a pressing global issue that sees millions of tons of discarded electronics ending up in landfills each year.

Norway's efforts to balance tourism and sustainability are being closely watched by other countries, as the world seeks to find a balance between economic growth and environmental protection.

By investing in green technologies and implementing responsible practices, Norway's fjords are serving as a model for sustainable tourism, demonstrating that it is possible to preserve the natural world while still catering to the demands of visitors.
