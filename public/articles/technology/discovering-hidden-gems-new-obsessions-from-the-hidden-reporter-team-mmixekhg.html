Discovering Hidden Gems: New Obsessions from The Hidden Reporter Team

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: The Verge

The Hidden Reporter team is always on the lookout for new and exciting art to share with our readers. From slept-on post-punk albums to new sci-fi novels and cult classic horror movies, we're constantly discovering new obsessions to share with you.

Sometimes, these discoveries are new releases, but often they're something a little older, something not necessarily plastered all over social media or sitting at the top of the charts. We believe that the best way to find new music, a new show to binge, or a self-help book that's worth reading is to skip the algorithm and get a recommendation from actual humans.

Our team is comprised of those humans, and we're excited to share our latest discoveries with you. Check back regularly for new art to fall in love with, and tell us about your latest obsessions in the comments.

Mabe Fratti's 2024 record Sentir Que No Sabes is a genre-hopping experimental cello pop masterpiece. The opening notes of "Kravitz" are lodged in my brain permanently, with a buzzing cello sound that's both industrial and jazzy. The album blooms with big piano chords and drums, straying further from Fratti's avant-garde roots.

Laurie Spiegel's 1980 album The Expanding Universe is a marvel of early ambient music, blending synth experimentalism with algorithmic composition techniques. Tracks like "Patchwork" and "A Folk Study" dabble in bouncy arpeggios, while "Old Wave" and "East River Dawn" conjure early M83 or Boards of Canada.

Lastly, we recommend watching the intensely surreal cult classic Eraserhead by David Lynch, a must-see for fans of surrealist cinema.
