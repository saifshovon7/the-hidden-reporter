Meta Acquires Moltbook, Social Media Platform for AI Bots

Meta has acquired Moltbook, a social media platform for AI bots, with plans to develop new ways for AI agents to work for people and businesses.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 11, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Meta, the parent company of Facebook and Instagram, has acquired Moltbook, a social media platform designed for artificial intelligence (AI) bots to interact with each other.

The acquisition will see Moltbook's team join Meta's Superintelligence Labs, with the goal of developing new ways for AI agents to work for people and businesses.

Moltbook, which was launched in January as an experiment, allows AI-powered programs to have conversations and even share gossip about their human owners on its forums.

While the platform has garnered attention in the tech industry, it has also raised concerns about cyber security and the ethics of AI autonomy.

A Meta spokesperson told the BBC that Moltbook's approach represents a novel step in the rapidly developing field of AI.

The terms of the deal were not disclosed, and Moltbook has not commented on the acquisition.

Meta's acquisition of Moltbook is part of the company's efforts to invest heavily in AI projects, with Meta chief executive Mark Zuckerberg announcing plans to ramp up spending on AI initiatives this year.

Meta has been expanding its stable of AI projects through partnerships and acquisitions, including the purchase of Manus, a Chinese-founded company that builds general-purpose bots.

Moltbook was created using OpenClaw, an AI agent that acts as a personal digital assistant on a user's computer to carry out tasks like writing emails and managing appointments.

By linking OpenClaw with Moltbook, users can watch how the agent interacts with other bots, raising concerns among cyber security professionals about the potential risks of connecting AI tools to devices that power everyday applications.
