Life EV Acquires Rad Power Bikes for $13.2 Million

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 6, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

Life Electric Vehicles Holdings, also known as Life EV, has officially acquired the intellectual property, inventory, and certain operating assets of Rad Power Bikes.

The acquisition, which was made for $13.2 million, marks a significant move for Life EV, which has been expanding its business by acquiring, developing, and scaling electric bicycle and micro-mobility brands.

Rad Power Bikes, a popular electric bike company that raised nearly $330 million in venture capital, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in December after struggling for months.

Despite its financial struggles, Rad Power Bikes had built a strong brand and loyal customer base, and Life EV has pledged to continue retail operations under the Rad Power Bikes brand in the United States.

The company also plans to expand its retail footprint in select key markets and support existing customers who may be wondering about their bike warranties or gift cards.

This acquisition is part of Life EV's broader strategy to expand across North America, and the company has already made significant investments in the electric bicycle and micro-mobility space.

Life EV's acquisition of Rad Power Bikes is a significant development in the electric bike industry, and it will be interesting to see how the company moves forward in the coming months.
