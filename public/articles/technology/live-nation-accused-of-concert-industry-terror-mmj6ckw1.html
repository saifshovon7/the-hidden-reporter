Live Nation Accused of Concert Industry Terror

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: The Verge

A core theme in the ongoing antitrust trial against Live Nation-Ticketmaster is retaliation, a charge the company denies.

Several witnesses have testified that Live Nation's promoters would withhold concerts from venues that didn't use Ticketmaster, creating fear and a perceived threat.

Some major venues declined to switch to what they viewed as a better ticketing product, fearing Live Nation's wrath, according to witnesses.

SeatGeek CEO Jack Groetzinger testified that venues' fear of losing concerts was costing his company business.

Groetzinger recalled that a deal with the Dallas Cowboys was nearly derailed by the 'concert issue,' which centered on Live Nation's control over concert bookings.

SeatGeek considered offering 'retaliation insurance' to protect venues from losing revenue if Live Nation passed them over for concerts.

The company would have had to set aside a large sum of money for the insurance, which Groetzinger found 'really uncomfortable.'

The antitrust trial has raised questions about the concert industry's competitive landscape and the potential for changes in the wake of a settlement.
