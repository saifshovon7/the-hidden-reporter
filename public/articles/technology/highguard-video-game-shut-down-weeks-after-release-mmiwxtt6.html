Highguard Video Game Shut Down Weeks After Release

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 4, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Highguard, a multiplayer shooter video game, will shut down on 12 March, just weeks after its release. The game's developer, Wildlight Entertainment, announced the decision, citing the game's inability to retain players.

Despite being developed by a team of industry veterans who worked on successful titles like Call of Duty, Apex Legends, and Titanfall, Highguard struggled to attract and retain players. The game's launch in January saw around 100,000 players on PC and 380,000 viewers on Twitch, but these numbers plummeted in the following weeks.

Advertisement

Wildlight Entertainment laid off multiple staff members just two weeks after the game's launch, and the game's player base continued to dwindle. According to SteamDB, the game's peak concurrent users were around 460 on the day the layoffs were announced, and just 3,600 on the day the game was released.

The game's developers asked fans to 'jump in with us one more time to show your support' and get in one last game before the servers shut down. They expressed gratitude to fans for playing and supporting the game, and for being part of Highguard's story.
