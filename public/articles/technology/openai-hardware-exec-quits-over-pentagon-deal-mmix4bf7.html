OpenAI Hardware Exec Quits Over Pentagon Deal

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

Caitlin Kalinowski, the executive leading OpenAI's hardware team, has resigned in response to the company's controversial agreement with the Department of Defense.

In a social media post, Kalinowski stated that the decision was 'about principle, not people' and expressed her 'deep respect' for CEO Sam Altman and the OpenAI team.

Kalinowski emphasized that her issue was with the rushed announcement and lack of defined guardrails, rather than the agreement itself.

OpenAI's agreement with the Pentagon was announced over a week ago, after discussions with Anthropic fell through due to disagreements over safeguards preventing domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons.

The Pentagon subsequently designated Anthropic a supply-chain risk, prompting Microsoft, Google, and Amazon to continue making Anthropic's Claude available to non-defense customers.

OpenAI's agreement with the Pentagon allows its technology to be used in classified environments, sparking concerns over the lack of judicial oversight and human authorization.

An OpenAI spokesperson confirmed Kalinowski's departure, stating that the company believes its agreement with the Pentagon creates a workable path for responsible national security uses of AI.
