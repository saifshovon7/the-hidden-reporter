Norway's Fjords: Can Sustainable Tourism Coexist?

The Hidden Reporter Staff February 14, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Alasdair Keane recently had the opportunity to climb aboard an electric boat in Norway, a country known for its breathtaking fjords and stunning natural beauty.

While Norway's tourism industry is a significant contributor to the country's economy, it also poses a threat to the environment. The influx of visitors can lead to erosion, pollution, and strain on local infrastructure.

In an effort to balance the economic benefits of tourism with environmental concerns, Norway is exploring sustainable options.

One such initiative is the use of electric boats, like the one Alasdair rode, which produce zero emissions and reduce the carbon footprint of tourism.

Yasmin Morgan-Griffiths, meanwhile, has been investigating ways to make electronic circuits in our devices more recyclable.

As technology continues to advance and electronic waste grows, finding ways to recycle and reuse these materials is becoming increasingly important.

From sustainable tourism practices to innovative recycling methods, Norway is setting an example for other countries to follow.
