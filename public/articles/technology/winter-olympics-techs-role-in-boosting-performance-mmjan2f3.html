Winter Olympics: Tech's Role in Boosting Performance

The Hidden Reporter Staff January 31, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Alasdair Keane visited the Olympic training centre in Oslo to explore how advances in technology are aiding Norway's efforts to excel in the Winter Olympics.

From high-tech prosthetics to sophisticated data analysis, technology is playing an increasingly significant role in enhancing athletes' performances.

At the centre, Keane spoke with experts and athletes about the various ways in which technology is being applied to improve training methods, equipment design, and athlete safety.

One key area of focus is the use of data analytics to gain a competitive edge. By collecting and analysing vast amounts of data on factors such as speed, distance, and power output, athletes and coaches can identify areas for improvement and develop targeted training programmes.

High-tech prosthetics are also being used to enhance the performance of athletes with disabilities. For example, a Norwegian skier with a prosthetic leg is able to compete at the highest level thanks to a specially designed prosthetic that provides unparalleled support and stability.

In addition to these technological advancements, the Olympic training centre is also investing in cutting-edge equipment such as 3D printing and virtual reality. These tools are being used to create customised training scenarios and simulate real-world racing conditions, allowing athletes to train in a highly realistic and immersive environment.

As the Winter Olympics continue to push the boundaries of human performance, it is clear that technology will play an increasingly important role in the years to come. By leveraging advances in fields such as data analytics, prosthetics, and equipment design, athletes and coaches can gain a vital edge in the pursuit of Olympic glory.
