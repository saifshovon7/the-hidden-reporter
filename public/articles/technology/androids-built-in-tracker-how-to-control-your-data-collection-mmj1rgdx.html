Android's Built-in Tracker: How to Control Your Data Collection

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: MakeUseOf

Kanika is a seasoned tech writer with a passion for exploring the latest trends and innovations. She has been writing about consumer technology since 2019 and has contributed to various tech websites. In this article, she delves into the world of Android's built-in tracker, explaining how it works and how users can control their data collection.

Most people are aware that their smartphones collect some data, but many are unaware of the extent to which this data is collected in the background. Search activity, app usage, location history, and more can be stored automatically, often without users even realizing it. Kanika recently stumbled upon her own activity history, which had a detailed record of places she had visited earlier.

Advertisement

Fortunately, Android allows users to control what gets tracked and stored. By understanding the various features that collect activity data, users can limit or even disable them entirely. In this article, Kanika shares her experience of adjusting a few settings and regaining a bit more privacy.

Android's built-in tracker is not a single entity, but rather a collection of features in the Google account that collect activity data for different services. These features include location history, web and app activity, and more. While these features are designed to offer a personalized experience, not everyone wants that level of data collection and personalization.

To control your data collection, start by checking what data has already been stored on your phone. The easiest way to do this is to check the Google Maps Timeline, which shows the places you've visited, dates and times of your past trips, and even the routes you've taken. From there, you can decide which features you want to turn off and regain more control over your data.

By understanding how Android's built-in tracker works and taking control of your data collection, you can enjoy a more private and personalized experience on your device.
