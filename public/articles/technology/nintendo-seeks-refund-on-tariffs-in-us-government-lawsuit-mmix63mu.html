Nintendo Seeks Refund on Tariffs in US Government Lawsuit

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 6, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

Nintendo filed a lawsuit against the U.S. government on Friday, seeking a refund for any tariffs it paid due to President Donald Trump's executive orders that invoked the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA).

The gaming giant is one of over a thousand companies that have sued for refunds on the tariffs, which have resulted in the collection of over $200 billion on imports in total, according to Nintendo's complaint.

The lawsuit comes after a Supreme Court decision struck down the tariffs imposed under IEEPA, arguing that the president exceeded his authority.

President Trump had raised tariffs from 10% to 15% in response to the Supreme Court's decision, which he called "extraordinarily anti-American."

Now, 24 states have sued to argue that the president has once again overstepped the limits of his power by making this change.

Nintendo told TechCrunch in a statement that it could confirm the filing of the request but had nothing else to share on the topic.
