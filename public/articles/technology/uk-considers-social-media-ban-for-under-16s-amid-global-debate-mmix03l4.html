UK Considers Social Media Ban for Under-16s Amid Global Debate

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 1, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

The UK government has launched a public consultation on whether to ban social media for under-16s, sparking a global debate on the issue.

The consultation, which will close on May 26, aims to gather views from young people, parents, and guardians on the proposal, as well as alternative interventions.

Technology Secretary Liz Kendall said the consultation will help establish how young people can 'thrive in an age of rapid technological change.'

Alongside the consultation, pilots will be conducted to test some of the proposed interventions and provide 'real-world evidence' of their effectiveness.

Kendall emphasized the importance of a good childhood, saying, 'The path to a good life is a great childhood, one full of love, learning and play. That applies just as much to the online world as it does to the real one.'

The UK's move comes as Australia became the first country to introduce a social media ban for under-16s in December, with Spain following suit in February.

Some experts and children's charities have warned against the idea, but it has strong backing from the House of Lords and over 60 Labour MPs.

Conservative leader Kemi Badenoch and Liberal Democrat education spokeswoman Munira Wilson have also expressed support for the policy, with Wilson stating that 'there is no time to waste in protecting our children from social media giants.'
