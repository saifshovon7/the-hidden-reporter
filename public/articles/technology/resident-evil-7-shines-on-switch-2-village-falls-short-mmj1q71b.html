Resident Evil 7 Shines On Switch 2, Village Falls Short

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Digitalfoundry.net

Capcom's Resident Evil 7 and Resident Evil Village have arrived on the Nintendo Switch 2, following the impressive port of Resident Evil Requiem in February. While Resident Evil 7 lives up to expectations, its sequel falls short in terms of performance and image quality.

The presentation in Resident Evil 7 is largely identical to the PlayStation 4 version, with some minor visual tweaks. The Switch 2 version features less aggressive shadow cascades, but reduced volumetric resolution and a noticeable colour shift. Docked, the Switch 2 renders at circa-720p, upscaled to 1080p, while the PS4 runs at native 1080p with TAA. The Switch 2's use of Nvidia DLSS results in improved performance and reduced foliage flicker, but retains the over-bearing chromatic aberration of the original game.

Resident Evil 7 runs smoothly at 60fps in both docked and handheld modes, despite an internal resolution of 432p. The VRR screen helps to hide small frame rate drops beneath the target frame rate, resulting in a very smooth experience.

Resident Evil Village, on the other hand, poses more of a challenge for the Switch 2's hardware. The game's grander manors and more expansive exterior views result in lower resolutions, with 720p docked and 432p handheld. Despite this, the Switch 2 version still emerges as the clear winner in terms of image quality, although snowfall rendering and fog density are affected by the upscaling solution.

Performance in Village is less stable than RE7, with the Switch 2 running unlocked but struggling to hit the 60fps target. Exterior environments often dip below 60fps, and handheld mode experiences frequent judder in challenging scenes. The Xbox Series S versions of both games were also tested, with Resident Evil 7 showing similar performance to the Switch 2.

In conclusion, while Resident Evil 7 is a solid port, Resident Evil Village falls short in terms of performance and image quality. The Switch 2 version is still a worthwhile experience, but it's clear that the game's grander scope and more demanding visuals are pushing the hardware to its limits.
