London Offers Alternative to US for Embattled AI Firm Anthropic

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 6, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

London Mayor Sir Sadiq Khan is inviting San Francisco-based artificial intelligence (AI) developer Anthropic to expand its operations in his city, citing concerns over the company's treatment by the US government.

The invitation comes as the Pentagon has designated Anthropic a supply chain risk, a move that Anthropic plans to challenge in court.

Sir Sadiq's letter to Anthropic boss Dario Amodei was released as the company's relationship with the US government continues to deteriorate.

Anthropic's AI tools have been at the center of a dispute between the company and the US Department of Defense, with the Pentagon seeking unfettered access to the tools.

Amodei refused to give the US defense agencies access to the tools, citing concerns over the potential use of the technology for mass domestic surveillance or autonomous military targeting.

The Pentagon has said the military must be able to use technology "for all lawful purposes" and argued those feared uses would violate the law and were not planned.

US President Donald Trump has directed federal agencies to stop using technology from AI developer Anthropic, and the company's talks with the Department of Defense have been put on hold.

Sir Sadiq's letter is seen as an attempt to position London as a viable alternative for Anthropic, which has been in negotiations with the Department of Defense.

The AI developer had been in talks with the Department of Defense but on Thursday, Under Secretary of Defense Emil Michael said talks were off.

Anthropic's relationship with the US government has been under scrutiny, with the company's CEO Dario Amodei taking issue with the prospect of having Anthropic's Claude model used to conduct mass domestic surveillance or autonomous military targeting.

The Pentagon's designation of Anthropic as a supply chain risk is the first time a US company has been labelled as such, and it has raised concerns over the company's business with partners that also conduct business with the US government.

However, tech giant Microsoft has said that it would continue to embed Anthropic technology in products for its clients, with an exception carved out for the US Department.

Sir Sadiq's letter to Anthropic comes as the company faces increasing pressure from the US government, with the mayor acknowledging the "extremely concerning" behavior of the Trump administration.

The letter is seen as a bid to attract Anthropic to London and provide the company with a more welcoming environment for its operations.
