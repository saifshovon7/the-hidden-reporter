Galaxy S26 Ultra Teardown Reveals Samsung's Thermal and Battery Replacement Efforts

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Wccftech

Samsung's Galaxy S26 Ultra has been put through a teardown, offering a closer look at the company's efforts to improve battery replacements and thermal management.

The teardown revealed extensive use of graphite films and thermal paste on the chipset, storage, and DRAM, allowing for effective heat dissipation.

It was relatively easy to access the device's internal components, with Samsung's implementation of a graphite film on the wireless charging coil and thermal paste between the motherboard's back and the frame helping to reduce temperatures.

The logic board features additional graphite film, and thermal paste has been added to both the storage and DRAM, a departure from previous Galaxy S Ultra flagships.

The device's battery is also designed for easy removal, with a pull pouch and clearly labeled sides making it simple to separate.

The teardown also provided a first look at the Galaxy S26 Ultra's vapor chamber, which is unchanged from the one used in the Galaxy S25 Ultra.

Overall, the YouTuber conducting the teardown gave the Galaxy S26 Ultra a 9 out of 10 rating, deducting points for the length of repairs and replacing the display.
