2027 Chevy Bolt Returns With a Twist, Leaving Fans Both Excited and Disappointed

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

The 2027 Chevrolet Bolt has made a comeback, but it's not the same as before. After being off the market for years, the new model has been met with a mix of excitement and disappointment from fans.

Like the McRib, the 2027 Bolt has a loyal following, with many enthusiasts eagerly awaiting its return. However, the new model has undergone significant changes, with a redesigned front and rear end, tweaked suspension, and refined interior plastics.

Advertisement

Despite being the cheapest EV on the market, starting at $28,995, including the destination fee, the new Bolt has sacrificed some features, including CarPlay and Android Auto. However, it has added Spotify and Apple Music apps to ease the pain.

The new Bolt's return is largely due to an 18-month production gap at GM's factory in Fairfax, Kansas, which executives saw as an opportunity to revive the model. While it's not a direct continuation of the original, the new Bolt has enough familiar elements to please its die-hard fans.

TechCrunch had the chance to test drive the new Bolt during a recent press trip, and while it's clear that GM has made an effort to improve the model, it's also evident that some compromises have been made.

The new Bolt's prices start at $28,995, but can go up to over $40,000 for a fully loaded model. Whether this is a good deal or not depends on individual preferences and priorities.
