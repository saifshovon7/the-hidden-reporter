Sony Tests Dynamic Pricing on PlayStation Games

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: The Verge

Sony appears to be testing dynamic pricing on PlayStation games, with some users seeing different prices for the same game, according to a site called PSprices.

PSprices, which tracks prices on Sony's digital game store, noticed that some games were being offered at different prices to different users, with experiment identifiers like IPT_PILOT and IPT_OPR_TESTING in the PlayStation API.

Dynamic pricing is not new and is used in various industries, but it is often met with backlash and is not typically found in online game stores.

Advertisement

According to PSprices, Sony is running A/B testing on prices for over 150 games in 68 regions, although the US does not currently appear to be part of the experiment.

The program appears to offer discounts to select users, ranging from 5 percent to 17.5 percent, on titles like Spider-Man 2, God of War, and Red Dead Redemption 2.

However, even if Sony's version of dynamic pricing is focused on lowering costs for some users, it is likely to draw ire from customers who may not receive the same discounts.

We reached out to Sony for comment but have yet to receive a response.
