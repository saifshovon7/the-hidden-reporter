Apple Unveils iPhone 17e, New iPad Air, and MacBook Neo in Latest Deals

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: GSMArena.com

Apple has made significant announcements this week, dropping a number of major developments in the wake of the MWC. While most of the MWC's developments are unlikely to come to the US, Apple's offerings are certainly worth exploring.

The new iPhone 17e addresses several major issues of the 16e, including proper MagSafe support, improved base storage (256GB), and an anti-reflective coating on the Ceramic Shield 2. The device also features a bumped-up Apple A19 chipset, all for $600. Although the 6.1-inch 60Hz display with a notch may not be ideal, and the single 48MP camera with a small sensor could be improved, this is the cheapest new iPhone available, and Samsung and Google are likely paying attention.

Advertisement

Speaking of Samsung, there are leaks about the Galaxy A57, but it is still in the works. In the meantime, the Google Pixel 10a is available, featuring a $100 gift card and a bigger display (6.3 inches with 120Hz refresh rate) compared to the iPhone 17e. However, the Pixel 10a's protection is limited to regular Gorilla Glass 7i, and its Tensor G4 chipset is no match for the A19 chip. The Pixel 10a does match the 17e with a small 48MP sensor in its main camera, but adds a 13MP ultra-wide lens. Both phones have satellite SOS, but Apple's base storage upgrade gives the 17e an edge in pricing.

Additionally, Apple has upgraded the iPad Air to the M4 chipset, increasing the RAM to 12GB, which is 50% more than the old model. The 11-inch and 13-inch displays are still 60Hz IPS LCDs, but the device now supports Wi-Fi 7 and Bluetooth 6.0. The iPad Pros, on the other hand, moved to the latest Apple M5 chip last year, with 12GB of RAM on the 256GB and 512GB versions and 16GB on the 1TB and 2TB models. They also feature tandem OLED displays running at 120Hz.

Apple has also shaken up the MacBook market with the new MacBook Neo, which runs on an iPhone 16 Pro chipset and starts at just $600. This drops to $500 with an education discount, making it a threat to Chromebooks, Surface laptops, and even iPads. The MacBook Neo features a 13-inch IPS LCD (3:2, 60Hz), two USB-C ports (one USB 3, one USB 2), a 3.5mm headphone jack, and a 36.5Wh battery. The A18 Pro chipset is super efficient by laptop standards, promising to last 16 hours of video playback or 11 hours of web browsing. At 2.7lbs, this is on the heavier side of 13-inch MacBooks, but it's not too heavy. The 512GB model is the only one to offer Touch ID, and the 8GB of RAM is the biggest limitation.
