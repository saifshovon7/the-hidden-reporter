PopSockets Founder David Barnett on Building a Viral Business

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

David Barnett, the founder of PopSockets, has learned a great deal since launching the company over a decade ago. The former philosophy professor created one of the most popular phone accessories of all time, a device that grips to the back of a phone and can be used as a kickstand or a handle.

Barnett sat down with Equity to discuss his journey building the company from his garage, why he decided not to take on traditional venture capital funding, and some of the lessons he's learned while scaling the business.

Barnett recalled that he had no experience in manufacturing when he first started, and he also lacked experience in business, tax, accounting, and finance. He burned through a lot of money with no revenue and faced wave after wave of manufacturing defects during the early days.

Despite these challenges, Barnett managed to adapt and eventually landed a spot in a local toy store where he could observe how customers interacted with his brand. The sales were slow at first, but he made adjustments to the PopSocket, and that's when everything started to take off.

Barnett discussed the hits and misses of entering the retail market, including a dispute he had with Amazon that briefly caused him to pull his product from the website. He also spoke about adapting the product, protecting intellectual property, and knowing when it was time to step down as CEO and let someone else take the reins.

The greatest lesson Barnett has learned is that it's all about the people, he said, adding that he was looking for this trait in his successor. He believes that this is the most important skill one can have as a leader.
