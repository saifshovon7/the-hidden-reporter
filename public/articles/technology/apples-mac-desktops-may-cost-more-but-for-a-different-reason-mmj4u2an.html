Apple's Mac Desktops May Cost More, But for a Different Reason

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: 9to5Mac

Apple recently unveiled a range of new Macs with M5 chips, featuring an interesting trend: increased base storage at slightly higher prices. This change impacts the MacBook Air and MacBook Pro, with the M4 versions of the Mac mini, Mac Studio, and iMac still maintaining their original pricing.

The M5 MacBook Air now comes with 512GB of base storage, a $100 price increase from the previous 256GB offering. This is a positive change for users who previously had to pay $200 for 512GB, effectively making the MacBook Air cheaper for those who would have purchased the extra storage.

A similar price adjustment applies to the base 14-inch MacBook Pro with M5, which now includes 1TB of storage as standard, a $100 increase from the previous base model. However, users who previously purchased 1TB of storage will find it more favorable.

The M5 Pro and M5 Max MacBook Pros differ slightly, with 1TB of storage included by default but at a $200 price increase. This effectively means users are paying for the additional storage without any added value.

Apple's desktop lineup has not yet been touched, but it is likely that future models will follow a similar price shift. The Mac mini and iMac may see price adjustments due to the outdated 256GB base storage, while the Mac Studio could see a significant price increase with the next generation.

It is worth noting that Apple has not adjusted its unified memory pricing despite the ongoing RAM crisis, so this is not an immediate concern.
