AI Tech Restores Lost Voices for Those with Motor Neuron Disease

The Hidden Reporter Staff February 24, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Yvonne Johnson, who suffers from motor neuron disease, has lost much of her ability to speak. However, thanks to artificial intelligence, she is now able to communicate with her own voice.

According to a recent report, AI tech is providing people with their lost voices back. In a conversation with BBC Sounds, Yvonne shared her remarkable experience of hearing her voice again.

Advertisement

The technology is giving new hope to those affected by motor neuron disease, a condition that progressively damages the nerve cells responsible for controlling voluntary muscle movement.

Motor neuron disease can lead to a loss of speech, but AI-powered systems are helping individuals like Yvonne to regain some control over their communication.

Additionally, the report highlights other significant developments in the tech world, including the impact of AI on the price of memory chips and the growth of video gaming in Africa.
