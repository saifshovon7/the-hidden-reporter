Slate Auto Appoints New CEO Ahead of Affordable EV Launch

Slate Auto has appointed Peter Faricy as its new CEO, replacing Christine Barman, who has taken on the role of President of Vehicles. The change comes ahead of the launch of the company's affordable electric truck.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: TechCrunch

Slate Auto, the electric vehicle startup backed by Jeff Bezos, has replaced its CEO, Christine Barman, with Peter Faricy, a former Amazon Marketplace vice president, just months before the launch of its affordable electric truck.

Faricy joined Slate on Monday and will oversee the company's operations as it prepares to convert its list of refundable preorders into vehicle orders, which now stands at around 160,000 people.

Barman, who was Slate's first hire, has taken on the role of President of Vehicles and will focus on delivering the truck 'on time and on budget,' according to Slate spokesperson Jeff Jablansky.

The company, which has raised around $700 million from Bezos and other wealthy backers, initially targeted a starting price of 'under $20,000' for its electric truck before the federal EV tax credit was removed. It is now targeting the mid-$20,000 range.

Buyers will be able to customize the truck in various ways, including converting it into an SUV, for an additional cost.

Faricy brings significant experience to the role, having previously worked as an adviser at McKinsey and Bessemer Venture Partners.

Jablansky cited Faricy's experience building Amazon Marketplace as a key factor in the company's decision to appoint him as CEO.

While the change in leadership may come as a surprise, it is not unexpected given the company's ties to Amazon and the importance of Faricy's experience in the e-commerce space.
