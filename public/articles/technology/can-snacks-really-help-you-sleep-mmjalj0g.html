Can Snacks Really Help You Sleep?

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 6, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Alice Mushrooms, a mushroom-infused chocolate brand, has gained a cult following since its launch three years ago. Its original product, Nightcap, is now available in 2,000 stores across the US and boasts a list of ingredients that claim to improve sleep quality.

Developed by co-founders Charlotte Cruze and Lindsay Goodstein, Nightcap features reishi mushrooms and chamomile, known for their calming effects; plus magnesium and zinc, which are said to increase the body's natural levels of melatonin, a hormone that triggers sleepiness.

The product also includes L-theanine, an amino acid that naturally occurs in green tea, which is understood to have a relaxing effect on the body to aid sleep. According to Cruze, sleep is a complex issue, and Alice Mushrooms worked with homeopathic doctors, PhD researchers, and professional product formulators to create a product that could help with every part of the sleep cycle that could be getting disrupted.

Cruze and Goodstein's business is playing into the rising demand for products promising better sleep. A recent survey by mattress company Sleepopolis found that 47% of Americans currently take a sleep aid, with natural supplements like melatonin proving the most popular.

In the UK, pharmaceutical products remain the largest sleep aid category, but natural remedies are the fastest-growing segment. Both the US and UK ranked in the bottom 10 for countries with good 'sleep scores' in IKEA's 2025 Sleep Report.

Alice Mushrooms' Nightcap chocolate is part of a new category of products that combine the wellness claims of health supplements with the format of a treat. Eating a chocolate to help you sleep feels more like a reward, whereas taking a capsule can feel more medicinal and less enjoyable.

Other sleep-enhancing snack foods include Goodnite dark chocolate sleep bites, Lagoon's Night Bites, and Good Day Chocolate's 'Calm' range. Reishi mushrooms are also the active ingredient in a number of chocolates, gummies, and beverages, all promoting relaxation.

However, while the long-term sleep benefits of magnesium and melatonin have been scientifically proven, the documented positive sleep impacts of ingredients like chamomile appear to be short-term, with placebo effects commonly occurring.

In the UK, melatonin is available on prescription only, and synthetic versions are prescribed for short periods to help with sleep problems. Studies on reishi mushrooms have also shown mixed results, with more research needed to fully understand their effects on sleep.
