Family of Tumbler Ridge Shooting Victim Sues OpenAI

The family of Maya Gebala, a 12-year-old girl injured in a Canadian school shooting, has sued OpenAI, alleging the company failed to alert authorities to the suspect's plans.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

The family of Maya Gebala, a 12-year-old girl critically injured in a mass shooting at a Canadian school in Tumbler Ridge, has filed a lawsuit against ChatGPT-maker OpenAI.

The lawsuit alleges that OpenAI was aware the suspect, 18-year-old Jesse Van Rootselaar, had been planning an attack but failed to alert the authorities.

An initial ChatGPT account linked to Rootselaar was banned by OpenAI in June 2025, but Canadian police were not notified.

The civil lawsuit, brought by Gebala's mother Cia Edmonds, claims that Rootselaar set up an account with ChatGPT before turning 18 and that no age verification took place on the site.

The lawsuit alleges that 12 OpenAI employees flagged the suspect's posts as indicating an imminent risk of serious harm to others and recommended contacting Canadian law enforcement, but the request was reportedly 'rebuffed'.

Instead of alerting the authorities, OpenAI allegedly banned Rootselaar's account and took no further action.

The lawsuit claims that OpenAI had specific knowledge of the shooter's long-range planning of a mass casualty event but took no steps to act upon this knowledge.

As a result of the company's conduct, Gebala has suffered a 'catastrophic brain injury' after being shot three times while trying to lock a library door to keep out the shooter.

In a statement, an OpenAI spokesperson called the events an 'unspeakable tragedy' and expressed the company's commitment to working with government and law enforcement officials to prevent similar tragedies in the future.

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has pledged to strengthen protocols on notifying police over potentially hazardous conversations, according to the Wall Street Journal.
