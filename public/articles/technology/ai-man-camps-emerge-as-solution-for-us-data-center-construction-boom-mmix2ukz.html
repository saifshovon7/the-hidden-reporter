AI Man Camps Emerge as Solution for US Data Center Construction Boom

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

To address the need for hundreds or thousands of temporary workers in the construction of AI data centers, developers are increasingly turning to temporary villages known as man camps.

These camps were initially popularized as housing for men working in remote oil fields.

For instance, a Bitcoin mining facility in rural Dickens County, Texas, is being converted into a 1.6-gigawatt data center, with its workers living in gray housing units equipped with a gym, a laundromat, game rooms, and a cafeteria that offers on-demand grilled steaks.

Advertisement

A company called Target Hospitality has secured multiple contracts worth a total of $132 million to build and operate the Dickens County camp, which could eventually accommodate more than 1,000 workers.

Target Hospitality appears to view the US data center construction boom as its most lucrative growth opportunity, with chief commercial officer Troy Schrenk describing it as “the largest, most actionable pipeline I’ve ever seen.”

The company also owns the Dilley Immigration Processing Center in Texas, which houses families detained by Immigration and Customs Enforcement.

Court filings have raised allegations of substandard living conditions at the Dilley center, including the presence of worms and mold in the food and inadequate accommodations for children with allergies and special diets.
