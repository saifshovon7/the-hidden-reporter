Musk Defends Social Media Posts in Twitter Lawsuit

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 5, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Elon Musk testified in a California court that stock market investors read too much into his social media posts, which some claim were intended to manipulate markets.

The billionaire was defending himself against claims from investors who say they lost money after acting on his allegedly misleading statements.

Musk told the jury that his posts were 'extremely literal' and not intended to reduce the price he would pay for Twitter, now called X.

'I was simply speaking my mind,' Musk said when asked about the impact of his posts, including one in which he declared his takeover plans had been put on hold.

'People tend to read too much into things that I do,' he added.

The dispute marks the first lawsuit over Musk's 2022 Twitter purchase to make it to trial, with investors seeking unspecified monetary damages.

Musk has faced similar claims before, but has successfully beaten back claims of misleading Tesla investors and defamation via his social media posts.

The trial, which started on Monday in San Francisco, is expected to last three weeks.

Investors, including Brian Belgrave, claim they sold Twitter shares at a loss after believing Musk was no longer going to buy the platform due to his public posts and comments.

Musk's lawyer argued that the billionaire's social media habit is a personal freedom, while the plaintiffs' lawyer compared it to a boxing strategy to force Twitter into accepting his takeover bid.
