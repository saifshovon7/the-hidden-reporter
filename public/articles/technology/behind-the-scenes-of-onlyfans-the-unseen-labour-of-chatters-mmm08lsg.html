Behind the Scenes of OnlyFans: The Unseen Labour of 'Chatters'

A hidden workforce of 'chatters' is behind the success of some OnlyFans models, while scientists use AI to advance their research and the White House is accused of manipulating footage for propaganda purposes.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

A hidden workforce is behind the success of some OnlyFans models, who make money by chatting with paying subscribers. These individuals, known as 'chatters', pretend to be the models and engage in conversations on their behalf.

One such 'chatter' is a woman from the Philippines, who shared her experiences with the BBC. She revealed that her job involves creating fake online personas and interacting with subscribers, often for several hours a day.

The 'chatter' system has raised questions about the authenticity of online content and the exploitation of workers. Some experts argue that the practice is a form of digital sweatshop, where individuals are paid low wages to engage in labor-intensive work.

Meanwhile, scientists are using artificial intelligence to advance their research. In a separate segment, the BBC explores how AI is being used to analyze large datasets and make new discoveries.

The White House has been accused of using manipulated footage from video games to create propaganda clips. An analysis of their strategy reveals a sophisticated approach to visual storytelling.
