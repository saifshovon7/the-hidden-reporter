The Uncomfortable Truth About Hybrid Vehicles

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: The Verge

The uncomfortable truth about hybrid vehicles is that plug-in hybrid owners rarely actually plug in their vehicles, practically negating the climate advantages of the technology.

Hybrid vehicles have experienced a major surge in popularity, with sales reaching record levels in 2024 and 2025, as car companies try to make up for lost time in the electric vehicle market.

Advertisement

The first hybrid vehicle of note was the Semper Vivus, developed by Ferdinand Porsche in 1900, which used two combustion engines to power generators that fed electricity to motors inside the wheel hubs.

Car companies are now shifting their focus to hybrids, particularly plug-in hybrids, as electric vehicle growth slows down and tastes in the market fluctuate.

The Toyota RAV4, America's most popular vehicle, is now only available in hybrid trims, but cracks are starting to show in that market as well, with some automakers shifting away from plug-in hybrids without strict fuel economy standards or EV tax credits.

For example, Jeep recently axed its plug-in hybrid models, despite being among the best-selling plug-in hybrids in the country.
