ICO Writes to Meta Over Concerns with AI Smart Glasses Report

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 4, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

The UK's data watchdog, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), has written to Meta expressing concern over a report that outsourced workers were able to view sensitive content filmed by the company's AI smart glasses.

The investigation by Swedish newspapers Svenska Dagbladet and Goteborgs-Posten found that subcontracted workers in Kenya were reviewing content, including videos of people using the toilet or having sex, captured by the AI smart glasses.

Meta said that subcontracted workers might review content, including films and images, to improve the user experience, but the company took the protection of people's data very seriously and was constantly refining its efforts and tools in that area.

The ICO stated that devices processing personal data, including smart glasses, should put users in control and provide for appropriate transparency.

Meta's AI smart glasses, such as the Ray-Ban Meta glasses, use AI to help users answer questions about the world around them, and the company said that users have to activate recording manually or through a voice command.

However, sources spoke to the Swedish papers said that sometimes the filtering process failed, and people's faces could be seen.

The ICO has requested information from Meta on how it is meeting its obligations under UK data protection law.

Meta has since updated its statement, saying that users' media stays on their device unless they choose to share it, and that the company sometimes uses contractors to review data for the purpose of improving the user experience.
