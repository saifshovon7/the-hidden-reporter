Data Centre Plan Sparks Concerns in Ayrshire

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 1, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

A large data centre is planned for a site near HMP Kilmarnock in East Ayrshire, sparking concerns among locals about the impact on the wider area.

The 100-hectare site, which is currently farmland, has been earmarked by energy firm ILI Group for a technology hub.

Supporters of the plan believe it could bring new jobs and investment to the region, while ILI says the vast majority of the land would be set aside for 'biodiversity and landscaping'.

However, opponents argue that the firm has not provided concrete details about the building, which would dominate Hurlford for decades to come.

They are also concerned about the large amounts of energy and water the data centre would consume, and the potential impact on the local environment.

ILI's Rufus data centre would be powered at 540MW, requiring millions of litres of water a day, according to plans.

As the world rushes to develop data centres, concerns are growing about the knock-on effects of these facilities, especially their energy and water consumption.

ILI has secured land rights, permission to use the local electrical grid, and is currently beginning the planning process with local councils.

The company has also announced plans for a data cluster called the Stoics, spread across sites in Ayrshire, Lanarkshire and Fife.

Action to Protect Rural Scotland estimates that 17 data centre applications are currently at various stages of the planning process across Scotland.

The global data centre market is expected to spend around $3tn (£2.2tn) on facilities that support artificial intelligence between 2025 and 2029.
