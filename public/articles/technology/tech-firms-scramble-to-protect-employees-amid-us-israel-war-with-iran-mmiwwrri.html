Tech Firms Scramble to Protect Employees Amid US-Israel War with Iran

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 4, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Some of the world's largest technology companies are taking measures to protect their employees and facilities in the Middle East amid the escalating conflict between the US and Iran. Amazon, Google, Snap, and Nvidia are among the firms that have implemented emergency protocols to ensure the safety of thousands of their workers in the region.

The conflict has raised concerns about global tech shortages, but Nvidia, a leading chipmaker, says it is not experiencing any supply chain disruptions at present. Francisco Jeronimo, a specialist covering the Middle Eastern and European markets for data firm IDC, has expressed concerns about the impact of the conflict on technology companies that have invested heavily in the region.

Since the US-Israeli attacks began, Iran has launched a series of strikes on US bases and its allies across the region. Images authenticated by BBC Verify show the US consulate building in Dubai in flames after being hit by a drone on Tuesday evening. Nvidia has said it is keeping a close watch on its operational footprint but has not confirmed reports of temporarily shutting its Dubai office.

Amazon has begun an assessment of its regional sites, which includes corporate offices, fulfillment centers, delivery stations, and quick commerce outlets. The company has confirmed that it is adjusting operations in response to the evolving situation, including temporary pauses where necessary. The safety of employees and partners is Amazon's top priority.

Amazon Web Services (AWS) confirmed on Monday that drones had hit three of its facilities in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) and Bahrain. The incidents happened on Sunday morning, and AWS said at the time that objects had hit a data center in the UAE, creating sparks and fire. The company is investigating power and connectivity issues at a facility in Bahrain.

Amazon has also asked its corporate workforce in the region to work remotely, a move mirrored by social media company Snap. Snap has urged its staff to follow advice from local authorities and foreign embassies, including specific instructions for shelter-in-place orders and departure recommendations. Google has activated security and safety protocols and remains in contact with its Middle East teams, advising staff to rely on official government channels as the conflict continues.
