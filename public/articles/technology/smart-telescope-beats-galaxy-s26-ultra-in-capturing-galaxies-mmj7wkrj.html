Smart Telescope Beats Galaxy S26 Ultra in Capturing Galaxies

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Tom's Guide

When it comes to capturing faraway targets, the Galaxy S26 Ultra is not the best option. Samsung's flagship packs a 100x Space Zoom, but it still can't achieve the zooming performance of DwarfLab's Dwarf Mini smart telescope when imaging deeper space objects.

The Dwarf Mini, which has been used by the author for nearly two months, is an ultra-compact smart telescope that can be easily set up in minutes. Its mobile app makes it easy to capture stunning astrophotography shots of galaxies, nebulas, star clusters, and more, without requiring any experience.

Advertisement

The beauty of the Dwarf Mini is its small size, which is smaller than traditional smart telescopes like the Unistellar Odyssey Pro. Weighing just 1.85 pounds, it's approximately the same weight as four Galaxy S26 Ultras, making it easy to carry to remote places.

The DwarfLab app makes the process of capturing stunning images easy and dummy-proof. It communicates with the Dwarf Mini to perform calibration and tuning, and allows users to browse a native sky atlas to select what to capture.

Once the imaging process begins, the Dwarf Mini takes care of the rest, producing stunning images without the need for touchup.

The author has been impressed with the Dwarf Mini's performance, capturing some of their favorite astrophotography shots of the year. The smart telescope's compact design and ease of use make it a game-changer for anyone interested in astrophotography.
