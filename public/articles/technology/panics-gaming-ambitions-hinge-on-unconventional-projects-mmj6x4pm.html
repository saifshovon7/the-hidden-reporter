Panic's Gaming Ambitions Hinge on Unconventional Projects

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: The Verge

Panic, a publisher known for its unconventional approach to gaming, is set to release Big Walk, a co-op multiplayer game that follows the success of Untitled Goose Game.

The game, developed by House House, is an offbeat multiplayer adventure that allows players to join forces with up to 12 people simultaneously.

Panic's cofounder, Cabel Sasser, credits the company's willingness to take risks with its gaming business, saying that there are no 'hard-and-fast rules' about how it approaches the titles it works on.

For Sasser, the key to Panic's success lies in its ability to bring new and interesting ideas to the world of gaming, and to feel good about being a part of it.

Advertisement

Panic was founded nearly 30 years ago as a Mac-focused software company, but it wasn't until 2016 that it dipped its toes into gaming by publishing Campo Santo's atmospheric Firewatch.

The success of those games made a lot of Panic's current gaming efforts possible, and the company has built off of that by pushing in bold and ambitious directions, including publishing a carefully curated catalog, selling its own hardware, and running a digital games store.

Panic's video game business is now bigger than its Mac software business, according to Sasser, and the company's games are all distinct, ranging from Time Flies, a game about life as a fly, to Blippo Plus, a game that turns flipping through channels into a game.

Panic's gaming ambitions are clearly centered around creating unique and engaging experiences that cater to a wide range of players.
