Meta Urged to Boost Oversight of Fake AI Videos

Meta's Oversight Board has called on the company to enhance its oversight of fake AI content, citing concerns about the proliferation of such content on its platforms, particularly during armed conflicts.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Meta, the social media giant, has been called upon to enhance its oversight of fake content created using artificial intelligence (AI) tools on its platforms. The 21-person Oversight Board, established by Meta itself, expressed concerns that the company was not doing enough to address the proliferation of fake AI content.

The board's concerns were sparked by an AI-generated video that claimed to show extensive damage in Haifa, Israel, caused by Iranian forces. The video, which garnered almost 1 million views on Facebook, was not labeled as AI-generated or removed, despite several user complaints. It was only after the Oversight Board took up the issue that Meta responded, claiming the video did not require a label or removal because it did not directly contribute to the risk of imminent physical harm.

The Oversight Board disagreed, stating that the company's current approach to policing content was inefficient and inadequate, particularly during armed conflicts. The board called on Meta to overhaul its AI rules, warning that an increase in fake AI videos related to global military conflicts was challenging the public's ability to distinguish fabrication from fact and risking a general distrust of all information.

Meta's current methods rely largely on users to self-disclose when content is produced by an AI tool, or for someone to complain to its content moderation team. The Oversight Board recommended that the company proactively label fake AI content much more frequently, as its current methods were neither robust nor comprehensive enough to contend with the scale and velocity of AI-generated content, particularly during crises or conflicts.
