Trump Orders Government Agencies to Stop Using Anthropic AI Tools

The Hidden Reporter Staff February 28, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

US President Donald Trump has directed every federal agency to immediately stop using technology from AI developer Anthropic. The move comes after a row between the White House and the company over access to its AI tools for the US military.

Anthropic, the developer of AI tools like Claude, had refused to give the US military unfettered access to its technology. The company had expressed concerns about the potential use of its tools in 'mass surveillance' and 'fully autonomous weapons'.

US Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth had deemed Anthropic a 'supply chain risk', a label that would make it the first US company to receive such treatment. Anthropic has said it will challenge any supply chain risk designation in court.

Trump's directive came after days of back and forth between Anthropic's CEO Dario Amodei and Hegseth. The company had grown concerned in recent months about the government potentially using its AI tools.

The Department of War, as Trump has referred to the defence department, will phase out Anthropic's tools over the next six months. Anthropic has said the impact will be limited to companies that contract with the military, which may have to stop using Anthropic for work being done on behalf of the department.

Trump berated the company on his Truth Social platform, saying Anthropic 'better get their act together' and be helpful during the phase-out period, or face 'major civil and criminal consequences'.

Anthropic has been in use by the US government and military since 2024 and was the first advanced AI company to have such a relationship.
