Landmark Trial Explores Social Media Addiction's Impact on Young Users

The Hidden Reporter Staff February 27, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

A young woman, known as KGM or Kaley to protect her privacy, has testified in a landmark trial that social media, particularly Instagram and YouTube, consumed her childhood years. The trial, which is expected to last until mid-March, is a lawsuit against Meta and Google over the addictive nature of social media.

Kaley told the court in Los Angeles that she began using YouTube at the age of 6 and Instagram at 9, without encountering any barriers to prevent her from using them despite her young age. She claimed that she spent all day on Instagram, from waking up to going to sleep, leading to difficulties at school, at home, and with her mental health.

Kaley has been diagnosed with body dysmorphia, a condition where people worry excessively about their physical appearance. She also experienced feelings of anxiety and depression from the age of 9 and 10, and was subsequently diagnosed with those disorders as a teenager. Kaley began engaging in self-harm at the age of 10 and has seen a therapist since she was 13.

Meta has argued that Kaley's struggles with her mental health stemmed from problems with her family life, not her use of Instagram. The trial is expected to set a legal precedent for social media operators' responsibility towards their youngest users, potentially affecting thousands of similar lawsuits filed across the US.
