Anthropic Challenging Pentagon's Supply Chain Risk Label

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 6, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

The US Department of Defense has officially designated artificial intelligence (AI) firm Anthropic as a supply chain risk, sparking an unprecedented legal battle.

The designation, which is the first of its kind for a US company, means the government considers Anthropic's technology not secure enough for use in defense agencies.

Anthropic, which has refused to grant defense agencies unfettered access to its AI tools due to concerns over mass surveillance and autonomous weapons, plans to sue the Pentagon over the decision.

In a statement, chief executive Dario Amodei said the company does not believe the action is legally sound and sees no choice but to challenge it in court.

Amodei noted that the supply chain risk designation has a narrow scope and does not limit uses of Anthropic's AI tool Claude or business relationships with the company if they are unrelated to defense contracts.

The move comes after weeks of tense negotiations between Anthropic and the Department of Defense, which were derailed when President Donald Trump publicly berated the company on his Truth Social platform.

Trump's statement, which claimed the company was not needed or wanted, was followed by a post from Pentagon spokesperson Kash Patel stating that Anthropic would be immediately designated a supply chain risk.

Anthropic's leadership felt blindsided by the move, as they had thought a resolution was near after weeks of negotiations.

Meanwhile, tech giant Microsoft has announced that it will continue to embed Anthropic technology in products for its clients, except for the US Department of Defense.

Anthropic's chief executive has faced criticism from some in the Trump administration for not donating large sums to the former president or publicly praising him.
