London Invites Embattled AI Firm Anthropic to Expand Amid US Tensions

London Mayor Sir Sadiq Khan invites embattled AI firm Anthropic to expand in London, as the company's relationship with the US government continues to deteriorate.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 6, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

London Mayor Sir Sadiq Khan has extended an invitation to San Francisco-based artificial intelligence (AI) developer Anthropic to expand its operations in his city, amidst growing tensions with the US government.

Khan's letter to Anthropic boss Dario Amodei came as the Pentagon designated the company a supply chain risk after Amodei refused to give US defence agencies unfettered access to its AI tools.

In the letter, Khan expressed concern over the Pentagon's decision, stating it was an attempt to intimidate and punish Anthropic for refusing to remove ethical safeguards.

Anthropic has announced plans to challenge the designation in court, while the company's spokesperson said officials from the Mayor's office have been in talks with 'senior leaders' of Anthropic this week.

The invitation from Khan could position London as a viable alternative for Anthropic as the company's relationship with the US government continues to deteriorate.

The Pentagon's designation is the first time a US company has been labelled a supply chain risk, which means the government considers Anthropic not secure enough for it to use.

Meanwhile, tech giant Microsoft has stated that it will continue to embed Anthropic technology in products for its clients, with an exception carved out for the US Department of Defense.

Anthropic's AI model, Claude, has been at the centre of controversy, with concerns raised over its potential use for mass domestic surveillance or autonomous military targeting.

The company has taken issue with the prospect of its technology being used for such purposes, and has expressed concerns over the potential impact on its business with partners that also conduct business with the US government.
