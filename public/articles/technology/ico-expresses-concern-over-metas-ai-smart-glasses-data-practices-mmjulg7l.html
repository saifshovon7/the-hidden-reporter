ICO Expresses Concern Over Meta's AI Smart Glasses Data Practices

The UK's data watchdog, the ICO, has written to Meta over a 'concerning' report claiming outsourced workers were able to view sensitive content filmed by the company's AI smart glasses. Meta has responded, stating that it takes data protection seriously and is refining its efforts.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 4, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

The UK's data watchdog, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), has written to Meta over a 'concerning' report claiming outsourced workers were able to view sensitive content filmed by the company's AI smart glasses.

The report, published by Swedish newspapers Svenska Dagbladet and Goteborgs-Posten, alleged that subcontracted workers in Kenya were able to review intimate videos and images captured by Meta's AI smart glasses, including footage of users using the toilet or engaging in intimate activities.

According to the investigation, the workers, known as data annotators, were tasked with reviewing the content to improve the performance of Meta's AI. They claimed to have seen everything from living rooms to naked bodies, and sometimes even failed to blur faces in images as promised by Meta's filtering processes.

Meta has responded to the allegations, stating that it takes the protection of people's data seriously and is constantly refining its efforts and tools in this area. However, the company failed to identify which sections of its terms of service covered the review of content by human contractors.

The ICO has expressed its concern over Meta's data practices and has requested information on how the company is meeting its obligations under UK data protection law.

In a statement, the ICO said: 'Devices processing personal data, including smart glasses, should put users in control and provide for appropriate transparency. Service providers must clearly explain what data is collected and how it is used.'

Meta has since updated its statement, saying that users have control over the data they share with the company and that only shared media is reviewed by human contractors.
