Signal Warns of Scams as Hackers Target Officials and Users

Signal has warned its users to be cautious of scams after hackers targeted high-profile users, including officials and journalists, using sophisticated phishing campaigns.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Signal, a secure messaging app, has issued a warning to its users to be cautious of scams after Dutch intelligence agencies revealed that high-profile users were being targeted by hackers.

The campaign, which was identified by Dutch cybersecurity agencies, saw hackers pose as support staff to obtain sensitive information that could give them access to accounts or hijack linked devices.

According to Simone Smit, AIVD director-general, individual user accounts are being targeted, and it is not the case that Signal or WhatsApp have been compromised.

Signal reiterated this in a series of posts on X, stating that its systems remain secure and robust.

The hackers used sophisticated phishing campaigns to trick users into sharing information, including SMS codes and Signal PINs.

Phishing attacks involve criminals attempting to convince users to part with passcodes, money, or details about their identity, often by impersonating customer support agents or friends and family.

Users are advised to never share their Signal PIN code or verification codes messaged to their phone number.

WhatsApp has also given similar advice, urging users not to share six-digit codes used to secure their account.

Signal has emphasized the importance of user vigilance in combating phishing attempts, as security features are being exploited by hackers.

Cybersecurity expert Muhammad Yahya Patel noted that hackers are now focusing on human vulnerabilities, such as how users interact with apps, rather than looking for bugs in code.

Patel urged people to regularly check their devices and be cautious of convenient features that can be used by hackers to gain access to their accounts.
