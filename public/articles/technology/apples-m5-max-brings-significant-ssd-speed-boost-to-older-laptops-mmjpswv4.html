Apple's M5 Max Brings Significant SSD Speed Boost to Older Laptops

Apple's new M5 Max chip brings significant SSD speed boosts to older laptops, making it a compelling upgrade for those who purchased their laptops three years ago.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: The Verge

Apple's new high-end M5 chip delivers improvements, particularly in SSD read/write speed, making it a compelling upgrade for those who purchased their laptops three years ago.

The new M5 Max chip, found in the 16-inch MacBook Pro, boasts a 13.6GB/s read speed and a 17.8GB/s write speed, representing an 86% increase in reads and a 123% increase in writes compared to the M4 Max's 4TB drive.

Our testing also revealed that the M5 Max's new super core, which replaces the efficiency core, provides a significant boost in single-core CPU performance, with an 8-9% increase in benchmarks like Geekbench 6 and Cinebench.

In comparison to the M4 Max, the M5 Max features six super cores and 12 of the new performance cores, optimized for power-efficient, multi-threaded workloads.

While the M5 Max may not offer a significant lead over its M4 counterparts in many areas, its faster SSDs and improved CPU performance make it an attractive option for those looking to upgrade from older laptops.
