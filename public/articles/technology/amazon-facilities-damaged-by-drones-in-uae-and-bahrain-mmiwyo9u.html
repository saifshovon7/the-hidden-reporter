Amazon Facilities Damaged by Drones in UAE and Bahrain

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 3, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Amazon Web Services (AWS) has confirmed that drone strikes damaged three of its facilities in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) and Bahrain. The incidents occurred on Sunday morning, following US and Israeli strikes against Iran. The affected facilities are part of AWS's cloud computing business, which provides data storage and other IT services to millions of large companies worldwide.

The company said two UAE facilities were hit directly, while a drone strike in close proximity to a facility in Bahrain caused physical impacts to its infrastructure. The damage resulted in structural damage, disrupted power delivery, and required fire suppression activities that caused additional water damage.

AWS is working to restore services to the affected areas, but warned that it could take time due to the nature of the physical damage involved. The company recommended that customers back up their data and potentially migrate workloads to alternative AWS facilities in the rest of the world.

Experts say the incident highlights the vulnerability of key technology infrastructure during military conflicts. Vili Lehdonvirta, professor of technology policy at Aalto University, noted that with many governments and firms relying on a small number of large cloud providers, their data centres have become attractive targets to disrupt a country.

The incident also raises concerns about the security of cloud infrastructure in the Middle East, where tech firms have invested heavily in recent years to boost AI development and infrastructure.

AWS is the world's largest cloud computing provider, with its infrastructure underpinning millions of large companies' websites and platforms. The company's cloud services are used in various sectors, including finance, healthcare, and government.
