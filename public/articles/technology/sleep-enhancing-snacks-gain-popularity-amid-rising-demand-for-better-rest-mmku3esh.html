Sleep-Enhancing Snacks Gain Popularity Amid Rising Demand for Better Rest

A mushroom-infused chocolate brand has gained popularity with its sleep-enhancing product, Nightcap, which boasts a list of ingredients that claim to improve sleep quality. The demand for sleep-enhancing products is on the rise, with natural supplements like melatonin being the most popular choice.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 6, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Mushroom-infused chocolate brand Alice Mushrooms has built a cult following since its launch three years ago. The company's original product, Nightcap, is now available in 2,000 stores across the US and boasts a list of ingredients that claim to improve sleep quality.

Nightcap features reishi mushrooms and chamomile, known for their calming effects, as well as magnesium and zinc, which are said to increase the body's natural levels of melatonin, a hormone that triggers sleepiness. It also includes L-theanine, an amino acid that naturally occurs in green tea, which is understood to have a relaxing effect on the body to aid sleep.

Alice Mushrooms co-founder Charlotte Cruze notes that sleep is a challenging issue, with numerous factors contributing to sleep disruptions. To address this, Cruze and co-founder Lindsay Goodstein worked with homeopathic doctors, PhD researchers, and professional product formulators to develop a product that could help with every part of the sleep cycle.

The demand for sleep-enhancing products is on the rise, with 47% of Americans surveyed by mattress company Sleepopolis currently taking a sleep aid. Natural supplements like melatonin are the most popular choice, ahead of prescription and over-the-counter medications.

In the UK, pharmaceutical products remain the largest sleep aid category, but natural remedies are the fastest-growing segment. The US and UK both ranked in the bottom 10 for countries with good 'sleep scores' in IKEA's 2025 Sleep Report.

Sleep-enhancing snack foods like Alice Mushrooms' Nightcap chocolate are part of a new category of products that combine the wellness claims of health supplements with the format of a treat. Other options include Goodnite dark chocolate sleep bites, Lagoon's Night Bites, and Good Day Chocolate's 'Calm' range.

While some ingredients in these products have been scientifically proven to have long-term sleep benefits, others, like chamomile, have only short-term documented positive impacts, with placebo effects commonly occurring.
