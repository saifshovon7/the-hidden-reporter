Can Snacks Help You Sleep? Experts Weigh in on Trendy Products

A mushroom-infused chocolate brand is among the growing number of products claiming to improve sleep quality, but experts question the science behind their ingredients.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 6, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Alice Mushrooms, a mushroom-infused chocolate brand, has gained a cult following since its launch three years ago. Its original product, Nightcap, is now available in 2,000 stores across the US and boasts a list of ingredients that claim to improve sleep quality.

Developed by co-founders Charlotte Cruze and Lindsay Goodstein, Nightcap features reishi mushrooms and chamomile, known for their calming effects; plus magnesium and zinc, which are said to increase the body's natural levels of melatonin, a hormone that triggers sleepiness. It also includes L-theanine, an amino acid that naturally occurs in green tea, which is understood to have a relaxing effect on the body to aid sleep.

Cruze notes that sleep is a complex issue, and the company worked with homeopathic doctors, PhD researchers, and professional product formulators to create a product that could help with every part of the sleep cycle that could be getting disrupted.

Alice Mushrooms is playing into the rising demand for products promising better sleep. A survey by mattress company Sleepopolis found that 47% of Americans take a sleep aid, with natural supplements like melatonin proving the most popular.

Meanwhile, in the UK, pharmaceutical products remain the largest sleep aid category, but natural remedies are the fastest-growing segment. The US and UK both ranked in the bottom 10 for countries with good 'sleep scores' in IKEA's 2025 Sleep Report.

Sleep-enhancing snack foods like Alice Mushrooms' Nightcap chocolate are part of a new category of products that combine the wellness claims of health supplements with the format of a treat. Other options include Goodnite dark chocolate sleep bites, Lagoon's Night Bites, and Good Day Chocolate's 'Calm' range.

However, the long-term sleep benefits of some ingredients, such as magnesium and melatonin, have been scientifically proven, while others, like chamomile, have only short-term documented positive sleep impacts, with placebo effects commonly occurring.

In the UK, melatonin is available on prescription only, and synthetic versions are prescribed for short periods to help with sleep problems. Studies on reishi mushrooms are ongoing, but their effects on sleep are still unclear.
