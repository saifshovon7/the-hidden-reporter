Palmer Luckey's ModRetro Seeks Funding at $1 Billion Valuation

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

ModRetro, the vintage gaming startup founded by Palmer Luckey, is reportedly in talks to raise funding at a valuation of $1 billion, according to the Financial Times.

The company launched its first product, the Chromatic, a Game Boy-style handheld device, in 2024. Reviewers have praised the device, with The Verge's Sean Hollister describing it as "the best version of the Game Boy ever made."

Advertisement

However, some have questioned whether the device's authenticity is tainted by Luckey's reputation as the founder of defense tech startup Anduril Industries. Luckey has said that the idea for the Chromatic has been gestating for nearly 17 years and is the result of "hundreds of irrational decisions."

ModRetro is also working on other devices, including a Nintendo 64 replica. Meanwhile, Luckey's other company, Anduril, is reportedly in talks to raise a new funding round at a valuation of $60 billion.
