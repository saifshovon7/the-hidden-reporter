Employees Support Anthropic's Lawsuit Against Pentagon

Employees from OpenAI and Google have filed an amicus brief in support of Anthropic's lawsuit against the Pentagon, citing concerns over the Trump administration's decision and the technology's risks and implications.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: The Verge

Employees across OpenAI and Google have expressed support for Anthropic's lawsuit against the Pentagon, filing an amicus brief in response to the Department of Defense's designation of the company as a supply chain risk.

The amicus brief, signed by nearly 40 employees from OpenAI and Google, including Jeff Dean, Google's chief scientist and Gemini lead, details their concerns over the Trump administration's decision and the technology's risks and implications.

The move follows a dramatic few weeks for Anthropic, which stood firm on two red lines regarding acceptable use cases for military use of its technology: domestic mass surveillance and fully autonomous weapons.

Negotiations broke down between Anthropic and the Pentagon, leading to the supply chain risk designation. This designation not only prevents Anthropic from working on military contracts but also blacklists other companies if they use Anthropic products in their line of work for the Pentagon.

The amicus brief argues that the supply chain risk designation is improper retaliation that harms the public interest and that the concerns behind Anthropic's red lines are real and require a response.

The brief also makes the point that Anthropic's two red lines are worth revisiting, stating that mass domestic surveillance powered by AI poses profound risks to democratic governance, even in responsible hands, and that fully autonomous lethal weapons systems present risks that must also be addressed.

The group behind the amicus brief described themselves as engineers, researchers, and scientists who are concerned about the implications of the Pentagon's actions and the potential consequences for the public interest.
