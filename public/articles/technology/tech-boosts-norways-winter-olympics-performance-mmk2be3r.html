Tech Boosts Norway's Winter Olympics Performance

Advances in technology are helping Norway's athletes compete at the highest level, with the Olympic training centre in Oslo boasting cutting-edge equipment and facilities.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · January 31, 2026 · Source: BBC Technology

Alasdair Keane visited the Olympic training centre in Oslo to explore how advances in technology are helping Norway's athletes compete at the highest level.

The centre boasts cutting-edge equipment and facilities, including high-speed treadmills and state-of-the-art strength training machines.

Keane spoke with Norway's Olympic team coaches and athletes to learn more about the role of technology in their training.

According to the coaches, technology plays a crucial part in their athletes' preparation, allowing them to fine-tune their performance and gain a competitive edge.

The centre's advanced equipment also enables athletes to recover more efficiently, reducing the risk of injury and improving overall performance.

Keane's visit to the Olympic training centre provided a unique insight into the intersection of technology and sports, showcasing the innovative ways in which Norway's athletes are harnessing technology to achieve success.
