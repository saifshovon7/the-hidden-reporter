Performative Porsche Culture Drives Up Prices, Ruining the Vibe

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Jalopnik

A dedicated Porsche enthusiast expresses frustration with the growing trend of collectors and speculators driving up the price of vintage Porsches, making them unaffordable for enthusiasts who simply want to drive them.

The author, a long-time Porsche owner, recalls buying their first Porsche in 1983 for $1,500 and has since purchased over a dozen more. However, with the rising values, owning a Porsche has become a financial burden, with insurance costs alone exceeding $1,500 per month.

The issue is not just about the financial aspect, but also about the changing culture of Porsche ownership. The author argues that the community has become too focused on collecting and speculating, rather than enjoying the cars as they were meant to be driven.

Experts, such as James Pumphrey of Speeed on YouTube, have also weighed in on the issue, stating that Porsches are no longer cool in 2026. The author agrees, citing the proliferation of customized options and the emphasis on resale value as evidence of a culture that has lost sight of the true spirit of Porsche ownership.

Efforts to make Porsches cool again, such as a recent podcast episode by Brian Scotto, are seen as a step in the right direction. However, the author remains skeptical, believing that a return to the values of the past is needed to restore the Porsche community to its former glory.
