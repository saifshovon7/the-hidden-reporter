Google and OpenAI Employees Defend Anthropic in DOD Lawsuit

Google and OpenAI employees defend Anthropic in a lawsuit filed against the U.S. Defense Department, arguing that the government's designation of Anthropic as a supply-chain risk was improper and arbitrary.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: TechCrunch

More than 30 employees from OpenAI and Google DeepMind have filed a statement in support of Anthropic's lawsuit against the U.S. Defense Department. The lawsuit was sparked by the Pentagon's designation of Anthropic as a supply-chain risk, a label usually reserved for foreign adversaries.

The employees argue that the government's action was an 'improper and arbitrary use of power' with serious ramifications for the industry. The statement is signed by Google DeepMind chief scientist Jeff Dean and other notable figures in the field.

The Pentagon had labeled Anthropic a supply-chain risk after the AI firm refused to allow the Department of Defense to use its technology for mass surveillance of Americans or autonomously firing weapons. The DOD had argued that it should be able to use AI for any 'lawful' purpose and not be constrained by a private contractor.

The employees' statement makes the point that if the Pentagon was 'no longer satisfied with the agreed-upon terms of its contract with Anthropic,' the agency could have 'simply canceled the contract and purchased the services of another leading AI company.'

The DOD did, in fact, sign a deal with OpenAI within moments of designating Anthropic a supply-chain risk - a move that many of the ChatGPT maker's employees protested.

The employees argue that the DOD's actions will have consequences for the United States' industrial and scientific competitiveness in the field of artificial intelligence and beyond. They also argue that it will chill open deliberation in the field about the risks and benefits of today's AI systems.

The filing affirms that Anthropic's stated red lines are legitimate concerns warranting strong guardrails. Without public law to govern AI use, it argues, the contractual and technical restrictions developers impose on their systems are a critical safeguard against catastrophic misuse.
