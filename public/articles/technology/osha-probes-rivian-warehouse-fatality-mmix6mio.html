OSHA Probes Rivian Warehouse Fatality

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 6, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) has launched an investigation into the death of a worker at a Rivian warehouse in Illinois, according to the agency.

The man, identified as 61-year-old Kevin Lancaster, reportedly died from "blunt traumatic compressional injuries" after being pinned between a tractor trailer and a loading dock at the facility.

Lancaster was trapped for around 20 minutes before firefighters arrived, and he was pronounced dead at a local medical center at 2:33 p.m. local time.

Rivian has released a statement expressing sympathy for Lancaster's family and friends and cooperating with the investigation.

The incident has raised concerns about safety at Rivian's facilities, particularly in light of a 2024 Bloomberg News report detailing 16 "serious" violations at the company's Illinois manufacturing plant.

However, OSHA had previously praised Rivian for improving its safety and health team and being cooperative with the OSHA process.

Rivian assembles its flagship vehicles, including the R1 pickup truck and R1 SUV, at its 4.3-million-square-foot factory in Normal, Illinois, which is currently undergoing an expansion to accommodate the production of its next EV, the R2.
