Bipartisan Coalition Unveils Roadmap for Responsible AI Development

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: TechCrunch

A bipartisan coalition of thinkers has assembled a framework for responsible AI development, dubbed the Pro-Human Declaration. The document, signed by hundreds of experts, former officials, and public figures, aims to address the lack of coherent rules governing artificial intelligence.

The declaration was finalized before the recent Pentagon-Anthropic standoff and coincides with a period of heightened urgency. In February, Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designated Anthropic a 'supply chain risk' after the company refused to grant the Pentagon unlimited use of its technology.

Advertisement

The Pro-Human Declaration outlines five key pillars for responsible AI development: keeping humans in charge, avoiding the concentration of power, protecting the human experience, preserving individual liberty, and holding AI companies legally accountable.

Among its provisions, the declaration includes an outright prohibition on superintelligence development until there's scientific consensus it can be done safely and with genuine democratic buy-in; mandatory off-switches on powerful systems; and a ban on architectures that are capable of self-replication, autonomous self-improvement, or resistance to shutdown.

Max Tegmark, the MIT physicist and AI researcher who helped organize the effort, noted that polling has shown 95% of all Americans oppose an unregulated race to superintelligence.

The declaration's release highlights the costly congressional inaction on AI, with experts warning that the lack of regulation has led to a "first conversation we have had as a country about control over AI systems."
