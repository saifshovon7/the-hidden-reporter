TikTok Abandons End-to-End Encryption for DMs, Citing User Safety Concerns

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 4, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

TikTok has announced it will not introduce end-to-end encryption (E2EE) for direct messages (DMs), citing concerns that it would put users at risk.

The decision comes as a surprise to many, as nearly all of TikTok's rivals have embraced E2EE, claiming it prioritizes user privacy.

E2EE means only the sender and recipient of a DM can view its contents, making it the most secure form of communication available to the general public.

However, critics argue that E2EE makes it harder to stop harmful content from spreading online, as it prevents tech firms and law enforcement from viewing material sent in DMs.

TikTok has long faced accusations that its ties to the Chinese state may put users' data at risk, although the company has consistently denied these claims.

Despite this, the social media firm has now told the BBC that it believes E2EE would prevent police and safety teams from reading DMs if needed.

TikTok says its approach is a deliberate decision to prioritize user safety, especially for young people, and to set itself apart from rivals.

The company has faced scrutiny over its data protection practices, particularly its ownership by Chinese tech giant ByteDance.

Social media industry analyst Matt Navarra said TikTok's decision to abandon E2EE is a savvy one, but comes with 'pretty combustible optics', highlighting the risks of grooming and harassment in DMs.

However, Navarra also noted that the move puts TikTok out of step with global privacy expectations and may reinforce wariness about its ownership.
