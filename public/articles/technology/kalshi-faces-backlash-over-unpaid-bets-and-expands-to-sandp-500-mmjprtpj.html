Kalshi Faces Backlash Over Unpaid Bets and Expands to S&P 500

Kalshi, a prediction market platform, faces a class action lawsuit over unpaid bets and expands to S&P 500 contracts, while reportedly working to attract more women to its platform.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: The Verge

Kalshi, a prediction market platform, is facing a class action lawsuit after allegedly failing to pay out as promised for wagers regarding when Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei would leave or be removed from office.

Khamenei was killed in strikes on Iran last month, but according to the lawsuit, Kalshi didn't implement a "death carveout" until after Khamenei had died.

Advertisement

Meanwhile, Kalshi is reportedly working to attract more women to its platform in an effort to grow its user base.

According to the Wall Street Journal, the percentage of women using Kalshi has doubled over the past ten months, growing from 13% to 26% of Kalshi's total users.

Co-founder Luana Lopes Lara stated that the shift involves "catering to [women's] interests and expertise in ways that traditional financial markets haven't."

Kalshi has also launched contracts on the S&P 500, further expanding its offerings.
