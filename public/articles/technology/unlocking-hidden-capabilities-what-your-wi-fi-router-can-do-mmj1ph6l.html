Unlocking Hidden Capabilities: What Your Wi-Fi Router Can Do

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: BGR

A surprising number of people have no idea that their wireless router, which serves as a gateway to the internet through a modem, also provides support for a variety of other tasks.

For example, did you know that you can take an older router and install custom firmware to turn it into an extender or repeater, allowing you to extend the wireless range of your network?

But there's a lot more that your home router can do that you probably don't know about.

From adding and sharing network drives to setting up shared printers, your router can do a lot more than just provide internet access.

Advertisement

Most routers support features like shared drives and printers, allowing you to access and share files and print documents from any device connected to your network.

Some routers even include a built-in USB port or network storage, making it easy to set up shared devices and access them from anywhere.

So, what are some of the cool things your Wi-Fi router can do?

1. Add and share a network drive: Many routers support shared drives, allowing you to set up devices like external or accessible drives to be shared across all devices on your network.

2. Add and share a network printer: Most routers support shared printers, making it easy to print from any device connected to your network.

To access these features, simply log in to the admin panel for your router and look for options like 'Shared Drive' or 'File Sharing.'
