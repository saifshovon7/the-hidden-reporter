Anthropic Refuses Pentagon Request to Drop AI Safeguards

The Hidden Reporter Staff February 27, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

Anthropic, a leading artificial intelligence (AI) firm, has refused to back down in a dispute with the US Department of Defense (DoD) over the use of its AI technology. The company's CEO, Dario Amodei, stated that Anthropic would rather not work with the Pentagon than agree to uses of its tech that may undermine democratic values.

The controversy began after a meeting between Amodei and US Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth, during which Hegseth demanded that Anthropic accept "any lawful use" of its tools. The Pentagon threatened to remove Anthropic from its supply chain if the company did not comply.

Amodei stated that Anthropic cannot in good conscience agree to the Pentagon's request, citing concerns over the potential use of its AI tools for mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons. The company believes that such use cases should not be included in its contracts with the DoD.

The DoD has argued that the uses of AI that Anthropic is fearful of are already barred by law and Pentagon policies. However, Anthropic's CEO remains unconvinced, stating that the company will not compromise on its values.

In a statement, an Anthropic spokeswoman emphasized that the company received updated wording from the DoD for its contract, but it represented "virtually no progress" in preventing the use of its AI tools for mass surveillance or fully autonomous weapons.

The dispute has sparked a heated debate, with US Undersecretary for Defense Emil Michael personally attacking Amodei on social media. Michael stated that Amodei wants to control the US military and put the nation's safety at risk.

The Pentagon has also threatened to invoke the Defense Production Act, which would allow the government to deem Anthropic's product essential to national defense and require the company to meet defense needs.

Anthropic's CEO remains firm in his stance, stating that the company will work to enable a smooth transition to another provider if the DoD chooses to offboard the company.
