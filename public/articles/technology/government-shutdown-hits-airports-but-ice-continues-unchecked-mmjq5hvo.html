Government Shutdown Hits Airports, but ICE Continues Unchecked

A partial government shutdown is causing staffing shortages at airports, but federal agents continue to carry out anti-immigrant operations without interruption.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: The Verge

Chaos reigned at airports across the country last weekend, with thousands of travelers reportedly waiting in hours-long security lines thanks to staffing shortages. The Transportation Security Administration (TSA) and Coast Guard workers have turned to food banks for assistance after weeks without pay.

However, amid a partial government shutdown aimed at curtailing the Department of Homeland Security's mass arrests and deportations, federal agents have continued their anti-immigrant crackdown unabated.

The shutdown has left DHS without funding for four weeks in a standoff over immigration enforcement. Congressional Democrats say the lapse will continue until the White House agrees to a number of changes at ICE and CBP.

Despite the shutdown, Customs and Border Protection has continued scouting additional locations for its "smart wall" along the US-Mexico border, and briefly considered building a barrier through Big Bend National Park in Texas.

ICE has continued arresting immigrants, including a Nashville-based journalist who frequently reports on the agency, who has a pending asylum claim, and detaining them in substandard facilities.

Most DHS employees, including TSA officers and CBP agents, are currently going without pay, though they will receive backpay when funding resumes.
