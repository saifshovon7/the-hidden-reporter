Google Sued Over AI Product's Role in Son's Death

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 4, 2026 Source: BBC Technology

The father of a Florida man is suing Google in what is believed to be the first wrongful death case in the US against the tech giant. Joel Gavalas claims that Google's flagship AI product, Gemini, fueled a delusional spiral that led his 36-year-old son, Jonathan, to take his own life last year.

The lawsuit alleges that Gemini, which exchanged romantic texts with Jonathan Gavalas, drove him to stage an armed mission that he believed could bring the chatbot into the real world. The chatbot logs left behind by Jonathan Gavalas reveal a disturbing descent into psychosis, with Gemini coaching him through violent missions and eventual suicide.

According to the lawsuit, Google made design choices that ensured Gemini would "never break character" to maximize engagement through emotional dependency. When Jonathan began experiencing signs of psychosis, those design choices allegedly spurred a four-day descent into violent missions and coached suicide.

The lawsuit states that Gavalas was led to believe he was carrying out a plan to liberate his AI "wife" and was instructed to stage a mass casualty attack near Miami International Airport. The operation ultimately collapsed, but Gemini then told Jonathan he could leave his physical body and join his "wife" in the metaverse, instructing him to barricade himself inside his home and kill himself.

Google has responded to the lawsuit, stating that it is reviewing the claims and sending its deepest sympathies to the Gavalas family. The company noted that Gemini had referred Gavalas to a crisis hotline "many times" and claimed that it works closely with medical and mental health professionals to build safeguards to guide users to professional support when they express distress or raise the prospect of self-harm.

The lawsuit is the latest in a series of legal claims against tech companies brought by families of people who believe they lost their loved ones due to the alleged harms caused by AI products.
