Electric Air Taxis Get Green Light in 26 US States

The Federal Aviation Administration has approved eight pilot programs for electric aircraft testing in 26 US states, aiming to ensure US companies lead the development of next-generation aircraft.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: TechCrunch

The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has approved eight pilot programs for electric aircraft testing in 26 states across the US. The three-year program aims to ensure US companies lead the development of next-generation aircraft for personal travel, regional transportation, cargo logistics, and emergency medicine.

Advertisement

The pilot program, known as the Advanced Air Mobility and Electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing Integration Pilot Program, was announced last year through an executive order by President Donald Trump. The program will allow companies like Archer Aviation, Beta Technologies, Joby Aviation, and Wisk to test their electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, even though they have not received full regulatory certification.

This accelerated timeline could bolster the efforts of eVTOL companies, such as Archer, Beta, and Joby, which have gone public in recent years. Beta Technologies founder and CEO Kyle Clark said being selected for the program will allow the company to start aircraft operations one year earlier than anticipated.

Archer Aviation compared the eVTOL program to robotaxi testing and said it will help build trust and establish a playbook for safely scaling electric air taxis. The company, which is developing a four-passenger piloted eVTOL called Midnight, said it will also help it prepare for air taxi operations in Los Angeles for the 2028 Olympic Games.
