Bluesky CEO Jay Graber Steps Down, Toni Schneider Takes Over

Bluesky CEO Jay Graber steps down, transitioning to chief innovation officer, and Toni Schneider takes over as interim CEO, amid the company's growth and challenges with moderation and age-assurance laws.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: TechCrunch

Bluesky CEO Jay Graber is stepping down from her position and transitioning to a new role as chief innovation officer, the company announced Monday. Graber, who has helped grow the X and Threads competitor to 43 million users and furthered the development of its underlying AT Protocol, will be replaced by Toni Schneider as interim CEO.

Schneider, the former CEO of Automattic and a partner at True Ventures, will take over as interim CEO. Both Automattic and True Ventures are investors in Bluesky. Graber explained in a blog post announcing the changes that, as a more mature company, Bluesky needs a 'seasoned operator focused on scaling and execution.'

Graber said she feels better suited to building Bluesky's technology itself, adding that she is most energized by exploring new ideas, bringing a vision to life, and helping people discover their strengths. The company said its board will search for a permanent chief executive.

Schneider's experience with Automattic, which commercialized WordPress's open source technology through WordPress.com, positions him to understand the challenges that come with balancing open source tech with the needs of a company to generate a profit.

Under Graber, Bluesky has seen remarkable growth at times, particularly after Elon Musk's acquisition of Twitter, now X, led to a number of changes to the platform's product and community. However, the company has also struggled with moderation issues as it scaled.

Bluesky has now faced a new challenge: complying with a growing number of age-assurance laws around social media. The company has begun age verification in states like Ohio, South Dakota, and Wyoming, following a law in Mississippi that led to Bluesky blocking the state entirely.

As Schneider takes over, the company is looking to continue its growth and navigate the challenges of compliance with age-assurance laws.
