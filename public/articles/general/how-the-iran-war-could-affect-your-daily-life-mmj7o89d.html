How the Iran War Could Affect Your Daily Life

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

The eruption of the US-Israel war with Iran has brought with it consequences that extend far beyond the Middle East. The region plays a key role in global energy supplies and shipping routes, prompting concerns that everything from heating bills to supermarket shopping baskets could become more expensive.

Here are six ways the conflict might affect your day-to-day life.

1. Petrol and diesel prices are set to increase as production and transport of oil and gas across the region has slowed or stopped. In the UK, petrol costs an average of 137.51p per litre, while diesel costs an average of 150.97p per litre, according to the RAC. In the US, average petrol prices have risen to $3.90 per gallon, while diesel prices have risen to $3.90 per gallon.

2. UK gas prices have doubled since the beginning of the conflict, with the benchmark UK price briefly hitting 171p a therm. Although the current crisis is not as severe as the 2022 Russia-Ukraine invasion, it could still have a significant impact on energy bills.

3. The conflict could lead to higher shipping prices, which could hit consumers later. Traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has almost completely stopped, with about 200 tankers effectively stranded. Insurance premiums for vessels considered American, British, or Israeli have risen significantly due to the perceived higher risk of attack.

4. Food prices could rise as a result of increased shipping costs and disruptions to global supply chains. Supermarket shopping baskets may become more expensive, with the conflict affecting the prices of imported goods.

5. The conflict could lead to higher inflation, as the cost of living increases due to higher energy and food prices. This could have a significant impact on household budgets and the overall economy.

6. The conflict could also lead to a shortage of certain goods, particularly those that rely on imports from the region. This could lead to shortages and rationing, further exacerbating the economic impact of the conflict.

The full extent of the impact of the Iran war on daily life is still unclear, but these consequences are likely to be felt in the coming weeks and months.
