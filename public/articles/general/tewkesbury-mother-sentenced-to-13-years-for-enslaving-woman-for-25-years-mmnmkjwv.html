Tewkesbury Mother Sentenced to 13 Years for Enslaving Woman for 25 Years

A mother-of-ten has been sentenced to 13 years in prison for enslaving a vulnerable woman for 25 years in Tewkesbury, Gloucestershire. The victim suffered physical and emotional abuse, including being squirted with washing-up liquid and being forced to live off scraps.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 12, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A 56-year-old mother-of-ten has been sentenced to 13 years in prison for enslaving a vulnerable woman for 25 years in Tewkesbury, Gloucestershire.

Mandy Wixon, 56, was found guilty of false imprisonment, forcing the woman to clean her squalid home, and other charges related to modern slavery.

The victim, who has learning difficulties and is now in her mid-40s, suffered physical and emotional abuse, including being squirted with washing-up liquid, having bleach splashed on her face, and being forced to live off scraps.

Passing sentence at Gloucester Crown Court, Judge Ian Lawrie KC said Wixon had 'cruelly and persistently held captive' the woman, causing her 'enduring persistent trauma'.

The victim, who wishes to remain anonymous, said in a statement: 'For 25 years, I lived in fear, control and abuse. I was treated as though my life, my freedom and my voice did not matter.'

Wixon was also found guilty of requiring a person to perform forced or compulsory labour and assault occasioning actual bodily harm. She was acquitted of another assault charge.

The court heard that the victim was born into a troubled family and was passed into Wixon's care in 1996. She was found by police in 2021 after one of Wixon's sons raised concerns for her welfare.

The victim suffered physical abuse, including having her teeth knocked out with a broom handle, and was forced to live in squalid conditions. She was also malnourished and had untreated dental infections.

Wixon claimed benefits on the victim's behalf for 25 years, earning over £100,000.
