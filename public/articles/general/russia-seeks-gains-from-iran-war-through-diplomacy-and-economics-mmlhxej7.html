Russia Seeks Gains from Iran War Through Diplomacy and Economics

Russian President Vladimir Putin is using the Iran conflict to boost his country's profile in the Gulf and Middle East, while also seeking economic benefits from the surge in global oil prices.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Russian President Vladimir Putin has been engaging in diplomatic efforts to resolve the Iran conflict, styling himself as an international peacemaker. However, this move may be a strategic ploy to gain economic benefits and boost Russia's profile in the Gulf and Middle East.

Putin has maintained a "Comprehensive Strategic Partnership" with Iran, which has been reinforced by his recent telephone conversations with Iranian President Ebrahim Raisi. This partnership, however, falls short of a mutual defense treaty. Instead, Moscow has offered to mediate in the conflict, potentially providing an opportunity for Russia to deepen its relationship with the US and Washington.

Despite Putin's calls for "de-escalation" in Iran, the conflict offers other benefits for Russia. The recent surge in global oil prices has provided a much-needed boost to Russian government revenues, which will help fund its ongoing war in Ukraine. Russia's federal budget is based on oil exports at $59 a barrel, and the current high oil prices will enable the country to continue its military operations.

US President Donald Trump has suggested that the US may waive oil-related sanctions on some countries to ease the shortage caused by the Iran war. If this happens, Russia could receive significant financial benefits, further exacerbating the situation for Ukraine. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has urged Trump against easing sanctions, warning that it would be a "serious blow" for Kyiv.

The Kremlin's motives behind its diplomatic efforts in Iran are multifaceted, and its true intentions remain unclear. While Putin may be genuinely seeking a peaceful resolution, his actions also seem to be driven by a desire to gain economic and strategic advantages.
