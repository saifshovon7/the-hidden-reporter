Two Deaths Linked to Weight-Loss Jabs in Northern Ireland

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

The Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) has reported two deaths potentially linked to weight-loss injections in Northern Ireland. The cases are among over 500 suspected adverse drug reaction reports submitted from the region over the last two years related to GLP-1 medications, which are prescribed under names such as Wegovy and Mounjaro.

The reports were made to the MHRA, which noted that a report of a suspected reaction "does not necessarily mean it has been caused by the medicine, only that the reporter had a suspicion it may have." The agency added that underlying or concurrent illnesses may be responsible, or the events could be coincidental.

The data shows that the two deaths were of a man and a woman, one who was in their 40s and the other in their 60s. The cases were reported to the MHRA in response to a Freedom of Information request, which also revealed that six reported cases of acute pancreatitis occurred over the two-year period - three suspected to have been a reaction to Mounjaro, one to Ozempic, and two to unspecified brands.

The MHRA issued a warning about the risk of acute pancreatitis in January, which is often linked to gallstones, a common side effect of GLP-1s. About 1.6 million adults in England, Scotland, and Wales are estimated to have used GLP-1s for weight loss between early 2024 and early 2025, according to recently published research by University College London.

Most of the reports on GLP-1s from Northern Ireland over the last two years – 458 – were registered in 2025, compared to 53 in 2024. As well as the two fatal outcomes reported, there were 242 serious reports and 267 categorised as non-serious. One of the serious reports relates to a child aged nine or under.
