Glasgow Business Owners Reel from Devastating Fire

A devastating fire in Glasgow destroyed dozens of retail units, leaving business owners heartbroken and without a place to work. Donations have been raised to help the affected businesses, but a clear recovery plan remains uncertain.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

More than 250 firefighters were called to tackle a massive fire in a Glasgow building that housed dozens of retail units, leaving business owners heartbroken and without a place to work.

The fire started in a vape shop on Sunday and quickly spread to the entire building, a B-listed Victorian property on Union Corner next to Central Station.

At least 20 businesses, including Cece's Salon and Tafftastic Nails, were destroyed in the blaze, with studios and expensive equipment lost in the fire.

Donations have poured in to help the affected businesses, with over £20,000 raised so far.

First Minister John Swinney visited the scene on Monday and pledged financial support from the Scottish government to help the council with recovery efforts.

However, business owners remain uncertain about their future, with no clear recovery plan in place.

Passer-by Courtney MacKinnon, who owned Cece's Salon, described the scene as "shocking" and said it felt like "watching a film." Emma Taff, owner of Tafftastic Nails, added that the experience was "like losing a little bit of myself."

The Scottish Fire and Rescue Service (SFRS) reported no casualties from the incident, but rail operators warned of days of disruption to travel due to the closure of Glasgow Central Station.
