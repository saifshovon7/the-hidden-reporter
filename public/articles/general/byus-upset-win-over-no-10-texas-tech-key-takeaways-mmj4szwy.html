BYU's Upset Win Over No. 10 Texas Tech: Key Takeaways

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Deseret News

BYU's men's basketball team secured an upset win over No. 10 Texas Tech in their regular season finale, marking a crucial victory as they head into the Big 12 tournament.

The Cougars, who trailed by eight points at halftime, staged a remarkable comeback in the second half, outscoring the Red Raiders 44-30. BYU's defense played a pivotal role in their success, limiting Texas Tech to 38% total shooting and 4 of 16 from behind the arc.

BYU's rebounding was a significant factor in their win, with the Cougars outrebounding the Red Raiders 41-34 and securing 20 offensive rebounds that led to 20 second-chance points.

The Cougars' strength down low was evident, as they scored 48 paint points compared to Texas Tech's 14.

Rob Wright III led the way with 27 points, while AJ Dybantsa added 21 points and four assists. Kennard Davis Jr. scored 16 points, including the game-winning triple in the second half's final 100 seconds.

With this win, BYU now heads to the Big 12 tournament in Kansas City with momentum and a chance to improve their NCAA tournament seeding.

The season has been filled with adversity for the Cougars, who have experienced injuries, losses, and a drop in the AP top 25 rankings. However, they now find themselves at the start of a new season, controlling their own destiny to create a successful March.

BYU's victory over No. 10 Texas Tech serves as a testament to their resilience and determination, as they look to build on this success in the postseason.
