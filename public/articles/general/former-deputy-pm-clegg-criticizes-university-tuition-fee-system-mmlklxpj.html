Former Deputy PM Clegg Criticizes University Tuition Fee System

Former Deputy PM Sir Nick Clegg has criticized the university tuition fee system, describing it as "deeply unfair" due to the repeated freezing of graduate repayment thresholds.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 11, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Former Liberal Democrat leader Sir Nick Clegg has described the current university tuition fee system as a "mess".

Speaking to the BBC, Sir Nick acknowledged his role in the coalition government that introduced higher tuition fees in 2012, but denied responsibility for changes made afterwards, including the repeated freezing of the graduate repayment threshold.

He criticized the decision to freeze the repayment thresholds, which he said contributed to a system that was now "deeply unfair".

Under the current system, student loans issued between 2012 and 2023 have been criticized for high interest rates.

The repayment thresholds for these loans will be frozen at £29,385 between 2027 and 2030, meaning more graduates will start repaying their loans sooner after starting work.

National Union of Students President Amira Campbell described the move as "breaking a contract" and creating "a massive issue of trust".

Sir Nick also criticized the decision to remove maintenance grants for students from low-income families and called for their reintroduction.

He suggested that an independent institution could oversee the terms of student loans to ensure fairness and restore trust.

The government has promised to reintroduce maintenance grants for some students and has defended the current system as "protecting lower-earning graduates".
