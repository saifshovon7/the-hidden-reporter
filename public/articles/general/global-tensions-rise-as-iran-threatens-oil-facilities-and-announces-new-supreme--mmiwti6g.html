Global Tensions Rise as Iran Threatens Oil Facilities and Announces New Supreme Leader

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

The front page of the Times features a photograph of Iran's new Supreme Leader, with speculation surrounding the health and protection of Mojtaba Khamenei, believed to be injured due to the ongoing war.

The Guardian reports that Iran has threatened to attack oil facilities in neighboring countries, escalating fears of significant disruption to the world economy and surging oil prices.

Advertisement

Wall Street analysts at Goldman Sachs have warned that oil could hit around $150 per barrel if the crisis hasn't eased by the end of the month, potentially leading to a £100 mark for filling a family car with petrol.

The Daily Mail and the Express focus on the special relationship between Britain and the US, with the Express criticizing the prime minister's response to the crisis and the Mail reporting on crisis talks between President Trump and Sir Keir Starmer.

Meanwhile, a study by the Times suggests that having a "hassler" in one's life can shorten lifespan due to chronic stress, with each additional "hassler" increasing the rate of ageing.
