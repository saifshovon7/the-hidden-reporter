Glasgow Vape Shop Fire: Eyewitness Describes Inferno

A passer-by tried to tackle a fire at a vape shop in Glasgow but had to abandon the attempt after a series of explosions. The fire spread throughout the 19th Century building and caused its partial collapse.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

An eyewitness has described the moment he tried to tackle a fire at a vape shop next to Glasgow Central station, but had to abandon the attempt after a series of explosions.

The fire at the shop in Union Street broke out at about 15:45 on Sunday, and later the entire building was engulfed in an inferno that caused its partial collapse.

Lamin Kongira, who witnessed the incident, told BBC Scotland News that he had been walking past when a shopkeeper ran out saying "fire, fire". He rushed into the shop and saw a small fire under the counter.

Lamin said there was an adapter charging system - full of chargers - and that's where the fire was. He then dashed next door to the Subway sandwich shop to get help and came back with a fire extinguisher to try and fight the fire.

However, two explosions in the vape shop meant that he had to turn around and shout at passers-by to "get back". Lamin said he thought it was the second explosion that spread the fire to other parts of the building.

Video posted on X shows Lamin trying to keep people away from the shop as smoke billows from its doorway. He runs back to the shop to grab another man who tries to tackle the fire with a fire extinguisher.

After firefighters turned up and started battling the blaze, Lamin said he "had to stand and watch" as the fire consumed the building. The flames spread throughout the 19th Century building and its famous dome, and parts of the building were seen to collapse.

Glasgow Central Station has been closed to all trains since Sunday afternoon and will remain shut during Monday at least. Many businesses have been destroyed in the fire, and nearby hotels were evacuated and roads were sealed off.

On Monday morning, fire crews used aerial platforms to hose water on to what remained of the building, as smoke hung in the air. Only the facade of the building at the corner of Gordon Street and Union Street is left standing.
