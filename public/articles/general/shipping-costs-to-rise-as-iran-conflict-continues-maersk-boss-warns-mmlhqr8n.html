Shipping Costs to Rise as Iran Conflict Continues, Maersk Boss Warns

The cost of shipping will rise due to the ongoing conflict in Iran, with the boss of Maersk warning that traditional contracting mechanisms will pass on fuel price fluctuations to customers.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 11, 2026 · Source: BBC News

The cost of shipping will be passed on to consumers due to the ongoing conflict in Iran, according to the boss of the world's second-largest shipping company, Maersk.

Vincent Clerc, CEO of Maersk, told the BBC in an exclusive interview that traditional contracting mechanisms will pass on fuel price fluctuations to customers, resulting in higher shipping costs.

The conflict in Iran has brought two vital shipping routes to a near standstill, causing widespread disruption to the global economy.

Clerc urged the US, Israel, and Iran to come to a deal to restore global trade routes in the Middle East, saying it would be a better option than escorts from Western navies to reopen the waterways.

He expressed concerns about the safety of crews and assets, citing the ongoing threat of drone strikes and the lack of a truce between the two sides.

At least seven seafarers have been killed in the Strait of Hormuz since the conflict began, with several others injured, according to the UN's International Maritime Organization.

The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed due to Iran's threats to target shipping, with around a fifth of global oil supplies traveling through the waterway before the conflict.

Clerc believes that restoring freedom of navigation and peaceful navigation is essential for the global economy and the safety of seafarers.
