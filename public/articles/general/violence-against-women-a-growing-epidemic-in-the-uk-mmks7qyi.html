Violence Against Women: A Growing Epidemic in the UK

A recent investigation has found that at least 90 women or girls were killed by men in the UK in 2025, with 24 resulting in convictions for murder and six for manslaughter.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A recent investigation has revealed a disturbing trend of violence against women and girls in the UK. The study, which analyzed news reports from across the country, found that in 2025, at least 90 women or girls were killed by men, with 24 resulting in convictions for murder and six for manslaughter.

The investigation, which was conducted by tracking news reports and cross-checking information with UK police forces, the Crown Prosecution Service, and other authorities, also found that four people had pleaded guilty to manslaughter but still face murder charges. There were 10 more incidents where a male suspect also died, and in 15 cases, males have been arrested on suspicion of murder or manslaughter but no charges have been brought.

The investigation's findings come as the UK government has pledged to halve violence against women and girls within a decade. While the exact number of incidents is difficult to determine, charities have long said that, on average, a woman is killed by a man every three days.

According to the Office for National Statistics, in the past five years, the highest number of female homicide victims in England and Wales was 128 in 2021/22, while the lowest was 104 in the year up to March 2025. In comparison, the number of male homicide victims was 254 in the same year, with 230 of those cases involving a male principal suspect.

The investigation's findings have been met with concern from ministers, who have described the situation as a "national emergency." The study highlights the need for greater awareness and action to address the issue of violence against women and girls in the UK.
