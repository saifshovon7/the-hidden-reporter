US Missile Strike on Iranian Military Base Near School Confirmed by Video Analysis

A US Tomahawk missile hit a military base near a primary school in southern Iran, killing 168 people, according to expert video analysis. The US and Israel have denied responsibility for the attack.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A US Tomahawk missile hit a military base near a primary school in southern Iran, where 168 people, including around 110 children, were killed, according to expert video analysis.

A video published by Iran's semi-official Mehr news agency shows a missile moments before it struck an Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) base next to the Shajareh Tayebeh primary school in Minab.

BBC Verify has confirmed the video as authentic and has previously established through satellite imagery, verified videos and expert analysis that the area near the school was hit by a series of strikes.

Experts who have seen the latest video told BBC Verify that the presence of a Tomahawk missile, along with evidence the area was hit with multiple strikes, indicates this was a US operation.

Neither Israel nor Iran is known to possess Tomahawks, experts said. It would also make the scenario of a single Iranian missile hitting the site at the same time and causing such a high reported death toll highly improbable, an expert told BBC Verify.

On Saturday, US President Donald Trump said Iran was to blame for the strike on the school, stating that Iran was "very inaccurate" with their munitions.

A preliminary assessment of the incident by the US suggests it was "likely" to have been responsible for the deadly attack but did not intentionally target the school and may have hit it in error.

An Israeli government source told CBS News that Israel was not behind the attack and its military was not operating near the school.

Iran has blamed the US and Israel for the attack. Neither the US nor Israel has publicly accepted or denied responsibility.

The BBC has asked the US government to comment on the experts' assessment of the new video.

BBC Verify's analysis of the video suggests a medical clinic - which Iranian media says belonged to the IRGC navy - at the base was likely hit by the Tomahawk missile seen in the footage.

The clinic is approximately 200m from the school. The footage was first analysed by online investigation group Bellingcat.
