US-Israeli Conflict in Iran: Impact on Oil Prices

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

As turmoil continues in the Middle East, the price of crude oil rose to more than $100 a barrel on Monday. The conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran is pushing up prices, and experts are warning of potential consequences for global markets.

The BBC's Nick Marsh examines the reasons behind the surge in oil prices and what it could mean for consumers. 'We got home': Passengers on Dubai-Dublin flight celebrate return after airspace reopening.

US Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth said the US military has sunk 'an Iranian warship' in the Indian Ocean with a torpedo. The Pentagon released video footage of the strike, which has raised concerns about the escalating conflict.

Ros Atkins, the BBC's Analysis Editor, charts how the Trump administration's messaging on war has shifted in his second term. The mixed messaging has left many questions unanswered, including the reported bombing of a school in Iran.

Footage verified by the BBC and news agencies shows large explosions in Iran following US and Israel strikes. Fire broke out near the US consulate in Dubai after a drone strike, and the US president criticized the Spanish government for blocking the use of its bases during the attack.

The conflict has widened in the region, with strikes being traded between opposing sides. US Secretary of State Marco Rubio confirmed a drone struck a parking lot adjacent to the US consulate building in Dubai.

President Donald Trump criticized Prime Minister Keir Starmer and the UK's initial response to US-Israeli strikes on Iran. The BBC questioned Rubio over the reported strike on an Iranian school, and he said the US 'would not deliberately target a school'.

Smoke was seen rising from the US-run naval base in Bahrain, highlighting gaps in air defences, which could worry Washington and its allies in the region. US Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth offered few details about the operation, leaving many questions unanswered.
