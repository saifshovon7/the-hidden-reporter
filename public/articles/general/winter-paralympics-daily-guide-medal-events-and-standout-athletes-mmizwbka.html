Winter Paralympics Daily Guide: Medal Events and Standout Athletes

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 2, 2026 Source: BBC News

The Winter Paralympics will feature almost 660 athletes from 50 countries competing for 79 medals at Milan-Cortina. The games will include six sports: Para-Alpine skiing, Para-biathlon, Para-cross-country skiing, Para-ice hockey, Para-snowboard, and wheelchair curling.

The Great Britain team features 25 athletes, including seasoned campaigners and newcomers. The action started on March 4 with wheelchair curling, while the opening ceremony took place in Verona on March 6.

Here's a daily guide to the Winter Paralympics, including medal events and standout athletes.

**Day 1: March 4**

Wheelchair curling action kicked off the Winter Paralympics, with Great Britain's mixed doubles team competing against hosts Italy.

**Day 2-3: March 5-6**

Medal events in Para-Alpine skiing include the women's Super-G visually-impaired, standing, and sitting, as well as the men's Super-G visually-impaired, standing, and sitting.

Scottish skier Neil Simpson defends his men's Super-G visually impaired title, after becoming the first British man to win a Winter Games skiing gold medal in 2022. Simpson's brother and guide Andrew created history with him four years ago.

Austrian Johannes Aigner and Italian Giacomo Bertagnolli are top contenders for the men's Super-G visually-impaired title, having won silver and bronze in Beijing respectively.

British athletes Fred Warburton and James Hannan make their Games debut in the same event.

**Women's VI Event**

Austrian pair Veronika Aigner and Elina Stary are favored to win the women's visually-impaired event, with questions over the fitness of British athlete Menna Fitzpatrick.

**Men's Sitting Event**

Norway's Jesper Pedersen defends his title, but Dutch star Jeroen Krampschreur and Canada's Kurt Oatway are strong contenders.

**Women's Seated Event**

Japan's Momoka Muraoka aims to retain her title, but faces stiff competition from Germany's Anna-Lena Forster, Spain's Audrey Pascual Seco, and Dutch athlete Barbara van Bergen.

**Wheelchair Curling**

The GB mixed doubles team concludes their round-robin campaign against Italy, while the mixed team faces Korea and Sweden in a double-header.

**Para-Ice Hockey**

The US continues against Germany, while Canada takes on Japan.

Great Britain has competed in every Winter Paralympics since 1976, but had to wait until Sochi 2014 for a first gold medal.
