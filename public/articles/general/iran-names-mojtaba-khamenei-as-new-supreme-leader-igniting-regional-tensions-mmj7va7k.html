Iran Names Mojtaba Khamenei as New Supreme Leader, Igniting Regional Tensions

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: CNBC

Iran named Mojtaba Khamenei its new Supreme Leader on Sunday, according to reports from the Associated Press and Reuters, citing Iranian state media.

Khamenei is the son of the late Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who ruled Iran since 1989 until his death.

The appointment ensures that hardliners remain in control of Iran's government, prompting concerns from the United States and Israel.

Advertisement

US President Donald Trump had previously stated that the US would have a role in approving Iran's next leader, and the Israel Defense Forces warned that it would target anyone involved in the successor selection process.

Iran's neighbors in the Gulf reported more damage to infrastructure over the weekend as Iran continues to attack regional allies in retaliation for ongoing strikes by US and Israeli forces.

The United Arab Emirates said it was dealing with incoming missile and drone threats from Iran, with its air defenses intercepting ballistic missiles and fighter jets tackling drones and loitering munitions.

A high-rise building in Dubai's Marina area was hit by falling debris, and a Pakistani driver was killed when debris fell onto a vehicle in the Al Barsha area.
