Olivia Dean and Niall Horan to Headline Radio 1's Big Weekend 2026

Olivia Dean and Niall Horan will headline the final day of BBC Radio 1's Big Weekend 2026, which will take place in Sunderland from May 22-24. The festival will feature a diverse lineup of artists across its three-day event.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Olivia Dean will headline the final day of BBC Radio 1's Big Weekend when it heads to Sunderland this year, sharing the main stage with Niall Horan and American singer Kehlani on Sunday.

The annual music festival will take place in the city's Herrington Country Park from Friday 22 to Sunday 24 May, featuring a diverse lineup of artists.

On the Saturday, singers Zara Larsson, Louis Tomlinson, and Lola Young will take to the main stage, while DJs Fatboy Slim and Sonny Fodera will kick off the festival on the dance-focused first day.

Olivia Dean's performance will be her first UK headline set, according to BBC Radio 1. The 26-year-old Londoner has been making waves in the music industry, recently picking up four awards at the Brits, including best British artist.

Her soulful second record, The Art of Loving, won best album, and she also won song of the year for her current number one collaboration with Sam Fender, Rein Me In.

Niall Horan will release his new single Dinner Party later this month, his first solo song since his last album in 2023. Irish singer CMAT and Brit Rising Star winner Myles Smith will also be on the main stage on Sunday.

The festival will feature about 100 acts across its three-day event, with tens of thousands of fans expected to attend. Tickets for the first day have already gone on sale, and Saturday and Sunday tickets will go on sale from 17:00 GMT on Wednesday, with local residents getting priority.

General sale Saturday and Sunday tickets will cost £44.50 per day, while VIP will cost £106.00.
