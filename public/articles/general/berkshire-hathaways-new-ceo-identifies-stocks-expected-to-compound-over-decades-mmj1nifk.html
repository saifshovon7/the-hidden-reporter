Berkshire Hathaway's New CEO Identifies Stocks Expected to Compound Over Decades

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Motley Fool

New CEO Greg Abel has delivered his first annual letter to Berkshire Hathaway's shareholders, a tradition that former CEO Warren Buffett carried out for the past six decades.

The letter provided a detailed overview of all of Berkshire's operating businesses and comments on its massive, roughly $318 billion equities portfolio.

Abel called out four stocks that Berkshire owns, which together account for a large portion of the portfolio, saying he expects "limited activity in these holdings."

The four stocks are Apple, American Express, Coca-Cola, and Wells Fargo.

Apple, the iconic consumer tech giant, has long been the largest position in Berkshire's portfolio, accounting for 40% of it at one point.

However, Berkshire has trimmed its stake in Apple by about 75% in recent years, but Apple continues to buy back stock, an attribute Buffett and the team have frequently looked for in their holdings.

American Express, the payments and credit card company, has been a constant in Berkshire's portfolio, with Buffett first purchasing it in 1964 and adding significantly to its stake in the 1990s.

Abel's comments on these stocks provide big clues about Berkshire's investment strategy, a departure from Buffett's rare comments on the company's holdings.
