Trump's Mixed Messages Leave Uncertainty on Iran War Timeline and Goals

President Trump's mixed messages on the Iran war have left uncertainty on the timeline and goals of the operation, causing market fluctuations and confusion.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

President Donald Trump and his administration have offered mixed messages and contradictory explanations on the joint US-Israeli military campaign against Iran, leaving uncertainty on the war's timeline and ultimate goals.

On the 10th day of the operation, Trump's comments were lacking in clarity, even when pushed for more detail. He told a reporter from the New York Post, 'I have a plan for everything, okay? I have a plan for everything. You'll be very happy.'

When asked about spiking oil prices, Trump said the war 'is very complete, pretty much.' He added, 'We're very far ahead of schedule,' but when asked whether the operation could end soon, he said, 'I don't know, it depends. Wrapping up is all in my mind, nobody else's.'

Trump's telephone spree had the desired effect, with stock markets rallying and the price of a barrel of oil dropping below $90. However, just days ago, Trump said he would not stop the war until Iran's 'unconditional surrender.'

Later in the day, Trump walked back his comments, saying, 'We could call it a tremendous success right now. Or we could go further. And we're going to go further.'

He said the US was 'very close to finishing' an 'excursion' but warned that the US would intensify its strikes if Iran continued to threaten oil tankers exiting the Persian Gulf.

Trump also laid out an expansive mission for the war, stating that his goal was to ensure that Iran could not develop weaponry to target the US, Israel, or any American allies 'for a very long time.'

Those looking for signs of when this massive military operation could end or clues as to its concrete goals were left with more questions than answers.
