Government Appoints Donna Ockenden as Leeds Hospitals Trust Maternity Inquiry Chair

The UK government has appointed Donna Ockenden as the chair of an independent inquiry into maternity failures at Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust, following a campaign by bereaved and harmed families.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

The health secretary has made a U-turn over who will lead an independent inquiry into 'repeated maternity failures' at an NHS trust. Donna Ockenden, a senior midwife, has been appointed to lead the review into maternity and neonatal services at Leeds Teaching Hospitals (LTH) NHS Trust.

Ockenden is currently leading the maternity review at Nottingham, which is the largest of its kind, examining about 2,500 cases of harm to mothers and babies. The review is expected to involve case reviews of stillbirths, neonatal deaths and serious injuries, hypoxic injuries and maternal deaths between 1 January 2011 and 31 December 2025.

The appointment comes after a campaign by bereaved and harmed families, who had urged Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer to 'intervene and appoint' the senior midwife immediately to head the Leeds inquiry. Families had lost confidence in the initial decision, which was made by Health Secretary Wes Streeting in October 2025.

Amarjit Kaur Matharoo, whose daughter Asees was stillborn in January 2024, said the family is 'relieved' by the appointment. 'It had been a really exhausting, long road to get to a point where we've got a chair that we all agree upon, is going to be completely independent,' she said.

Lauren Caulfield, whose daughter was stillborn in March 2022, also welcomed the appointment, saying it was 'the best gift I could give her, ensuring her little life is actually going to make a change.'

Ockenden said she 'commends the secretary of state' for 'making the right decision from the families' perspective'. 'Families have been very clear for a very long time that their request was for me to chair their independent maternity services at Leeds. They have met with the secretary of state on a number of occasions, and in the last meeting that I believe went on for several hours... he listened very carefully and came back to them and said 'actually this is the right decision'. So I am pleased with that but I do recognise, as we all will, that it has taken a very long time to get to here today,' she said.
