Inmate Charged with Murder of Notorious Killer Ian Huntley

A prison inmate has been charged with the murder of Ian Huntley, the former school caretaker who murdered 10-year-olds Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman in 2002.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A prison inmate has been charged with the murder of Ian Huntley, the former school caretaker who murdered 10-year-olds Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman in 2002, police have said.

Anthony Russell, 43, will appear at Newton Aycliffe Magistrates' Court via video link on Wednesday, a spokesman for Durham Constabulary said.

Huntley died on Saturday following an attack at HMP Frankland in County Durham at the end of February.

The former school caretaker's case remains one of the most shocking and devastating in the nation's history, with Holly and Jessica's families still seeking justice.

Huntley was serving a life sentence, with a minimum term of 40 years, for murdering the two schoolgirls.

He had been attacked in prison twice before, once with boiling water at HMP Wakefield in 2005 and again with a makeshift weapon at HMP Frankland in 2010.

Emergency services were called to reports of an assault in the workshop on the morning of Thursday, February 26, police said.

Huntley, 52, was taken to hospital with serious injuries where he died nine days later, police said.

His girlfriend, Maxine Carr, was also jailed in 2003 after being found guilty of conspiring to pervert the course of justice for giving him a false alibi.

The schoolgirls vanished after leaving a family barbecue in Soham in August 2002, and their disappearance made national headlines.

Their bodies were found in a ditch a fortnight after they went missing, and Huntley was arrested the same day.
