New Zealand Charity Shop Receives Accidental Donation of Cannabis and Cash

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Two teenagers have been arrested in New Zealand after accidentally leaving a backpack containing cannabis and thousands of dollars in cash outside a charity shop, police have revealed.

A shop volunteer discovered the strong-smelling backpack in the donation area and found 43.2g of marijuana and NZ$3,700 ($2,200) in banknotes inside.

The 16-year-old pair had left the bag outside the shop in the Southland region on February 18 as they waited for a vehicle to be serviced.

When the shop worker took the bag inside, they noticed a distinctive pungent aroma, which led to the discovery of the cannabis and cash.

Police arrested the teenagers and searched their vehicle, finding an air pistol, a police scanner, and more cash.

Although possession of a police scanner is not illegal in New Zealand, distributing or acting on information heard on it is a crime.

Police have not released any details of charges or court appearances due to the country's strict rules about reporting cases before the Youth Court.
