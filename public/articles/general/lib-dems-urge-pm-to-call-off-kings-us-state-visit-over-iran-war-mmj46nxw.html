Lib Dems Urge PM to Call Off King's US State Visit Over Iran War

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

The Liberal Democrats are calling on Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer to cancel the King's upcoming state visit to the United States over concerns about President Donald Trump's criticism of the British response to the Iran conflict.

The monarch and Queen Camilla are set to meet the US president at the end of April, but Liberal Democrat leader Sir Ed Davey has urged the prime minister to advise the monarch that the trip 'should be called off'.

Sir Ed argued that the visit would hand a 'huge diplomatic coup' to Trump during US-Israeli strikes on Iran, which have strained transatlantic ties.

The conflict has led to criticism from Trump, who branded Sir Keir 'no Winston Churchill' last week and accused him of wanting to 'join wars after we've already won'.

Sir Keir Starmer and Trump spoke for the first time since the row escalated, but the details of the call remain limited.

In a statement, Sir Ed said: 'At a time when Trump has launched an illegal war that is devastating the Middle East and pushing up energy bills for British families, it's clear this visit should not go ahead.'

A Labour MP has also suggested that the prime minister may face pressure from the party's MPs to cancel the King's visit as the date approaches.
