Iranians Divided Over Mojtaba Khamenei's Rise to Power

Iranians are deeply divided over Mojtaba Khamenei's rise to power, with some celebrating his appointment as a hardliner close to the IRGC, while others believe it will not bring about change.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Iran's new supreme leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, has been named successor to his father, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who was killed on the first day of the war with the US and Iran.

While some pro-establishment crowds have taken to the streets to celebrate the appointment of a hardliner close to the powerful Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), others believe it will not bring about change.

Advertisement

A man in his 30s in Tehran told the BBC that he believes the Assembly of Experts, an 88-member clerical body, could not have selected anyone closer to Ali Khamenei, and that everything will remain the same under Mojtaba's rule.

For years, there have been rumours that Mojtaba Khamenei held considerable influence behind the scenes in Iran, and many expect him to continue his father's hardline policies.

A woman in her 20s in Tehran expressed concern that Mojtaba Khamenei would be 'even more oppressive than his dad', while another resident said he is vengeful and will take revenge on ordinary people if he can't target the US.

Iranian state TV news channel IRINN has been showing pro-establishment rallies held for Mojtaba Khamenei in different cities, but he has yet to make a public appearance or speech.
