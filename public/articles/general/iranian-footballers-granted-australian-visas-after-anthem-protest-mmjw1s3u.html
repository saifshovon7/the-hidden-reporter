Iranian Footballers Granted Australian Visas After Anthem Protest

Five Iranian women's football team members have been granted humanitarian visas in Australia after their elimination in the Asian Cup, following their decision not to sing the national anthem.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Five members of the Iranian women's football team have been granted humanitarian visas in Australia after their elimination in the Asian Cup, according to the Australian government.

Immigration Minister Tony Burke said the women were moved to a safe location by Australian police and were happy to have their names confirmed as Fatemeh Pasandideh, Zahra Ghanbari, Zahra Sarbali, Atefeh Ramazanzadeh, and Mona Hamoudi.

Speaking early on Tuesday, Burke said the women were not political activists but athletes who wanted to be safe.

The group left their hotel on Monday and were moved to a safe location by police, where Burke met with them and signed off for their applications to go onto humanitarian visas.

Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese later confirmed that the five players had been given humanitarian visas.

The decision comes after the team declined to sing the national anthem ahead of their match against South Korea last week, prompting criticism in Iran.

Australia's humanitarian visa programme grants permanent protection to refugees and people in humanitarian need, allowing visa holders to live, work, and study in the country.

Talks between the Australian government and the Iranian women had been going on for several days, with Burke saying that on Monday it was made clear that there were five women who wanted to stay in Australia.

The move has been welcomed by supporters, who had raised fears for the women's safety after the team's decision not to sing the national anthem.

On Monday, hundreds of supporters surrounded the Iranian Lionesses' coach as it left the stadium on the Gold Coast, with chants of "save our girls".

A family member of one of the women, speaking on condition of anonymity, told Australian news site ABC that they were being protected by police and intended to claim asylum.

US President Donald Trump also weighed in on the issue, calling for Australia to give asylum to the women on his Truth Social platform.
