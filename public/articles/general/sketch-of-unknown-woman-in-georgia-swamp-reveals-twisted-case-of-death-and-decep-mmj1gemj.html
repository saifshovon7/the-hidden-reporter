Sketch of Unknown Woman in Georgia Swamp Reveals Twisted Case of Death and Deception

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: CBS News

Investigator Jack Frost, now with the Liberty County, Georgia, district attorney's office, was one of the first detectives called to the scene of a swamp in south Georgia on Dec. 2, 2022.

Frost was tasked with investigating the discovery of a female torso in the ditch, which was found by hunters.

During the initial investigation, Frost and his team recovered a razor-sharp Milwaukee brand knife, a plastic storage tub with what looked like traces of blood, and wipes.

It would take five days for Frost to find the rest of her body, and authorities later confirmed that the woman had defensive wounds.

Laurie Baio, an assistant district attorney with the Atlantic Judicial Circuit, explained that the discovery of a dismembered body in the woods is typically a sign of homicide.

To identify the victim, the Georgia Bureau of Investigation released two sketches, which sparked hundreds of calls and leads from civilians.

One call came from Heather Thomas, a woman living 500 miles away in Virginia, who recognized the victim as Mindi.

Heather Thomas was married to Mindi's ex-husband, Nick Kassotis, a Naval officer and lawyer who had traveled the globe.

Thomas described Kassotis as confident and reassuring, but also outgoing and talkative.

However, their marriage eventually faded, and Kassotis started dating Mindi in the same year.

Mindi was a legal secretary in Washington D.C., and Angela Wynn, a friend of Mindi's, described her as a consummate romantic.

The investigation into Mindi's death is ongoing, but the discovery of her body in the swamp has shed light on a twisted case of death and deception.
