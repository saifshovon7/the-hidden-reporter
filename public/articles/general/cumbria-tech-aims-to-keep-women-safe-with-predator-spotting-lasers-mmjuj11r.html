Cumbria Tech Aims to Keep Women Safe with Predator-Spotting Lasers

A technology company in Cumbria is developing a tool to use laser technology to identify and prevent potential threats to women's safety.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A new application of laser technology is being developed by Createc in Cockermouth, Cumbria, to help identify and prevent potential threats to women's safety.

The tool, currently in its early stages, uses laser technology to track people and detect unusual behaviors, such as loitering or following someone.

According to Rosie Richardson, a technology company product and strategy director, the tool is based on a system the company already uses to monitor crowds at airports and railway stations, including King's Cross in London.

The system tracks people using laser technology, showing each as a dot on a screen. If unusual behaviors are detected, security teams on the ground are alerted and can check if there is a problem.

Richardson, who has experience of unwanted predator attention, believes the technology can help spot the pattern of a predator and lead to intervention before anything happens.

The idea is that the lasers would spot the unnecessarily close choice of seat, registering it as unusual and a potential threat, and security teams would be alerted to direct CCTV for a closer look or send staff in person if needed.

Richardson says the technology in use at train stations and airports has enabled the company to use real-life data to map a pattern of behaviors.

The system spots patterns of movement and only goes in for a closer look if, based on what it knows about how threatening behavior manifests, it is deemed suspicious.
