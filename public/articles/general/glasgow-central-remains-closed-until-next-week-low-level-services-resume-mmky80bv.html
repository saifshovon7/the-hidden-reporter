Glasgow Central Remains Closed Until Next Week, Low-Level Services Resume

Glasgow Central's main station remains closed until next week due to a fire that destroyed a neighboring building, but low-level services will resume on Wednesday.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

The main train station at Glasgow Central will remain closed until next week following a huge fire that destroyed a neighboring building on Union Street.

Scotland's busiest railway station has been shut since Sunday, when a blaze spread from a vape shop through a B-listed Victorian building.

Services from the smaller low-level station, which runs services to Balloch and Milngavie in the west and Motherwell and Cumbernauld to the east, will resume on Wednesday.

However, Network Rail said the high-level station, which is used by the vast majority of routes, would stay shut for the rest of this week due to a 'high level of instability' to the building façade on Gordon Street.

Network Rail route director Ross Moran said, 'Unfortunately, further work is required to ensure the high-level station is safe for trains to operate.'

Glasgow Central is one of the busiest stations in the UK, with 25 million passenger visits each year. It is the main station for all services on the west coast mainline to England and also operates services throughout central Scotland.

A fire break between the Union Corner building and the external wall of the station stopped Sunday's inferno from spreading further.

A step-by-step process will need to be undertaken before the main station can re-open, including damping down hot spots in the rubble, handing the site over to Glasgow City Council, and carrying out safety inspections.
