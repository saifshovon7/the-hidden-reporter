Iran Backs Off Regional Strikes Unless Provoked, Warns Israel on Successor to Supreme Leader Khamenei

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Fox News

Israel warned Iran on Sunday that it "will not hesitate to target" anyone who participates in the session to select a successor to Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who was killed in a joint attack launched by the U.S. and Israel on Feb. 28.

Col. Avichay Adraee, a spokesperson for the Israeli Defense Forces to the Arab media, posted the warning on X, stating that the Iranian terrorist regime is attempting to regroup and select a new leader.

The Israeli Defense Forces said it struck F-14 fighter jets Saturday that were being stored at an airport in Isfahan, Iran, and destroyed 16 aircraft used by the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps' Quds Force at Mehrabad International Airport in Tehran.

Israel launched a new wave of attacks across Iran, targeting Iranian regime infrastructure, and the military said missiles had been launched from Iran toward Israel and that Israeli defense systems were "operating to intercept the threat."

Exiled Iranian Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi said Saturday that the Islamic Republic is "crumbling" one week after U.S. and Israeli military operations rocked the regime, resulting in the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and nearly 50 other regime figures.

Pahlavi described the situation as a pivotal moment for change, saying that people were waiting for an opportunity to get back to the streets to take their country back and that elements of the regime were crumbling.
