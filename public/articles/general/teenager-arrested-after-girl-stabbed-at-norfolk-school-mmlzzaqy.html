Teenager Arrested After Girl Stabbed at Norfolk School

A 16-year-old boy has been arrested after a teenage girl was stabbed at Thorpe St Andrew School in Norfolk, with the students placed under lockdown conditions. The victim suffered minor injuries.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 11, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A 16-year-old boy has been arrested after a teenage girl was stabbed at a high school in Norfolk.

The victim was taken to hospital with minor injuries following the incident at Thorpe St Andrew School, near Norwich.

The school's students were placed under lockdown conditions as police investigated the incident.

Norfolk Police received a call at around 10:24 GMT and arrested the 16-year-old boy on suspicion of causing grievous bodily harm with intent.

Launder Lane, where the school is located, was closed by police.

A police spokesperson stated that the incident is not believed to be linked to a hate crime reported at the same school last week.

Parents have expressed concerns for their children's safety after limited information was provided by the school and Norfolk Police.

The school's management, Broad Horizons Education Trust, declined to comment further on the incident.
