Penn State Dominates 2026 Big Ten Tournament

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: NCAA.com

Penn State secured the 2026 Big Ten tournament title with a strong performance from its wrestlers, earning multiple individual belts in the process.

Ohio State's Jesse Mendez was one of the few individuals not representing Penn State, winning the 141-pound title with a 7-2 victory over Nebraska's Brock Hardy.

Shayne Van Ness pinned Ohio State's Ethan Stiles to win the 149-pound crown for Penn State, while PJ Duke defeated Antrell Taylor by major decision to win the 157-pound title.

Mitchell Mesenbrink won a second straight major decision with a 12-3 victory over Iowa's Michael Caliendo, securing his third Big Ten title and remaining undefeated in the conference.

Levi Haines earned his fourth Big Ten individual title with a narrow 2-1 win over Nebraska's Christopher Minto, while Rocco Welsh survived a challenge from Max McEnelly to win the 184-pound title.

Josh Barr dominated the 197-pound competition with a 19-4 tech fall over Nebraska's Camden McDanel, becoming the 197-pound Big Ten champion.

In the heavyweight match, Taye Ghadiali secured the title with a 5-2 victory over AJ Ferrari, giving Michigan an individual title at the expense of Nebraska.
