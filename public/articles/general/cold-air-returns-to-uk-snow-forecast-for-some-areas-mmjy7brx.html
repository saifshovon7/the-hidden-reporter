Cold Air Returns to UK, Snow Forecast for Some Areas

Single-digit temperatures and the chance of snow are forecast for some areas of the UK this week, as colder air returns from the north-west.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

After a taste of springlike warmth across the UK, single-digit temperatures are set to return this week with the chance of snow for some areas.

A number of fronts are set to progressively bring colder air from the north-west across the UK, with daytime temperatures dropping and potentially struggling to reach double figures by Friday.

The cold air is being brought about by the ramping up of the jet stream, delivering a succession of weather fronts with increasingly wet conditions.

There is even the possibility of wintry showers by the end of the week, with strong winds sweeping away mist and fog in many areas.

Wintry showers of sleet and snow are possible over high ground in the north of the UK, with the chance of accumulating snow on some hills and mountains.

While snow is unlikely to settle in the south, fast-moving hail showers are possible almost anywhere and brief flurries of wet snow are not out of the question.

At the weekend, a potentially nasty area of low pressure may sweep across the country, with gales most likely over Scotland and gusts of between 40 and 50mph expected in many parts of the UK.

The good news is that there will be lulls between the successive weather systems, with sparkling sunshine and crisper conditions.

Rain and gales are likely on Saturday night, with the prospect of wintry showers later in the day on Sunday.

The duration of the colder weather remains unclear, with some computer models suggesting a return to high pressure and drier conditions beyond the weekend, while others predict a longer period of unsettled weather.
