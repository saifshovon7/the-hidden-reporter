Draper Faces Djokovic in Indian Wells Fourth Round, Norrie Advances

Jack Draper and Cameron Norrie secured victories in the Indian Wells tournament, with Draper set to face Novak Djokovic in the fourth round and Norrie to meet Australian qualifier Rinky Hijikata.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

British tennis player Jack Draper will continue his Indian Wells title defence against Novak Djokovic after an impressive win on Monday. Draper, who returned to the ATP Tour last week after an eight-month absence due to an arm injury, beat Argentina's Francisco Cerundolo 6-1 7-5.

Draper's victory sets up a last-16 meeting with 24-time Grand Slam champion Djokovic, who overcame American Aleksandar Kovacevic 6-4 1-6 6-4. British number two Cameron Norrie also reached the fourth round by upsetting the sixth seed, Australia's Alex de Minaur, 6-4 6-4.

Draper, 24, is glad to be back competing but acknowledges that his tennis is not yet at its peak. 'I'm only just getting to the point where I'm playing without pain in my arm, so it's not easy to feel really confident,' Draper told Sky Sports.

Draper's win means he will face Djokovic for the first time since he took the first set off the defending champion on his Wimbledon debut in 2021. 'I've been watching him since I was a young boy and in my opinion he's the greatest player of all time,' said Draper.

Norrie is the 27th seed in the men's draw and will face Australian qualifier Rinky Hijikata in the next round. Hijikata beat Kazakhstan's 10th seed Alexander Bublik in three sets.
