Rape Victim Warned Police About Wrong Suspect

A woman who wrongly identified a suspect in a 2003 rape case has told a jury she later had doubts, but police reassured her it was just 'trial nerves'. The case is now against Paul Quinn, who denies rape and other charges.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A woman who wrongly identified Andy Malkinson in a video identity parade after being raped has told a jury she later had doubts he was the attacker, but police officers reassured her she had 'got the right man'.

Malkinson was wrongfully convicted and spent 17 years in jail for the 2003 rape in Salford.

The woman, who was attacked and strangled unconscious before being raped twice, has no memory of the assault and did not know her attacker.

She picked Malkinson out in a police video identification procedure weeks after the attack, and her evidence at his 2004 trial led to his conviction.

However, she later raised her concerns with police at court, telling them she was unsure it was the right man, but they reassured her it was just 'trial nerves'.

The woman said she was 'very naive' and had 'listened to what the police said' when she expressed her doubts.

The case is now against Paul Quinn, who denies rape, strangulation, and inflicting grievous bodily harm with intent.

Quinn was linked to the crime years later, after scientific advances matched his DNA profile from samples left on the victim.

The DNA findings suggest it would be at least one billion times more likely if Quinn was a contributor to the sample found at the crime scene than if he was not.
