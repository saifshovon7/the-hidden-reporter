Iran Conflict Pushes Up Oil Prices Amid Turmoil in Middle East

The price of crude oil has surged to over $100 a barrel due to the ongoing conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran, causing disruptions in air travel and trade.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

As turmoil continues in the Middle East, the price of crude oil rose to more than $100 a barrel on Monday. The conflict between the United States, Israel, and Iran has led to increased tensions in the region, causing a surge in oil prices.

The BBC's Nick Marsh examines the reasons behind the price hike and how it may impact consumers. The conflict has resulted in the closure of nearly all airspace in the Middle East, disrupting air travel and trade.

A timelapse of marine traffic in the Strait of Hormuz shows a decrease in the flow of ships, further exacerbating the oil price increase. The US Secretary of Defense, Pete Hegseth, confirmed that the US military has sunk an Iranian warship in the Indian Ocean using a torpedo.

The Trump administration's messaging on war has shifted in its second term, with President Donald Trump criticizing the Spanish government for blocking the US from using its bases during the attack on Iran. The US president also lashed out at Prime Minister Keir Starmer, stating that he is 'not Winston Churchill that we're dealing with.'

The conflict has resulted in a significant increase in oil prices, with the price of crude oil exceeding $100 a barrel. The situation in the Middle East remains fluid, with the US, Israel, and Iran trading strikes and escalating tensions.
