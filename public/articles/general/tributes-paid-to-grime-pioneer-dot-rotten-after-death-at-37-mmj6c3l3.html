Tributes Paid to Grime Pioneer Dot Rotten After Death at 37

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Tributes have been paid to grime pioneer Dot Rotten, who has died at the age of 37. The musician's death was confirmed to the BBC via his family.

There were no details of the circumstances, although online reports suggested he had died in Gambia.

Born Joseph Ellis in Stockwell, London, Dot Rotten developed a distinctive sound that mixed hard-edged beats with emotional hooks. He worked with prominent artists including Chip, D Double E, Cher Lloyd, and Ed Sheeran.

The rapper scored a Top 20 hit in 2012 with the melancholy single Overload, which was inspired by his experiences with depression. The song's lyrics were free-styled, and he had reservations about releasing such a personal song.

Although he achieved success with Overload, his debut album Voices In My Head stalled outside the Top 100 due to a dispute with his record label. After extricating himself from the contract, he released a series of independent albums and gradually moved behind the scenes as a writer and producer.

Dot Rotten started his career at the age of seven, rapping and making his own music on an Atari computer system. He released his first mixtape, This Is the Beginning, in 2007, and later changed his stage name to Dot Rotten, which was an acronym for "Dirty on Tracks, Righteous Opinions Told to Educate Nubians".
