Mapped: US-Israeli Strikes Across Iran and the Middle East

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: BBC News

Israel's military has continued to strike targets in Iran and the Middle East, with reports of oil depots being hit in and around Tehran. Verified video shows thick black smoke in the sky above the city after at least four oil facilities were hit overnight.

Fires could still be seen at the Shahran oil depot in the north-west of the city on Sunday. The Israeli military has also continued attacks on Lebanon, saying it had carried out a 'precise strike' targeting commanders from Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps.

Lebanese officials said four people were killed after a strike on a hotel in Beirut. In the Gulf, a government tower block caught fire in Kuwait following a drone strike early on Sunday morning, while Bahrain said a water desalination plant was struck.

Missile alerts were also issued in the United Arab Emirates (UAE). The US-based Human Rights Activists News Agency (HRANA) says 1,205 civilians are reported to have been killed in Iran since 28 February.

Six US service personnel were killed in a strike on a military base in Kuwait. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) posted on Sunday morning that it had attacked 400 targets in western and central Iran in the past 24 hours.

Iran's foreign ministry said the strikes had released 'hazardous materials and toxic substances into the air' and accused Israel of 'poisoning civilians'. The first US-Israeli strikes on Iran began on 28 February with Israel's military saying it had targeted Iran's leadership and military infrastructure.

At least 168 people, including children, were killed at a girls' school in Minab, in the south of Iran, when it was hit on 28 February, Iranian authorities said.

Israeli forces have also carried out air strikes in Lebanon targeting Hezbollah, the militant group that has long been backed by Iran. Hezbollah launched missiles towards Israel on 2 March following the killing of Khamenei.
