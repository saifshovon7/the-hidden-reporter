Iran and Middle East Hit by Ongoing Strikes

The US-Israeli war with Iran continues for a tenth day, with the Israeli military launching more strikes on Iran and targets in Lebanon. Iran has launched strikes across the Middle East, with several countries reporting missile and drone attacks.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

The Israeli military has launched more strikes on Iran, along with targets in Lebanon, as the US-Israeli war with Iran continues for a tenth day.

Turkey's defence ministry said a ballistic missile launched from Iran was destroyed by NATO air and missile defence assets.

Qatar, Kuwait, the United Arab Emirates (UAE) and Saudi Arabia reported new attacks overnight, with the UAE intercepting 17 ballistic missiles and six drones from Iran on Monday.

The US-based Human Rights Activists News Agency (HRANA) reports that 1,205 civilians have been killed in Iran since February 28.

Seven US service personnel have been killed since the start of the conflict, according to the Department of Defense.

The US-Israeli strikes on Iran began on February 28, with Israel's military targeting Iran's leadership and its military infrastructure.

Iran's former Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, was killed in Tehran during the initial strikes.

The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) said 'approximately 3,400 strikes' have been carried out so far, with more than 150 Iranian defence systems disabled.

BBC Verify has seen footage of smoke and fires at four Iranian oil facilities following Israeli strikes.

Iran's foreign ministry accused Israel of 'poisoning civilians' after releasing 'hazardous materials and toxic substances into the air'.

IDF spokesman Lt Col Nadav Shoshani said such sites were a 'legal, military target'.

BBC Verify has confirmed visual evidence of more than 110 attacks across Iran, including at least 49 strikes in Tehran.

Satellite imagery reviewed by BBC Verify has shown at least 168 people, including children, were killed at a girls' school in Minab, in the south of Iran, when it was hit on February 28.

A US Tomahawk missile was reportedly used in the strike, which experts say neither Israel nor Iran possess.

Iran has also launched strikes across the Middle East, with the governments of Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain and the UAE reporting what they said were Iranian missile and drone attacks on Sunday.

Kuwait said it contained a major fire in a government office block following a reported attack during the early hours of the morning.

The UAE said its air defences intercepted 16 ballistic missiles and 113 drones on Sunday.
