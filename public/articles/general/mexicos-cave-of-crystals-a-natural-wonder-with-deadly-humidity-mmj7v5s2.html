Mexico's Cave of Crystals: A Natural Wonder with Deadly Humidity

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Discoverwildlife.com

The Cave of Crystals, located in Chihuahua, Mexico, is a staggering 300 metres underground and features incredible 11-metre-tall gypsum crystals.

The cave has earned the nickname 'Sistine Chapel of crystals' due to the sheer size and majesty of the crystals, some of which are large enough to walk on.

Advertisement

The cave sits atop a fault line above a chamber of magma, where millions of years ago, scorching magma forced mineral-rich groundwater up through the rock, creating caverns in the limestone and forming deposits that slowly cooled and crystallised.

However, the cave's temperature of 58 degrees Celsius, while not extreme, is accompanied by a deadly 90% humidity level, making it impossible for humans to cool themselves down through sweating.

Without specialist equipment, any person in the cave would quickly overheat, making it a place best viewed through photographs.
