High-Voltage Power Lines Expansion Sparks Local Resistance

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Associated Press

John Zola stands in an apple tree grove on his property on a spot where he says the local electric utility wants to build a 500-kilovolt line across his land, March 4, 2026, in Sugarloaf, Pa. (AP Photo/Marc Levy)

The expansion of high-voltage power lines, spurred by advancements in artificial intelligence, is meeting resistance from landowners and local communities across the country. The proposed 500-kilovolt power line in Sugarloaf, Pennsylvania, has sparked concerns among residents who fear the impact on their properties and the environment.

John Zola, a local landowner, is one of those opposing the plan. He believes the power line will not only affect the aesthetic value of his property but also pose a risk to the health and safety of his family. Zola’s property, which includes an apple tree grove and a basketball court, would be directly affected by the proposed power line.

According to Zola, the local power utility has not provided sufficient information about the project, including the potential environmental impact and the compensation he would receive for the land acquisition. He is not alone in his concerns, as many landowners and local residents are fighting back against the expansion of high-voltage power lines.

The proposed power line is part of a larger effort to modernize the country’s energy infrastructure, which is being driven by the increasing demand for electricity and the need to reduce carbon emissions. However, the push for high-voltage power lines has been met with resistance from communities who are concerned about the impact on their properties and the environment.

As the debate continues, it remains to be seen whether the expansion of high-voltage power lines will proceed as planned or will be delayed due to local resistance.
