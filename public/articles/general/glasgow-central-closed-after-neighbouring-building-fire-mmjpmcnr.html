Glasgow Central Closed After Neighbouring Building Fire

Glasgow Central Station has been closed after a fire in a neighbouring building, with disruption expected to continue for several days.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

A fire in a B-listed Victorian building next to Glasgow Central Station has caused significant disruption to rail services. The blaze, which began in a vape shop, destroyed the building and forced the closure of the station.

Network Rail has warned that the station will remain closed on Tuesday for safety inspections, with no services operating in or out of the station. Disruption is expected to continue for several days, with scores of ScotRail services affected.

ScotRail's chief operating officer, David Ross, advised passengers to check the ScotRail website and app for updates on services, but warned that there would be no clarity on services until the fire service gave Network Rail access to the station.

Trains will not stop at the low-level platforms, but will pass through the station to stop at nearby Argyle Street and Anderston stations. Limited replacement buses will be available for some services.

Avanti West Coast has offered passengers the option to use tickets at no extra cost on LNER services between Edinburgh and London King's Cross. TransPennine Express has also announced that services between Glasgow Central and Liverpool Lime Street and Manchester Airport will not run on Monday.

Police Scotland has closed several roads around Union Street and Central Station, including First Bus Greater Glasgow routes. The Scottish Fire and Rescue Service (SFRS) has scaled back its response to the incident, with eight fire engines still on site.
