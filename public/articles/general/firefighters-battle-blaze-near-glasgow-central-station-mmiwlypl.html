Firefighters Battle Blaze Near Glasgow Central Station

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Firefighters are working to extinguish a blaze that broke out near Glasgow Central Station on Sunday. The fire, which is reported to have started at a vape shop, has spread to engulf a four-storey Victorian building.

The station remains closed until further notice, with ScotRail announcing that no trains will operate in or out of the station on Monday. The fire has caused significant disruptions to travel, with many passengers facing delays and cancellations.

According to eyewitnesses, the fire was quickly spreading due to strong winds, making it challenging for firefighters to control. The cause of the fire is currently unknown, but an investigation is underway.

Glasgow residents have expressed concern about the safety of the area, with some calling for increased security measures to be put in place. The fire has also raised questions about the safety of nearby buildings and the potential risks associated with the vape shop.

As the situation continues to unfold, residents and commuters are advised to check for updates and follow instructions from authorities. The fire is a stark reminder of the importance of fire safety and the need for vigilance in preventing such incidents.
