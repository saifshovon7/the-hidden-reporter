Iran Names Mojtaba Khamenei as New Supreme Leader Amid Ongoing Tensions

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: Associated Press

Mojtaba Khamenei, the son of Iran's late supreme leader, has been named his successor, Iranian state TV announced early Monday.

The announcement came as tensions between Iran and the US-Israeli coalition continue to escalate, with Iran's president threatening to step up attacks on American targets throughout the Middle East.

State TV read a statement saying Mojtaba Khamenei was selected based on 'strong' votes and showed people celebrating in public areas of Tehran.

The selection of Mojtaba Khamenei as the new supreme leader is seen as a move to maintain continuity and stability within the Iranian government, despite his lack of elected or appointed government positions.

The Israeli attack on Iran's oil depot and refinery on Sunday morning resulted in a cloud of black smoke covering parts of Tehran, with the US-Israeli aerial bombardment campaign continuing.

Iran has accused the US and Israel of targeting four oil storage tankers and a petroleum transfer terminal, killing four people, and has threatened to respond with further attacks.
