Spain Grants Amnesty to Half a Million Undocumented Migrants

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Spain plans to give half a million undocumented migrants legal status, providing them with a one-year, renewable, residency visa. The scheme will be open from April 1 to June 30, and applicants must prove they have spent at least five months in the country and have no criminal record.

Diana, a 40-year-old migrant from Peru, has been living in Spain undocumented for two years. She has struggled to find stable work and has been exploited by employers.

But with the recent announcement by the Spanish government, Diana and many others are hopeful. 'It's going to help us in every possible way,' she says. 'It'll be good for the government too, because we will be paying taxes, generating income, and setting up businesses.'

The scheme has been welcomed by the international community, with the police's National Centre for Immigration and Borders (CNIF) estimating that between 750,000 and 1.1 million migrants may seek to benefit from it.

The Spanish government has cited humanitarian reasons for the initiative, with Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez describing immigrants as people 'who have built the progress of this country alongside us'. The government also sees the measure as highly pragmatic, given Spain's unemployment rate is at an 18-year low and its economy grew by nearly 3% last year.

Elma Saiz, Minister for Inclusion, Social Security and Immigration, underlines that foreign workers play a crucial role in Spain's economic success. 'Since 2022, half of Spain's economic growth has been driven by foreign workers,' she adds.
