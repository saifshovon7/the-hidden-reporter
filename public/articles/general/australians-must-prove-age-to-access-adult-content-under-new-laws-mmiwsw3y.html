Australians Must Prove Age to Access Adult Content Under New Laws

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Australians must now prove they are over 18 before accessing adult content, including porn, R-rated video games, and AI chatbots, under new laws aimed at protecting children from harmful material.

The changes, introduced by Australia's online safety regulator, require platforms to implement stricter age-verification checks, including facial recognition technology, digital IDs, and credit card details.

Companies behind search engines, app stores, social media, gaming platforms, porn sites, and AI systems must take "meaningful steps" to prevent children from being exposed to adult content, according to the new rules.

Research by the agency found that one in three children aged 10-17 had seen sexual images or videos online, and more than 70% had been exposed to online content showing high-impact violence, self-harm, and suicide material.

Days before the new measures came into effect, several major adult content platforms, including RedTube, YouPorn, and Tube8, stopped Australians from registering accounts and accessing content.

Experts have raised concerns over data privacy and users trying to trick age-verification technologies, with some arguing that the new laws will have limited impact in preventing young people from accessing restricted content.

Australia's eSafety Commissioner, Julie Inman Grant, said the new laws are necessary to protect children from harmful online content, comparing it to age restrictions in physical spaces like bars, bottle shops, and casinos.
