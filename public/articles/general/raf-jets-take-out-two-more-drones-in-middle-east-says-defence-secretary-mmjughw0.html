RAF Jets Take Out Two More Drones in Middle East, Says Defence Secretary

RAF jets have destroyed two drones in the Middle East, Defence Secretary John Healey has confirmed. The UK has also taken steps to protect its interests in the region, but has been accused of not acting fast enough to protect Cyprus.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Defence Secretary John Healey has confirmed that RAF Typhoon fighter jets have taken out two drones in the Middle East. The jets destroyed one drone over Jordan and another that was heading to Bahrain.

Healey also announced that the warship HMS Dragon would set sail for the Eastern Mediterranean in the next couple of days. However, one of the UK's two aircraft carriers, HMS Prince of Wales, is now unlikely to head to the Middle East as part of efforts to protect British interests.

The carrier was placed on an advanced state of readiness on Saturday, meaning it could set sail within five days if ministers gave the order for it to be deployed. This led to speculation that it could be sent to the Mediterranean to protect the British base in Cyprus from Iranian drones and missiles.

But sources now say it is likely that the carrier will instead head to the Arctic, to take part in long-planned NATO exercises there. The decision to bolster the ship's readiness was not connected to the events in Iran, according to sources.

The UK has already taken steps to protect its interests in the Middle East, with extra fighter jets and the HMS Dragon warship being sent to the region following drone attacks on UK bases. However, the UK government has been accused of not acting fast enough to protect Cyprus from enemy drones and missiles.

Last week, a small drone struck the runway at RAF Akrotiri in Cyprus, causing minimal damage. Healey said the drone came from Lebanon or Iraq and that fragments of the drone were being analysed for foreign military hardware.
