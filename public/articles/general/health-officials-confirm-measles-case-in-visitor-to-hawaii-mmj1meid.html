Health Officials Confirm Measles Case in Visitor to Hawaii

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 7, 2026 Source: Honolulu Star-Advertiser

Hawaii health officials have confirmed a case of measles in a vaccinated adult visitor to Oahu. The visitor, who recently arrived in Hawaii from a region of the continental United States with known measles transmission, became ill and is recovering at a private residence on Oahu, according to a state Department of Health news release.

The health department advised the public that they may have been exposed to measles if they visited the following locations during the specified times:

> Daniel K. Inouye International Airport, A gates and baggage claim area, on Feb. 26, from 12:30 p.m.-4 p.m.

> Terminal 1 check-In, security, and A gates on Tuesday, from 9 a.m.-12:30 p.m.

> A gates and baggage claim area on Wednesday, from 8:30 p.m.-11 p.m.

> Laie Mormon Temple, on Feb. 27, from 4:30 p.m.-9 p.m.

> Hilo International Airport, Gate areas, baggage claim, on Tuesday, 11:30 a.m.-2:30 p.m.

> Hilo Siam Thai restaurant, on Tuesday, from 5 p.m.–9 p.m.

> Hawaii Volcanoes National Park, Visitors Center and other attractions, on Tuesday, from 12 p.m.-6 p.m.

Flight notifications have been issued to the airlines and airports through which the confirmed case traveled. The Health Department is also reaching out directly to individuals who had known contact with the confirmed or suspected case.

Health officials offered guidance for anyone who was at any of the above locations on the specified dates and times:

> Unvaccinated individuals should contact their healthcare provider immediately if they have never received a measles-containing vaccine. Vaccine or immune globulin can be given to prevent measles if received shortly after exposure.

> Immunocompromised individuals should consult with their healthcare provider if they have questions or develop symptoms.

> Vaccinated individuals who have received two doses of a measles-containing vaccine or were born before 1957 do not need to take any further action.
