Cumbria Firm Develops Predator-Spotting Tech for Women's Safety

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

A new application of laser technology is being developed by Createc in Cockermouth, Cumbria, to quickly identify and respond to potential threats against women in public spaces.

The tool, currently in development, uses laser beams and advanced mathematics to track people and detect unusual patterns of behavior, such as loitering or following someone.

According to Rosie Richardson, product and strategy director at Createc, the technology can help put the responsibility back on public authorities, space owners, and police forces to protect women.

Richardson, who has experienced unwanted predator attention, believes the technology can help spot the patterns of a predator and lead to intervention before anything happens.

The system, based on one already used to monitor crowds at airports and railway stations, including King's Cross in London, tracks people as dots on a screen and alerts security teams if unusual behavior is detected.

Richardson explains that the technology uses real-life data to map a pattern of behaviors and only goes in for a closer look if it is deemed suspicious.

The idea is that, on a deserted railway platform, the lasers would spot an unnecessarily close choice of seat and register it as unusual, prompting security teams to take action.

Richardson hopes that the technology will help reduce the number of assaults that occur daily and make public spaces safer for women.
