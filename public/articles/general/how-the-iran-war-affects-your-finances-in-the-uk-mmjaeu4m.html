How the Iran War Affects Your Finances in the UK

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

The US-Israeli war with Iran has already had an impact on people's finances in the UK, with rising petrol prices and mortgage rates.

According to the RAC motoring organisation, average petrol prices had ticked up by 4.68p to 137.51p a litre, while diesel had increased by 8.59p to 150.97p by Sunday.

Analysts say every $10 increase in oil pushes up pump prices by roughly 7p a litre, making average petrol prices of more than 140p a litre inevitable.

Motoring organisations are encouraging people to reduce non-essential journeys and amend their driving style to conserve fuel.

However, not everyone has a car, and the rise in petrol prices can carry through to higher prices for goods and services, such as food.

Some of the UK's biggest lenders have raised mortgage rates due to rising funding costs and an expectation that the base borrowing rate will not fall as previously anticipated.

The average rate on a two-year mortgage deal has risen to 4.87%, and the average five-year fix is up to 4.98%, according to Moneyfacts.

Lenders may pull mortgage products off the shelves during times of economic uncertainty, reducing choice for borrowers.

Energy bills and heating oil costs are also affected, although the price cap in England, Wales, and Scotland set by Ofgem provides some protection for household gas and electricity bills.

However, this protection is time-limited and does not cover everyone.

As the war with Iran continues, it remains to be seen how deep and sustained the impact on finances will be.
