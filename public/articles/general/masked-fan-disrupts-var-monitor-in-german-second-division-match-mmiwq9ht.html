Masked Fan Disrupts VAR Monitor in German Second Division Match

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

A masked fan caused a technical malfunction in a German second division match on Sunday, unplugging a video assistant referee (VAR) monitor at the end of the first half.

The incident occurred during Preussen Munster's 2-1 home defeat by Hertha Berlin in Bundesliga 2 in Cologne.

Referee Felix Bickel was asked by VAR to review a penalty award on a pitchside monitor, but found the screen blank due to the malfunction.

As a result, the decision was left to VAR official Katrin Rafalski, who ruled that it was a foul and awarded a penalty to Hertha Berlin, which they scored.

Preussen Munster stated that a masked spectator from the active fan section illegally entered the pitch and unplugged the technical equipment.

The club regrets the incident and is taking immediate measures to prevent similar disruptions in the future.

Initial findings indicate that the action was planned, with a banner reading 'Pull the plug on VAR' displayed in the home section shortly after the technical malfunction.

German media reported that a similar banner was displayed before the match.
