US Bombers Arrive at RAF Fairford Amid Conflict with Iran

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Three B-52 bombers have landed at RAF Fairford in Gloucestershire, UK, as the country approved 'defensive' US action against Iranian missile sites from UK bases.

The 49-meter-long bombers are capable of launching missiles over 1,500 miles and are part of a fleet of US military planes at the base.

Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer granted permission for the US to use RAF Fairford and Diego Garcia in the Indian Ocean, stating that the government 'does not believe in regime change from the skies.'

The decision came after a disagreement with President Donald Trump, who had initially refused to grant permission for the US to use UK bases in its joint offensive with Israel against Iran.

RAF Fairford has been used by the US in the past to conduct long-range heavy bombing missions and is one of the few European bases certified to operate bombers like the B-1, B-52, and B-2.

The B-52 is one of the US Air Force's longest-serving and most versatile heavy bombers, capable of carrying up to 70,000 pounds of mixed weapons.

The arrival of the B-52 bombers at RAF Fairford marks a significant development in the conflict with Iran, which has seen a surge in strikes on the country.
