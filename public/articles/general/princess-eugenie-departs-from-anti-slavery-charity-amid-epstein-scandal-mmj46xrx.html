Princess Eugenie Departs From Anti-Slavery Charity Amid Epstein Scandal

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Princess Eugenie has stepped down as patron of the UK-based charity Anti-Slavery International, a role she has held for seven years.

The charity thanked the princess for her support and confirmed the end of their relationship in a statement, citing no reason for the severing of ties.

The move comes amid renewed scrutiny of her father, Prince Andrew, over his links to the late convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein.

Anti-Slavery International, which has been running for 180 years, helps to prevent slavery in its many forms, including child slavery, forced labour and human trafficking.

In a statement, the charity said: 'After seven years, our patronage from HRH Princess Eugenie of York has come to an end. We thank the princess very much for her support for Anti-Slavery International. We hope that she continues to work to end slavery for good and deliver freedom for everyone.'

Princess Eugenie's departure from the charity follows the recent release of Epstein files, which have put additional pressure on her father.

The Charity Commission has confirmed it is 'assessing concerns' about a separate charity, The Anti-Slavery Collective, which the princess co-founded in 2017.

The Anti-Slavery Collective has been described as a 'preposterously inappropriate cause' for Eugenie to be involved with, and has faced criticism over its high-profile fundraising events and low charitable spending.

The charity's most recently available accounts show donations had slumped to £48,000, with the charity spending £191,537 on salaries, double what it spent on charity programmes.

The Charity Commission spokesperson said: 'We are assessing concerns raised in the media about charitable spending at The Anti-Slavery Collective to determine what role there is, if any, for the Commission.'
