G7 Considers Tapping Emergency Oil Reserves Amid Global Price Surge

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

As the global oil price soared to $115 a barrel, a meeting of G7 finance ministers was held to discuss a potential release of 300 million barrels of emergency stockpiles coordinated by the International Energy Agency.

The speculation surrounding the possible release was enough to temper the rise in prices, but they remain significantly higher than pre-conflict levels.

The potential drawdown of reserves has the potential to be the biggest oil shock in history, representing a quarter of global stockpiles and less than three days of global oil consumption.

However, there are concerns that the G7 finance ministers may not all agree on the release, with issues surrounding naval escorts and insurance for tankers operating in the Strait of Hormuz still unresolved.

The US has been pushing for Russia to be considered as a partial solution through the use of waivers against sanctions for Putin's war against Ukraine.

Releasing 300 million barrels of oil will not address all issues, including the supply of gas to key customers such as China, India, and South Korea.

The potential release is seen as a significant intervention, but few in the markets are willing to bet on declines in energy prices in these circumstances.

Experts warn that the impact of the potential release on UK petrol and diesel prices is unclear, and the situation remains highly volatile.
