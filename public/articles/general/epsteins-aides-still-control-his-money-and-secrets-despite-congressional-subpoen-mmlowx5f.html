Epstein's Aides Still Control His Money and Secrets, Despite Congressional Subpoena

Richard Kahn and Darren Indyke, the sole executors of Jeffrey Epstein's estate, are still controlling his wealth and possessions, despite being subpoenaed to testify before a congressional committee.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 11, 2026 · Source: BBC News

When the FBI raided Jeffrey Epstein's New York mansion in July 2019, agents found a large safe containing diamonds, bundles of cash, passports, and binders of CDs and hard drives. However, due to an issue with the warrant, they could not leave with the items, and when they returned with a new warrant, the safe had been emptied.

An investigation by The Hidden Reporter has revealed that Richard Kahn, Epstein's accountant and bookkeeper since 2005, and Darren Indyke, his long-serving lawyer, are still controlling Epstein's wealth and possessions, including compensation owed to survivors and secrets contained in documents held by the Epstein estate.

Kahn and Indyke are the sole executors of Epstein's estate, a position they were appointed to in August 2019, just two days before his death in jail awaiting trial for sex-trafficking minors. As executors, they have agreed compensation packages paid to survivors, which included conditions that prevented survivors from taking further legal action against them personally.

The value of Epstein's estate remains unclear, but it was estimated at roughly $635m at the time of his death. Kahn and Indyke could be paid tens of millions of dollars each from whatever remains when the claims are settled.

The pair have been subpoenaed to testify before a congressional committee as part of its investigation into Epstein's network. Kahn is set to appear on Wednesday, while Indyke is due to testify on Thursday, March 19.
