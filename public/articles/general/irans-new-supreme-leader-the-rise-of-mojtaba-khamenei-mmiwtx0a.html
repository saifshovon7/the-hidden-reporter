Iran's New Supreme Leader: The Rise of Mojtaba Khamenei

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: BBC News

Mojtaba Khamenei, the son of Iran's Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, has been chosen as his successor following his father's death in US-Israeli strikes. The 56-year-old has largely kept a low profile, but has been described as a 'capable and forceful' figure within the regime by US diplomatic cables.

Despite his lack of experience in government and public speaking, Mojtaba has been rumored to hold considerable influence behind the scenes in Iran. Born on September 8, 1969, in the north-eastern city of Mashhad, Mojtaba received his secondary education at the religious Alavi School in Tehran and served in the military during the Iran-Iraq War.

He continued his religious studies in Qom, a holy city considered an important centre of Shia theology, but did not wear clerical clothing until his late 20s. Mojtaba remains a mid-ranking cleric, which could pose an obstacle to his acceptance as the new supreme leader.

The appointment of Mojtaba Khamenei could prove controversial due to the Islamic Republic's ideology, which is based on the principle that the supreme leader should be chosen for his religious standing and proven leadership, not through hereditary succession.

One member of Iran's Assembly of Experts, the clerical body that selects the supreme leader, said two years ago that Ali Khamenei opposed the idea of his son being a candidate for future leadership. However, Ali Khamenei never publicly addressed such speculation.
