Cocoa Farmers in Ghana and Ivory Coast Suffer as Chocolate Market Slumps

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

The price of chocolate bars has surged across the world over the past year, but West Africa's cocoa farmers have not seen the benefit. In fact, many are in a desperate state as they have not been paid for months, with some saying they are struggling to survive.

52-year-old Ghanaian cocoa farmer Akosua Frimpong told the BBC that her husband fell sick and she couldn't get money to take him to the hospital, resulting in his death. The recent collapse in cocoa prices has made their beans around 40% more expensive than international traders are willing to pay.

The prices have fallen due to a combination of factors, including a good harvest around the world and lower demand. As a result, chocolate bars have become smaller and chocolate makers have been using less cocoa, leaving the cocoa industry in Ghana and Ivory Coast in a mess.

Even when prices were high, West Africa's cocoa farmers felt they were not benefiting from the world's chocoholics. Their farms are in remote areas deep in the jungle with poor infrastructure, making it difficult for them to access basic necessities like electricity and running water.

The payment delay is thought to be affecting some 800,000 cocoa farmers, and it has had a knock-on effect on hundreds of thousands of rural livelihoods. The Ghana Cocoa Board (Cocobod) has stepped in to buy much of the cocoa to save the industry from collapse, but many farmers say they haven't been paid.

Cocobod officials have taken a pay cut in response to the financial difficulties, with executive management taking a 20% pay cut and senior staff taking a 10% pay cut. However, the board's debt now amounts to some $3bn, adding to the financial difficulties faced by cocoa farmers.

The recent collapse in cocoa prices has left many farmers, including Frimpong, struggling to make ends meet. 'The money I was anticipating from my cocoa bean sales is currently inaccessible. I'm a widow now and I don't have anyone to support me,' she said.
