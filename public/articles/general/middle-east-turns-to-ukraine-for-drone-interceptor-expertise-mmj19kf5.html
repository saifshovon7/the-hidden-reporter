Middle East Turns to Ukraine for Drone Interceptor Expertise

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

President Volodymyr Zelensky has announced that Ukrainian drone experts will arrive in the Middle East this week to assist Gulf states under attack from Iran.

In recent days, Iran has fired a number of Shahed drones across the Middle East, with some air defenses in the region struggling to cope.

Ukraine has developed cheap but effective ways of defending its skies from similar Russian strikes over the past four years, and now some countries have come knocking on their door to ask for help.

The BBC's Diplomatic Correspondent, James Landale, has visited Ukraine to see the latest anti-drone technology the country has to offer.

Ukraine's expertise is in high demand as the Middle East grapples with the threat of Iranian drones.

The US Secretary of State, Marco Rubio, has confirmed that a drone struck a parking lot adjacent to the US consulate building in Dubai.

President Donald Trump has criticized the Spanish government for blocking the US from using its bases during the attack on Iran.

Meanwhile, the conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran continues to escalate, with strikes being traded between opposing sides.

Ukraine's drone interceptor technology is seen as a game-changer in the region, and countries are eager to learn from their expertise.
