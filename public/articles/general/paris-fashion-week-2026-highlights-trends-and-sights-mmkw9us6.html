Paris Fashion Week 2026 Highlights: Trends and Sights

Paris Fashion Week 2026 showcased autumn/winter collections from major labels, with trends including coral and jade party dresses, oversized checked overcoats, and deconstructed boudoir looks.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

As Paris Fashion Week drew to a close on Tuesday, the major labels debuted their autumn/winter collections, giving a tantalising peak into what might filter onto the high street later this year.

All the big fashion houses showcased their wares, including Chanel, McQueen, Chloe, Givenchy, Christian Dior, Balenciaga, Saint Laurent, Balmain, Jean Paul Gaultier, and Stella McCartney.

The usual A-list set were in town, including Margot Robbie and Zendaya, who was spotted at Louis Vuitton's show sporting some sparkly jewellery alongside a simple gold ring, which looked like it could be a wedding band.

Designer Victoria Beckham thanked her family for their support after her show at Paris Fashion Week over the weekend, following a very public fallout with eldest son Brooklyn.

Beckham's collection was described as 'resurgent' by The Guardian's fashion editor, Jess Cartner-Morley, who noted its coral and jade party dresses inspired by the palette of Art Deco portrait painter Tamara de Lempicka.

Grammy-winning singers Chappell Roan and Lola Young attended Vivienne Westwood's show and were clearly inspired by the late designer's deconstructed boudoir look.

UK heritage was evident in the oversized checked overcoats in muted autumnal shades, alongside punk tailoring and some very interesting headwear.

Christian Dior's collection, showcased in the Tuileries Garden, featured mod-inspired designs and received a boost from the sunny weather.

Other notable attendees included Chase Infiniti, Blackpink's Lisa, and actress Anna de Armas.
