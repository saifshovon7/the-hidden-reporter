Eyewitness Recounts Vape Shop Fire That Led to Inferno

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

An eyewitness has shared his account of trying to tackle a fire at a vape shop near Glasgow Central station, which ultimately led to an inferno that engulfed the building.

Lamin Kongira, a resident of Glasgow, told BBC Scotland News that he was walking past the shop when a shopkeeper ran out shouting "fire, fire".

Kongira rushed into the shop and saw a small fire under the counter, and he then dashed next door to the Subway sandwich shop to get help.

He returned with a fire extinguisher and attempted to fight the fire, but two explosions in the vape shop forced him to turn around and warn passersby to "get back".

Kongira believed that the second explosion spread the fire to other parts of the building, causing it to engulf the entire structure.

Video footage shows Kongira trying to keep people away from the shop as smoke billows from its doorway. He is also seen grabbing another man who tries to tackle the fire with a fire extinguisher.

Kongira stated that he prioritized the survival of people during the incident and eventually had to stand back and watch as firefighters battled the blaze.

Later on Sunday evening, the flames from the fire spread throughout the 19th Century building and its famous dome, causing parts of the structure to collapse.

Glasgow Central Station has been closed to all trains since Sunday afternoon and will remain shut during Monday at least.

Many businesses have been destroyed in the fire, and nearby hotels were evacuated, with roads sealed off.

On Monday morning, fire crews used aerial platforms to hose water on to what remained of the building, as smoke hung in the air.

Only the facade of the building at the corner of Gordon Street and Union Street is left standing.

People standing behind the police cordon expressed their shock at the destruction, with some describing it as "iconic" and "beautiful" parts of the city.
