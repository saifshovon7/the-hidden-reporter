New Faces Revealed in Bid to Solve Reservoir Mystery

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 9, 2026 Source: BBC News

Detectives in Wales have released new images of a man's face in a bid to solve the mystery of his death in a remote reservoir.

The man, whose body was found in a £200 XXL wetsuit, had been decomposing for 18 months before being discovered by a lone walker in October 2024.

Specialists at a Liverpool university have used photographs, dental records, and CT scans of the man's skull to create a digital recreation of his face.

The images reveal that the man had striking facial features, including an overbite.

Det Insp Anthea Ponting of Dyfed-Powys Police said the new images were their best chance to identify the man and return him to any family members who may be missing him.

The mystery of the man in the wetsuit has puzzled police since his body was found in Claerwen Reservoir, the largest and most remote of a series of reservoirs in the picturesque Elan Valley in Powys.

Police search teams and a helicopter were deployed to find any clues, but no belongings or distinctive features were found.

The reservoir's depth and remote location have made it difficult to determine how the man ended up there.

Despite international appeals and checks with national records, no loved ones have come forward to claim the man.

Police are now hoping that the new images will finally solve the mystery of the man in the wetsuit.
