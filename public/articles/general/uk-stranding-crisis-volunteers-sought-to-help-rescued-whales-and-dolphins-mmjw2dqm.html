UK Stranding Crisis: Volunteers Sought to Help Rescued Whales and Dolphins

The British Divers Marine Life Rescue organisation is seeking more volunteers to help rescue stranded whales and dolphins in the UK, where the number of live strandings has more than doubled in recent years.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

More whales are stranding on UK shores, prompting a call for additional volunteers to help rescue and protect the growing number of cetaceans washing up alive. The British Divers Marine Life Rescue (BDLMR) organisation is seeking extra volunteers to assess, provide first aid, and refloat stranded animals.

According to BDLMR data, the number of live strandings in the UK has more than doubled in recent years, from around 22 in 2018 to 46 in 2025. In 2023 and 2024, the number of strandings peaked in the 60s for both years.

BDLMR Director of Welfare and Conservation Dan Jarvis attributes the increase to a combination of factors, including man-made hazards and growing public awareness, as well as an abundance of prey in coastal areas. Jarvis noted that Cornwall is a particularly busy area, with a high volume of call-outs, but other areas of the country lack sufficient volunteers and equipment to respond effectively.

The organisation runs regular training sessions, using specialist equipment to help safely refloat stranded animals. Volunteers undergo training and can participate in simulated exercises, including refloating a life-sized two-tonne replica pilot whale.

Volunteers are required to pay for a £150 Marine Mammal Medic Course run by BDLMR, after which they are added to a register and wait to be called out to the next incident. Participants like Cat Douglas, a youth work manager, and James Merrikin, a police officer, have joined the course to gain skills and experience in supporting marine life rescue efforts.

Jarvis encourages individuals to join the cause, stating that while some coastal areas lack sufficient responders, volunteers are crucial in providing the necessary support and resources to effectively respond to strandings.
