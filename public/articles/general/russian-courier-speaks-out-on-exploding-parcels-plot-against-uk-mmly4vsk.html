Russian Courier Speaks Out on Exploding Parcels Plot Against UK

A Russian courier has spoken out on his involvement in a plot to send exploding parcels to the UK and other countries, alleging he was hired to deliver the parcels without knowing their true contents.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 11, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Aleksandr Suranovas, a Russian courier, has spoken out on his involvement in a plot to send exploding parcels to the UK and other countries. The parcels, which contained sophisticated incendiary devices, were disguised as sex toys, body lotion, and massage cushions.

The parcels were sent from Lithuania to the UK and Poland, and three of them went up in flames over the course of two days in July 2024. The operation was run by Russia, according to an international investigation involving UK counter-terrorism officers.

Suranovas maintains that he had no knowledge of the parcels' true contents and was simply hired to deliver them. He was arrested and charged with carrying out an act of terrorism on behalf of Russian military intelligence, the GRU.

Twenty-two people are now in custody in Lithuania and Poland as a result of the investigation. The plot is believed to have been an attempt to shatter support for Ukraine and has been condemned by officials as "active aggression" against European nations.

Vilmantas Vitkauskas, head of Lithuania's National Crisis Management Centre, warned that the plot is a "clear message" to those who support Ukraine and that it is "very dangerous" and could lead to "situations where a lot of people would be suffering."

Suranovas' account provides a unique insight into the plot and exposes how Russia is recruiting individuals within Europe to carry out increasingly serious attacks.
