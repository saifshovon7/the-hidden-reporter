Ukraine Offers Anti-Drone Tech to Middle East Amid Iran Strikes

Ukraine is set to send drone experts to the Middle East to help Gulf states defend against Iranian drone attacks, amid a growing trend of countries seeking to acquire Ukrainian anti-drone technology.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 9, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Ukrainian drone experts are set to arrive in the Middle East this week to help Gulf states defend against Iranian drone attacks. The move comes as Iran has fired a number of Shahed drones across the region, with some air defenses struggling to cope.

Ukraine has spent the last four years developing cheap but effective ways of defending its skies from similar Russian strikes, and now some countries have come knocking on its door to ask for help. The BBC's Diplomatic Correspondent, James Landale, visited Ukraine to see the latest anti-drone technology the country has to offer.

The move is part of a growing trend of countries seeking to acquire Ukrainian anti-drone technology. The technology has been effective in defending against Russian drone attacks, and its success has caught the attention of other nations.

Iran's drone attacks on the Middle East have been a major concern for regional countries. The strikes have caused significant damage and loss of life, and have highlighted the need for effective air defense systems.

The Ukrainian anti-drone technology is seen as a potential solution to this problem. The technology uses a combination of sensors and interceptors to detect and destroy drones, making it an attractive option for countries looking to boost their air defenses.

The deployment of Ukrainian anti-drone technology in the Middle East is expected to be a significant development in the region's efforts to counter Iranian drone attacks. The move is likely to be closely watched by other countries, which may also seek to acquire the technology.
