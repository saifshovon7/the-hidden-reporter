Clumber Spaniel Bruin Wins Best in Show at Crufts 2026

The Hidden Reporter Staff March 8, 2026 Source: BBC News

A Clumber spaniel named Bruin has been crowned best in show at Crufts 2026, fending off competition from nearly 19,000 dogs.

Handled by owner Lee Cox, Bruin was victorious in the Gundog group earlier in the show and went on to receive the Kendall Memorial Trophy and a £200 cash prize.

Cox described Bruin as 'a bit of a diva' who 'likes his own way' but praised his ability to perform under pressure, saying 'he'll never ever let me down.'

Judge Tamas Jakkel's reserve dog choice was a petit basset griffon vendeen called Meghan from Croatia, who earlier won the Hound group.

Crufts Chief Executive Jannine Edgar congratulated Cox and Bruin for 'closing this year's competition with a truly outstanding win and for taking home the prestigious best in show title.'

The event, organised by the Royal Kennel Club, welcomed 18,698 dogs across its various categories, a record number for the event.

Last year's best in show title was taken home by a whippet called Miuccia from Italy.
