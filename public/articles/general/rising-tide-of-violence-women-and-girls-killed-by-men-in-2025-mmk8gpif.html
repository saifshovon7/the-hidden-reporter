Rising Tide of Violence: Women and Girls Killed by Men in 2025

A rising tide of violence against women and girls in the UK has been revealed, with 100 cases reported in 2025. The government has pledged to halve the problem within a decade, but more needs to be done to address the issue.

The Hidden Reporter Staff · March 10, 2026 · Source: BBC News

Warning: This article contains details of violent deaths that some readers may find distressing.

Helena, a mother who lost her daughter to domestic violence, is calling for action against the 'epidemic' of violence against women. Her daughter, Isobella Knight, was killed by her husband in 2025, along with two other women who were friends from school.

The UK government has pledged to halve violence against women and girls within a decade, but recent data suggests that the problem persists. Our investigation found that in 2025, 100 cases of women or girls killed by men were reported, with 24 convictions for murder and six for manslaughter.

We analyzed news reports and cross-checked our findings with UK police forces, the Crown Prosecution Service (CPS), Scotland's Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service (COPFS), and the Northern Ireland Courts Service. Our research revealed that males had been charged in 90 cases, with 10 incidents resulting in the death of a male suspect.

The Office for National Statistics (ONS) records show that in England and Wales, the number of female homicide victims where the principal suspect was male has been steadily increasing over the past five years. In 2021/22, the highest number was 128, while in the year up to March 2025, it was 104.

Helena's story highlights the human cost of violence against women and girls, which is often hidden from public view. Our investigation aims to shed light on this issue and bring attention to the need for urgent action.
